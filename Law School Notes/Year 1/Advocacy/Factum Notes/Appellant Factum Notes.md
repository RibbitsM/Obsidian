### Promissory Estoppel
- Argue that the trial judge misrepresented promissory estoppel as preventing parties from insisting on strict legal rights when it would be unjust
- Instead, this is only possible in regard to the dealings which took place between the parties
- Requires three things in the interactions between the parties
	- One party makes a promise or assurance affecting the parties' legal relations, either expressly or through their conduct
	- Known that the promise or assurance will be acted on
	- Promise or assurance was acted on
- Also requires a contractual right between the parties as a prerequisite, which in this case comes from the employer-employee relationship between Ian and the College
