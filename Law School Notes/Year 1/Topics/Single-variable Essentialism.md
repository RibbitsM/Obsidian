- The right to exclude others is the core attribute of property
- This is both a necessary and sufficient condition of property
- Mainly upheld by William Blackstone, but supported by other prominent thinkers like Jeremy Bentham and Felix Cohen
- Cohen described property as a label which says: 
"To the world: Keep off X unless you have my permission, which I may grant or withhold. Signed: Private citizen Endorsed: The state."
- This view has also been upheld by decisions of the US Supreme Court
- Interference with this right is often seen as more serious than other violations of property rights
