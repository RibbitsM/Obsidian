- F stands for facts
	- Summarize the relevant facts of the case
- I is issues
	- Summarize the main legal issues at hand, put this in question format
- B is brief conclusion
	- State the answers to all of the question(s) raised in the previous statement
	- This section should be short and pretty light on details
- L is law
	- This is where you summarize the relevant law which can include primary and secondary sources you used in your research
- A is analysis
	- Apply these laws to the facts at hand
	- Remember that you should be doing an objective analysis, try to give the greatest advantage possible to both parties
- C is conclusion
	- Simple summary of your analysis, and evaluate the decision based on the analysis


- For case briefs, this approach is slightly different because you are only writing about a single case
- Instead of FIBLAC we use FIRAC, with "rule/ratio" replacing the "brief conclusion" and "law" sections of your brief
