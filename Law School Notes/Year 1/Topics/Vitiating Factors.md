- There are a number of circumstances that can arise that would make a contract unenforceable even if all four factors for a contract are present
- The two we will examine this term are misrepresentation and illegality
### Misrepresentation
- This occurs when one party convinces the other to make a contract on the basis of a false promise
- This may be a lie about the good being exchanged, a lie about the value of a good, the origin of a good, etc.
- Just means any kind of intentional misrepresentation of what is being exchanged
### Illegality
- This can refer to any agreement where carrying it out would break another law
- For example, hiring someone else to commit a crime
### Duress
- Refers to any contract signed using threats of violence or violence itself to force another party to sign the contract
### Undue Influence
- Happens when both parties are in a situation of trust, for example like in a relationship or related, or even in a mentor/student relationship
- Similar to duress, where there is an outside factor affecting the parties willingness to sign the contract
### Unconscionability
- This occurs when one party uses their status as a position of power to pressure the other party into signing
- Similar to duress, but often the threat or influence in this situation is implicit, while in duress cases it is almost always explicit
