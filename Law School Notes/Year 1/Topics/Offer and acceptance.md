- To form a contract, all involved parties must have a "meeting of the minds" first
- This is a meeting where the essential terms of the contract are decided, and all parties agree to engage with the contract
### Offer
- An offer is an action by which one party will make an offer to the other party involving the prospective terms of the impending contract
- This offer must be as clear as possible, for example a car salesman cannot offer a car for $5000 without specifying which car exactly they are offering
- If the offer is deemed insufficiently clear, there was never a "meeting of the minds" and the contract is considered void
### Acceptance
- On the other hand, the person being offered to must clearly accept the terms of the contract being offered
- This can be as simple as a "yes" or an affirmatory hand gesture
- The acceptance must be in accordance with the initial offer, if the second party attempts to change the terms of the contract this is considered a counter-offer
