- Like tort and property law, contract law is a part of private law
- It deals with interactions between citizens, and between citizens and companies or even the government
- Does have a public element, especially with the government contracting out work to private entities
- The goal of contract law is basically just to shore up the institution of contracts
- Not all areas of human life necessitate legal backing, but contracts are an example of an ordinary part of life where the law has a place
- Contracts are legally binding agreements, does not describe all possible agreements you can make
- There are plenty of promises and agreements we make every day that may be morally binding, but not legally
- Usually commercial in nature, but not always
- Promises made as part of a contract make up the terms of a contract, which determine the rights enjoyed by the parties to that contract
### Three Key Questions
- Was a contract formed?
- If a contract was formed, is it enforceable?
- If it is enforceable, is there reason to negate the contract?
- These reasons are called [[Vitiating Factors]], which consist of situations like duress
- Bad behaviour factors, reasons to annul a contract due to the way it was formed or carried out
### Types of Contracts
- We can categorize contracts according to the number of parties, and they way in which the contract is communicated
- Two kinds of contracts party-wise, which are [[Unilateral Contracts]] and [[Bilateral Contracts]]
- Another way to divide contracts is between [[Explicit Contracts]] and [[Implicit Contracts]]
### Essential Elements of a Contract
- You need four things to delineate a contract:
	1. [[Offer and acceptance]]
	2. [[Certainty of terms]]
	3. [[Intention to contract]]
	4. [[Consideration]]
- The 2014 *Bhasin v. Hrynew* case determined that all contracts have a fifth implied element that it will be carried out with good faith and honesty regardless of the intentions of the party
- This is something inherent to all contracts that you cannot escape
- Helps protect against harms created by intentionally misleading contracts
### Remedies
- There's a couple of remedies for contract issues, and the only one we'll cover this semester is rescission
- This is where the court annuls the contract, usually done in the presence of vitiating factors
- Often, people going to court seeking to prove vitiating factors are seeking rescission as a remedy
- Other three are Damages, Specific performance, and Injunction
- Usually applied in the case where a term of a contract has been breached
