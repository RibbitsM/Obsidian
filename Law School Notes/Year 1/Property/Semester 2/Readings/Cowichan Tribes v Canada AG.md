- Plaintiffs are seeking declaration of Aboriginal title to one of their village sites and surrounding lands on the Fraser River
- Also want declaration of the right to fish the river for food
- Claim area is located on Lulu Island in Richmond, land is owned by the Crown, VFPA, City of Richmond, and some private owners
- After a long trial involving a lot of oral, historical, and ethnographic evidence, found title was established on a portion and the right to fish was also established
### Findings of Fact
- Plaintiffs are the modern successors of the Cowichan and are the proper rights holders
- Historically occupied several winter villages across east Van Island and on several of the Gulf Islands
- Relied on fishing, hunting and gathering, travelling around the area to harvest food in the spring
- Traveled en masse to their permanent village in the claim area every summer to live, fish, and harvest
- Prior to and after sovereignty, they sufficiently and exclusive occupied this village and the surrounding lands
- James Douglas made a promise of peace based on his Crown authority in 1853, engaging honour of the Crown
- Appropriated several Indigenous settlements in 1859 and 1860
- Cowichan stayed in their village but it was never designated as a reserve and was sold without their knowledge
- Purchased by Richard Moody in part, most Crown grants on the title land were made without authority
- BC entered Canada in 1871 which extended the appropriation of settlement lands and limited their ability to sell land without consent from the Cowichan
- Because of this, all post-Confed grants on title lands were done without constitutional authority
- Crown grants deprived the claimants of their village lands, infringed their title, and limited their ability to fish
- Further grants to Richmond and some activities of Canada and the VFPA on these lands were also unjustifiable infringements
- Province cannot extinguish Aboriginal title, Crown grants did not displace or extinguish title
- Aboriginal title is beyond the *LTA*, any *LTA* claims do not apply
- BC and Richmond's defences are not made out
- Plaintiffs establish the right to fish for food based on long-standing, distinctive, integral, and continuous practice
- Right not dependent on permission from any other Indigenous group
### Declarations
- Cowichan have Aboriginal title to the title lands under s. 35(1)
- Crown grants and Crown vesting of interests on lands within the title lands are unjustifiable infringements of title
- Except for the Airport Fuel Delivery Project Lands, all fee simple titles and interests held by Canada and Richmond in the area are invalid
- Canada owes the Cowichan a duty to negotiate in good faith on reconciliation of third part interests and the vesting of land to Richmond
- Cowichan have a right to fish the south Fraser river for food under s. 35(1)
### Indefeasible Title Under LTA
- Based on the above information, Aboriginal title was not displaced by Crown grants in fee simple
- Also, Aboriginal title and fee simple can coexist on title lands
- Cowichan still seek a declaration that the free simple interests in the claimed lands except for the airport lands are invalid
- Richmond argues that ss. 23 and 25 of the *LTA* bar a declaration that their fee simple lands are invalid
- Richmond holds indefeasible title to each of the lands Richmond owns in the Claim Area, Aboriginal title found on two lots
- Argue that s. 23(2) means that their title cannot be defeated, revoked, or made void by any means, and s. 25 prevents action for recovery against the registered owner
- Current declaration can't be made because it dispossesses Richmond of land they are indefeasibly entitled to
- Doesn't matter that the Crown grants were made without authority, s. 23(2) cures any defect in the original grants
#### Plaintiff Argument
- Argue that the *LTA* does not apply to Aboriginal title, it's not mentioned in the statute at all
- Point out that Aboriginal title is *sui generis*, previous case called *Hwlitsum v Canada* said that Aboriginal tile is beyond the BC title system
- Also not a registrable interest according to *Delgamuukw*
- Even if the *LTA* did apply, that would be extinguishment which legislature can't do plus no clear and plain intention
- Generally, registered are indefeasible under the *LTA* but it does not apply, even if it does, it would be an unjustified infringement
### Analysis
- BC title system is a Torrens system which is designed to be a definitive statement of title not affected by anything outside the system
- No chain of title, the registered owner is indefeasibly entitled to the property regardless of how the previous owners got it
- Interests in land are only transferrable by registration, and registration protects against actions for recovery
- Indefeasibility is the cardinal principle of the *LTA*, reinforced by amendments to the statute
#### Are The Interests Invalid?
- First thing we need to consider here is whether the *LTA* is a law of general application and if it applied to Aboriginal title
- Given the nature of Aboriginal title and the text of s. 23(2), it seems that legislature did not intend registration to be good against Aboriginal title claimants
- Need to read this provision in context, the *LTA* as a whole only deals with estates and interests coming from fee simple grants
- Because Aboriginal title is not a registrable interest, we can consider it to be upstream of indefeasible title
- If we accept this argument, then we cannot recognize Aboriginal title to any land the Crown has transferred to third parties
- How can you have an act that prevents registration of Aboriginal title but also extinguished Aboriginal title
- This would basically just extinguish the rights of Aboriginal title holders
- Inconsistent with the principle that only Parliament could extinguish Aboriginal title before s. 35, and that it can't be extinguished, only infringed where justified after s. 35
- Overall, Aboriginal title is beyond the *LTA*, can't say registered owners are entitled to the land against Aboriginal tile holders
- Even if it did apply, the Crown grants would be unjustifiable infringements made without constitutional authority
