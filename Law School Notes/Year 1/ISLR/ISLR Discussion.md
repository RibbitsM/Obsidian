- This week we learned about how the criminal justice system in many ways serves as a replacement for previous systems of colonial oppression and continues the legacy of settler colonialism in Canada. Discuss the role that colonialism plays in the overrepresentation of Indigenous inmates in the Canadian prison population, and how this connects to other forms of oppression such as residential schools and the Indigenous child welfare system.
- *Gladue* Reports are a key part of the Canadian government's efforts to limit the incarceration rates of Indigenous offenders, but so far has had little effect. Discuss the potential problems that this approach may have, and what changes might be made to the process to fix these problems. The supplementary reading "Understanding Gladue from the Perspective of Indigenous People" may be helpful to you.

Focus of the discussion questions
- Trying to draw out the narratives that have been passed down about Indigenous peoples
- Each question relates to a core topic for the week
- Organized into a funnel approach, starting with the broadest question and going to the most specific, showing at how overarching themes of oppression manifest themselves in statutes and in practice
- First question is mostly about the narratives and the historical issues
- Second question is about criticizing the existing regime of incarceration and looking to future solutions and what lawyers can do about it
- Third question is more about examining the practical outcomes of these practices and how the theoretical issues from the first two manifest themselves
