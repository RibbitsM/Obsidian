1. Arraignment
	- This is where the charge is formally read to the accused
2. Plea Entered
	- If guilty, accused is sentenced, otherwise proceed
3. Crown Case
	- In this step, Crown witnesses are called and cross-examined and evidence is submitted
	- Evidence can be either documentary or real
4. Crown Case is closed
5. Defence may motion for No Evidence
	- The Crown may reply to this motion
	- If the judge rules in favour, accused is acquitted and trial ends
	- If the judge denies the motion, defence can proceed to the Defence Case or choose not to call evidence and proceed to Closing Arguments
