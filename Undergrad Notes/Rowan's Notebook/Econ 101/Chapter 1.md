What is Economics

- Economics is managing scarce resources for unlimited wants
- Resources go in, goods and services come out
- Factors of productions transform inputs to outputs
- Goods and services are consumed
- Production vs consumption, opposite forces

5 Factors of production

- K Capital (PEIR Plant, Equip, Inventory, Residential Const.)
- L Land
- N Labour
- T Technology
- E Entrepreneurship
- These are all inputs

Goods and Services

- Good: tangible (car, phone)
- Service: Intangible (haircut, plumber)

Scarcity

- Production is limited, demand is unlimited
- Politics decides which of these demands are important
- Economics manages these demands
- Scarcity principle: all things are scarce
- Almost no good is unlimited, therefore economics can be applied to anything
- Time and innovation are unlimited

Choice

- When scarcity is present (always), we have to make a choice
- Doing everything is impossible, you must weigh what you are giving up
- People make decisions as tradeoffs

Opportunity Cost

- Value of what you give up when making a choice
- What do I lose by making this decision?
- **Value/benefit of the next best foregone alternative**

EX: go to ubc vs working

- Lose 4 years salary
- All the money you spent on tuition/books
- Is that worth the experience and degree?
- Opportunity cost is a benefit
- The benefits of the alternative

**Sunk Cost:** cost incurred in the past and can't be salvaged

- Irrelevant because they are irreversible
- Time travel does not exist in economics
- OC only looks forward, not back

PPC Curve

- Shows all possible combinations of producing 2 outputs
- Ideal production conditions
-   
    
 
butter

guns

- Here you can either make 10 guns or 15 sticks of butter at maximum capacity either way
- Curve represents the limits of production
- For optimal production, use a point on the curve
- In this example, for every gun you make, you must give up 2 butters
- This is the OC
- MRT (marginal rate of transformation) is the slope of the curve
- Shift of the curve is caused by technology change
- Better or worse production

Problem: why is the curve concave?

- The most efficient workers in their respective industries are the last to be moved
- If you only need 2 gun workers vs 13 butter makers, moving the worst butter maker doesn't change much
- But it drastically improves gun production.
- When the sides are evenly balanced, the OC reaches equilibrium
- A shift on either side would have the same OC
- MRT is different of different parts of the curve
- Higher the production, higher the OC and vice versa

Four Key Economic Problems  
Microeconomics

- What is produced and by who? (allocation of resources)
- What is consumed and by who? (distribution of income)

Macroeconomics

- Output gaps, why is the maximum capacity not being met
- How do you increase maximum capacity?
- Government policy seeks to solve these 4 questions
- When should government policy influence markets?
- Is efficiency or equity more important?

Complexity of the Modern Economy

- Free Market is self organizing
- Spontaneous order generated by demand and supply
- Adam Smith believed in this concept that he called "the invisible hand"
- Coordination Principle: Free Markets automatically coordinate

Efficiency

- Efficient: sellers produce what buyers want at the least cost
- Free markets are far from perfect and can fail or be unfair

Incentives

- Assumption that rational decision makers respond to incentives
- Rational Self Interest Principle: Assumption that most people behave in self interest and want to minimize cost and maximize benefits
- Buyers and Sellers will negotiate to the best price

**Rules of the Market**

- Rule of law
- Property rights, freedom of contract, limited liability for corporations
- Institutions (court, financial)
- Instincts, habits, culture

Assumptions of Decision Makers

- 3 primary principles
- People make decisions based on incentives
- People want to increase benefit and decrease cost
- Decision makers always want to maximize something
- People generally prefer being happy to being unhappy

**Marginal Principle**

- Someone will take an action if the marginal benefit exceeds the marginal cost
- People are more likely to take marginal actions as long as the cost is lower

Macroeconomics

- Households, Firms, Government, Rest of the World

Household

- Make joint decisions as 1 unit
- Maximize satisfaction
- Exchange inputs for income, exchange income for outputs

Firm

- Make joint decisions as 1 unit
- Maximize profits
- Produce outputs for income
- Spend income on production

Government

- Do not act consistently
- Theoretically maximize household satisfaction

Rest of the World

- Act as consumers of our exports and producers of our imports

Productions and Trade

- Market: area where buyers and sellers negotiate the exchange of a commodity
- Division of Labour: Adam smith's pin production line ^dba783
- Specialization of Labour: dividing a job into smaller more specialized jobs
- These are both techniques that increase efficiency
- Markets trade these specialized outputs
- If each individual is more specialized (therefore higher quality) and all benefit from it via the market, overall efficiency goes up
- Specialization of labour requires markets

Globalization

- Caused by advancements in communication technology and travel
- The world is more interconnected
- Markets are now worldwide instead of nationwide

Alternatives to Markets

- Feudal: jobs based on tradition and custom
- Prices are traditional and fixed
- Works in an unchanging environment
- Only present in very remote and isolated areas in the modern age
- Command: or planned economy, economy is controlled by a central authority
- USSR had a command economy, CCP used to have one
- Only modern examples are kind of Cuba and perhaps North Korea
- Free Market: decisions are decentralized
- Buyers and sellers communicate through price
- Mixed Market: free market with planned aspects
- EX: North Korea, Canada, Hong Kong in order of most planned to least

Great Debate

- Adam Smith championed efficiency and resource allocation
- Karl Marx championed equity in the economy
- This is the greatest issue of modern economics by far
- The general answer is that mixed economies are best
- The debate now is that where on the spectrum you should lie
- Economics are not necessarily related to politics
- Freedoms and Economic Freedoms are not always connected

Failure of Planned Economies

- Misplaced Incentives
- Failure of Coordination
- Failure of Quality Control
- Environmental Degradation

Governments in Mixed Economies

- Markets are designed to be voluntary and free choice
- Governments exist in economies to ensure these principles are upheld (ex law against freedom to contract)
- They also protect rule of law and private property
- Big Five: Monopolies, Externalities, Public Goods, Asymmetrical Information, Merit Goods
- Governments can improve on market outcomes

**Marginal Thinking:** Unlike other sciences, economists look towards the future and try to predict what decisions will be made in the future, not explaining the decisions already made (though we can learn from them)

- Marginal benefit is usually diminishing (the more you consume the less benefit you get)
