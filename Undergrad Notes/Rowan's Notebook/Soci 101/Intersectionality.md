Intersectionality

- A way of understanding how social statuses and identities shape their life, including what inequalities they might experience
- Focusing on one identity gives an incomplete picture of an individuals experience
- These identities include race, ethnicity, nationality, class, gender, sexuality, religion, age, ability
- Race: system of classification based on differences in physiology that cultures consider to be significant
- Can self-identify as a race, but it is also imposed onto you by others
- Perception of race is subjective and differs based on context
- Racial identity is fluid over time
- Inequalities based on race determine life opportunities
- Race and ancestry are different things
- Difference between what you are and who you are
- Every person has ancestry, but race depends on how you interpret your ancestry
- Modern understanding of race in the Western world was created several hundred years ago
- Our common understanding of race emerged from ideologies of enslavement and colonization
- By creating a racial hierarchy and "ranking" groups of people allowed British scientists to justify colonization and slavery

Different Classification Systems

- In brazil, race is classified through physical features instead of ancestry
- It's possible for 2 kids from the same parents to have different races because one may have darker or lighter skin
- Many people considered white today were not considered white in the past
- Legal definitions varied wildly over the ages to limit immigration of certain groups
- Shows how social understandings of race change over time

Individual Fluidity

- Races tied to places (west asian) had very low stability
- The white category was the most stable
- Higher education was associated with higher stability
- Social statuses and experiences were a better predictor of stability than economic factors
- Immigrants identifying as visible minorities had more stability, same with those who lived near many other people who identified in the same way
- Leads to neighbourhoods overwhelmingly populated by just one ethnic group

Race and Racism

- While race differs wildly depending on context, it still affects treatment greatly
- Stereotypes: widely held generalizations about as group
- Prejudice: preconceived ideas about a group
- Discrimination: exclusion of a certain group
- Racism: beliefs, ideologies and practices that disadvantage people who are perceived to be a certain race
- Seen at the individual and the societal level
- Also institutional, racial biases are engrained to legal and economic systems
- Also intersects with gender, class, sexuality, age, ability etc.
