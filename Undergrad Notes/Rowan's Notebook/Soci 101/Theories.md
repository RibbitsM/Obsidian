Structural Functionalism

- Focuses on norms and traditions of society, macro theory
- Talks about how aspects of society bind people together to form a utility
- Society is like a human body, groups and organizations are the organs
- Individuals fill roles to run the system
- Does not critique power relations
- Often overlooks inequalities in society, assumes all people benefit
 
Durkheim

- Established structural functionalism
- Relationship between individuals and systems
- For example suicide, a personal act caused by societal pressures

**Anomie**: when norms or values break down in society

- When people feel solidarity, social order is maintained (opposite of anomie)

Conflict Theory

- Emphasizes struggle within society
- Talks about class relations very frequently
- Hierarchy within society, which classes or groups submit to others
- How groups gain or lose this advantage in society
- Doesn't examine the more stable aspects of society

Marx

- Established social conflict theory

Origin of the concept of constant class struggle

- Believed that society was constructed on the base of distribution
- Groups with different resources would conflict
- Studied the dynamics of capitalism

**Capitalism**: system based on private ownership and competition, focused on profit

- Marx only imagined 2 classes, proletariats and bourgeoisie
- Owners vs workers

Symbolic Interactionism

- Talks about interactions between individuals
- Social life is only given meaning by people
- Social circumstances are created by those involved in them
- Extremely micro level vs the other theories
- Often loses sight of the big picture, can't always explain society wide phenomenon

Weber

- Talked mostly about micro level theories, father of symbolic interactionism
- Understood sociology as a social science
- Looked at individuals and the meaning they observe
- Culture influences people, who influence society, which shapes culture
- Observed the economy by looking at the cultural values that shape it
- Believed protestant values were responsible for capitalism
- Predicted the rise of modern bureaucracies

DuBois

- First black man to earn a PhD from Harvard
- Studied lives of black and white men during the Jim crow era
- First sociologist to describe structural racism but was ignored for decades

**Double Consciousness:** fractured identity by seeing themselves through the lens of white society  
**Colour line:** separation of race physically economically, emotionally  
**Psychological wage:** symbolic rewards granted to white people in society but not black people

Jane Addams

- Encouraged sociologists to actually apply their findings in order to enact social change
- Pushed the field into practical fields instead of merely observing and recording
