- Ethnicity isn't always tied to a geographical region
- Many countries have a variety of ethnic groups residing within them
- An example would be in Myanmar, where many different ethnic groups are in conflict
- Ethnicity can be defined by language or religion
- In the example of Korean children adopted by Americans, they are of ambiguous ethnicity
- All are racially Korean, but not all are ethnically Korean
- Ethnicity is self determined
- One is not part of an ethnic group unless they identify themselves to be part of one
- Unlike race, which is socially determined

- Ethnicity is less about phenotype than race
- Understanding that a group of people have a shared cultural origin
- Can have broader and narrower elements
- Emerged similar time as the concept of race (with eugenics)
- Immigrants from places like Ireland and Italy used to be considered different races from whites
- Race riots because white working class people couldn't pay the draft exemption fee and black people were exempt from the draft
- Intersectional struggle between class, race, ethnicity, status etc.
- Many black people were killed in the riots
- Brings up the argument that complaints from minorities now are irrelevant because of discrimination against Irish in 19th century
- Ethnic groups can be brought into the majority under certain circumstances
- Struggles like this turned a class issue into race/ethnicity issue, both black and Irish people should've been mad at the upper class
- However, racial differences led them to target eachother
- Heritage is a key component of ethnicity
- Because of migration, new kinds of ethnicities emerge
- Concepts like "chinese-american" is a new concept of ethnicity
- Distinguishes what constitutes a shared heritage (polish) and what is just a shared culture (liking vintage clothing)
