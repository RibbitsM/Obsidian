Surveillance in the Pandemic

- Mass surveillance
- Global spread of surveillance to track covid
- Monitor peoples health and location
- Many governments released contact tracing apps
- Increased use of drones and camera's to track sick people
- Must be used proportionately to the need
- UK has facial recognition database for tracking criminals
- Many other police services use this technology too
- Some countries use id cards tied to biometrics
- China has one of the most sophisticated surveillance systems in the world
- Camera for every 8 people
- Used to track social credit system
- Tracks health, people have to prove good health to leave house
- This tech can help detect emotions and opinions
- Emergency measures tend to last beyond emergency
- Fear that heightened surveillance will last beyond covid
- Parallels to countries clinging on to wartime measures
- Example of US Patriot act after 9/11
- Government transparency is key in this kind of situation
- Government should be watched more carefully in emergency situations

Surveillance Capitalism

- Very few privacy laws on how companies use your location
- If an app or service is free, then you are the product
- Most free apps gather and sell your information, hidden in terms and services
- Can identify people from easily purchasable data
- Even weather apps can provide detailed location data
- Clearview is an advanced facial recognition tool, used by several governments
- So advanced that it can identify people in background of photos clearly
- Can identify individual protestors from a crowd shot
- Uses deep learning AI, constantly gets better at identification
- No longer allowed to operate in Canada, but millions of Canadians have their photos in the Clearview database

Surveillance in China

- China has an abundance of surveillance cameras
- Taxis, businesses, all help government track location of citizens
- Personalized messages from government telling you who to not associate with
- Uses cameras to publicly shame jaywalkers
- Gives personal recommendation at restaurants and allows you to pay with face
- Limits toilet paper use at public toilets
- Used for punch in and out at companies
- Cameras can recognize clothing, car make and model, age, sex, all at a glance
- Surveillance used in private residences as well, like government housing
- Tracks movement of residents, and who and how many people visit them
- Low transparency, very few protections
- Universal ID card required
- Can locate criminals in public by cross referencing video feed with criminal databases
- Integrated closely with "social credit score" system
- Used to improve overall moral integrity of the Chinese public
- Problem with this system is that Chinese morals and legal system must be perfect
- No inherent problem with social credit system
- Many countries across the world already use face recognition for policing, transit, airports, etc.
- In the US mass surveillance is mainly contained to the private sector
- Data helps reveal societal patterns
- Little to no regulation in the US
