- Sex is a biological construct
- Based on reproductive organs, chromosomes, and hormones
- Intersex people are people with biological characteristics that are neither male or female
- Can be XXY, XYY, or XX people with a penis or XY people with a vagina
- History suggests that intersex people have existed all throughout history
- Research has determined that testosterone does not affect behavior as much as we thought it did
- Testosterone is also related to various essential functions like embryo development and red blood cell growth
- In differences between male and female brains and thought processes, there is around a 92% overlap when testing sex differences

Gender

- Gender is a social construct
- Also called gender identity
- There is also masculine or feminine expression, which is a separate concept
- Identity and expression are related however

Gender Identity

- Transgender people are people who's gender does not line up with their gender assigned at birth
- Cisgender people are people who's gender matches their gender assigned at birth
- There are many many gender identities specific to individual cultures
- Most of these identities don't match up with our common understandings of gender identity
- Colonization has almost wiped out many of these identities like hijras in Hindu culture
- Imposed values from the English harm local gender identities

Sociological Approaches to Gender

- Sociology explores the effects of culture on sex and gender, not the nature vs nurture debate
- Cultural understandings of gender change over time
- High heels were originally considered very masculine
- The Kingdom of Dahomey had an all female army, the Amazons
- The culture was very similar to Sparta, with a focus on combat and physical training
- The tradition ended when France colonized the region
- In the 1800s, the word manly was used much more frequently than masculine or masculinity
- Manly typically referred to only upper class, white masculinity
- What culture considers manly has changed greatly over time
- Physical affection among men was more common in the 1800s
- Many old photos of friends show men holding hands, resting their hands on each-others legs, sitting on eachother
- This persisted across racial and class differences

Masculinity across Social Contexts

- In Arab culture physical affection among men is more common
- Because society is so strictly segregated, feelings are channeled between the same sex
- As segregation decreases, so does the level of men showing affection for eachother
- In South Korea masculinity is much more defined by popular culture, by kpop bands
- In the midwest US it is much more common for females to be masculine

Gender Socialization

- Gender socialisation is closely tied to clothing and colours
- Mainly learn about gender through family, peers, schools, and religion

- In the 1800s pink was a boys colour and blue was a girls colour
- Young boys also used to wear dresses
- Prior to gendered clothing, children's clothing was unisex and monotone
- All children used to wear dresses for convenience
- Babies looked very similar no matter their gender
- When coloured infant clothes were first introduced there was no universal consensus
- Through firms examining which colours sold best, the modern colour binary won out
- By the 1980s the gendered clothing colours were cemented
- With the rise in ultrasound technology, new parents were encouraged to decorate determined on gender prior to birth
- This prevented parents from handing down unisex clothes and furniture
