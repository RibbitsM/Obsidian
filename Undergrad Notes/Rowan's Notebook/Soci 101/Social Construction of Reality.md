**Thomas theorem:** If people believe situations to be real, it will be real in their minds

**What is Social Structure?**

- Set of social statuses, roles, groups, networks, and institutions that shape your daily life
- Operate beyond individual actions, but those actions affect the structure
- Do not determine actions, but they influence attitudes, behaviors etc.
- Within the structure, individuals have resources, and follow rules

Resources: things people have that let them achieve goals

- EX: money, education, status, knowledge
- Lack of resources restricts opportunity, surplus of resources increases opportunity
- Based on location in the structure, your opportunity is determined
- Rules: formal and informal expectations on how to act
- Can be formal (laws, organizational policies) or informal (social norms, expectations)
- Key points of social structure are networks, status, roles, groups, institutions

**Social Networks**

- Series of relationships that link people directly and indirectly to many others
- Can restrict or broaden opportunities
- Even ties considered weak can have huge returns by connecting you to different network clusters
- Norms, values, and emotions are shaped by networks
- "it's not what you know, it's who you know"

**Statuses and Roles**

- Status: socially determined position in society
- It is also the informal aspects such as prestige
- Can be one of 2 types
- Ascribed: not chosen, ex race, age
- Ascribed statuses determine place in society and resources allocated
- Achieved: voluntary status, chosen as a result of work or effort
- Student, athlete, award winner
- Role: set of expectations built around a certain social status
- Ex: baristas (example of an achieved role) are expected to be friendly and good at making coffee
- Role strain: when one role requires too much (engineering student)
- Role conflict: when multiple roles contradict each other (work or studying)

**Social Groups**

- Two or more people with shared interests who interact on a regular basis
- May form social networks
- Groups shape values, and social connections, can also provide resources
- Groups are a subset of networks, many interconnected groups make up a network
- Groups can enact pressure on behaviour (peer pressure)
- 2 kinds of groups, primary and secondary
- In primary groups, members are not interchangeable
- Made up of enduring meaningful relationships, like family, partners, close friends
- Most groups are secondary
- Secondary groups are impersonal, goal oriented, and temporary
- They also may have structure, roles, and authority figures
- Groups have to be 2 way relationships, networks can be 1 way

Institutions

- Institution: Central powers of life that create rules (formal or informal) and meet basic needs
- Education, family, religion, media, law, government, police
- Governed by norms, that guide the behaviours of all group members
- Institutions affect behaviour absolutely, no matter your opinion on them
- Independent aspects of social life, exist beyond individuals but are subject to the opinions of people over time
- Institutions are often invisible, until they break down
- They are the walls, floor, and ceiling of the social world
- Institutions in Canada specifically are moderately trusted, except for religious institutions which are widely distrusted

**Socialization**

- The process in which you learn the rules of social life
- Involves learning culture and expectations of roles
- This is where you receive your identity and learn to interact in society
- Occurs throughout your life, but most significantly as a child
- Agents of socialization: parts of the social structure that influence the process of socialization
- Primary socialization: first experience of society, in childhood
- Socialization will teach you about your class, race, and gender, all of which will prepare you to join these groups
- Secondary socialization: socialization outside the home, teach about bureaucracy and learn through kids outside your primary groups
- Over time impact of socialization moves from the family to the peer group
- Total Institutions: places where society is self contained (boarding school, military, etc.)
- Resocialization occurs in this situation by breaking down your previous social identity and rebuilding it with reward/punishment to instill new values
- 4 key agents: family, media, peers, school
- There is some agency even in the social structure
- Our free will is strongly shaped by our rules and resources but not absolutely controlled
- Constant struggle between personal agency and social structure
- Agency: acting by your own will within the constraints of your circumstances
