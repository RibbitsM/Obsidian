- Theology: study of god
- Sociology of Religion: study of religion's affect on society
- Religion: belief about what is sacred, held by a group of people
- Religion focuses on the dichotomy of sacred vs profane
- Sacred things are exceptional, have special importance
- Profane things are not bad, simply normal and not-sacred
- Many things can be sacred, something profane in one religion may be sacred in another
- Anything can be sacred (clothing, places, animals, people)
- Collective Effervescence: feeling of delight and excitement from collective worship
- Powerful experience of connectedness that comes from worshipping together

Global Religion

- Christianity is the most popular religion in the world currently
- It's quite spread out over the world
- Most religions (Hindu, Buddhism, Muslim) tend to be concentrated
- High level of government restriction of religion currently
- Most countries have no preferred religion, about 40% support certain religions, and a small percentage are hostile toward religion (mostly ex-communist bloc)
- Americas have least restriction on religion, Middle east and north Africa have most restriction
- Social hostility between religions across the world varies greatly, but most high hostility zones are in north Africa and the middle east
- However, hostility in this region has declined drastically over time
- Religious terrorism is at an all time low
- Religion is most important to people in south America, middle east, south Asia, and north Africa
- Worship attendance is highest in Africa
- Same difference in belief that religion is necessary for being moral, except for surprisingly low levels in Israel and surprisingly high levels in Japan
- While faith has declined in some countries, it has increased greatly in others
- Countries with higher income inequality tend to be more religious
- Countries with higher GDP per capita are less likely to tie religion to morality
- Secularization is the process of more people becoming agnostic or atheist over time
- Canada, Australia, US, Japan, West Europe all have high secular levels
- Muslims, Hindus, and Christians tend to have higher than average household sizes
- Hindus are more likely to live with extended family
- Jewish people are least likely by a large margin
- Muslims are most likely to have two parent households
- Large age gaps in opinion on whether faith is necessary for morality
- Biggest gaps are in south Korea, Greece, Argentina, US
- Young people less likely to believe, old people more likely
- In only 2 countries were young people more likely (Georgia and Ghana)
- Higher educated people are less likely to connect faith and morality
- Biggest discrepancies in Mexico, Argentina, Greece
- Smallest divides are France, Spain, UK
- In almost all countries women are more likely to pray daily (or no difference)
- Only exception is Israel
- About 43% of countries experience this gender difference
- In general women are more likely than men to believe where gender gaps exist
- Except in Mozambique and Israel
- Gender gap consistently occurs in South America
- In general religious people are happier than inactive and unaffiliated people
- Biggest gaps are in Mexico, Guatemala, Colombia
- Just belief won't make you happier, but getting involved in religion will
- When you feel that connection and compassion it reduces loneliness
- Religion is a key aspect of the social structure, is deeply connected to every aspect of society
- Intersects with politics, inequality, well being, gender, class, race, etc.

Supernatural Beliefs and Religion

- Belief in the supernatural is growing in countries with shrinking religion
- Provides an outlet for belief without the many demands of religion
- Non-scientific phenomena can be 1. central to a religion (angels, demons etc) or 2. not central (ghosts, telekinesis etc.)
- Share similar ontological status
- Religious people tend not to believe in 2, atheists are more likely to believe in 2
- A "ghost sighting" by a religious person may be an angel sighting to them
- People who are moderately religious are most likely to believe in the supernatural
- Highly religious and highly atheist people are least likely to believe
- Most recent survey was 2018
- No data outside US or Canada more recent than 2009
- Small sample size, no intersectionality
- Most people believe in haunting and ancient civilizations
- Bigfoot and psychics are believed the least
- Many people believe in one or two beliefs, but not all
- Supernatural beliefs become more common over time
- Patterns of belief by education vary depending on belief
- Overall women are more likely to believe in the supernatural across the board (except for bigfoot and aliens)
- Less educated people were more likely to fear zombies and believe in bigfoot and telekinesis
- However they were less likely to believe in psychics
- Latinx people were more likely to believe in aliens
- Men are less likely to admit fear in general, can explain results
- Historically women are more associated with the supernatural
- Children's media can contribute to differences in belief by gender
