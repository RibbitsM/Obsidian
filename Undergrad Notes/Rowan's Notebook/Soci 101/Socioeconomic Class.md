- Social class is a group of people who share the same position in society based on income, wealth, education, and occupation
- Absolute poverty: poverty so extreme that people cannot afford basic necessities of survival
- Ex: famine in Yemen
- Relative poverty: being poor in comparison to the majority if society
- Income: money earned from a job or investments, what is reported on income tax
- Wealth: total value of all assets owned by a person
- Wealth is not taxed in most countries, unlike income
- Mobility: moving from one class to another
- Intergenerational mobility: differences in class among generational lines in a family
- Ascribed status is born into, achieved status is gained through effort
- Class is determined by a combination of both
- Early on in life, ascribed statuses are most significant

Intergenerational Wealth

- Many first time house buyers are given financial assistance from their family
- Gifts amounted to 10% of down payments
- Average of 82,000$ gift size
- Gift often comes from parents savings
- Purchasing housing is often only possible with intergenerational wealth
- In Vancouver especially, buying a house without assistance from your family is almost impossible
- Demonstrates how housing is influenced partly by achieved status but also by ascribed status
- In Canada, intergenerational mobility is decreasing over time
- It is harder to make more money than your parents, meaning class is more ascribed
- Despite this, Canada still has a fairly high level of mobility compared to the US
- Low income mobility (how many low income people who move up) has increased since the 90s

Stratification

- Social stratification: A system where people are categorized and ranked in a social hierarchy according to class and life opportunities
- Persists across generations
- Key feature of inequality, different levels have different access to resources
- Closed system: Hierarchy where class is set and cannot move (apartheid, feudal system, caste system)
- Open system: Hierarchy where class is determined entirely by achieved status (do not exist)
- Socioeconomic class system: Most common system, combines ascribed and achieved status to determine class and access to resources
- Equality of opportunity: Everyone has equal access to resources and chance at success but has no effect on outcome
- Equality of condition: Everyone has roughly equal wealth, status and power
- Generally people prefer equality of opportunity
- Meritocracy: social mobility is determined by personal merit and talent
- Structural factors have a great affect on class
- Childhood, family, location of birth shape life extensively
- Hard work gives different results based on where they start in life
- No system is truly meritocratic
- Poverty Line is relative to the Market Basket Measure
- Basically does this family have access to a bundle of goods that allows them to live modestly
- Depends on the cost of living in each community
- Low Income Measure determines if household is below 50% of the median income, if so it is Low Income
- Poverty line is an absolute definition of poverty, low income measure is a relative measure of poverty
- Canada's poverty line has decreased over the years
- However, the Market Basket Measure hasn't been updated in a while, and it tends to raise the poverty rate when it is updated
