- Outliers are Spain, South Korea, Taiwan
- Majority of adults in Canada are in a married couple
- Divorce in Canada has fallen sharply over the past decade
- 7 in 10 Canadians are married or common law
- Average age is 28-30 to marry
- Being common law is much more common in Quebec
- Primary reason for marriage was not love for most of history

Polygyny

- Most common plural marriage
- One man in a union with multiple women
- Illegal in several countries
- Allowed mostly in Africa, Middle East, and some parts of Indonesia and Asia

Polyandry

- Much less common
- One woman married to several men
- Non-monogamous relationships do exist in the western world
- Open relationships is when there is a primary partner but are involved with other partners
- Swinging is couples having sex with other couples generally
- Polyamory is any sexual/romantic involvement with multiple people
- Very flexible definition
- Much more common among gay and bisexual people
- Highest percentage is among gay men
- STI testing and condom use is higher among participants but sexual satisfaction is lower
- About 20% of people have been in a CNM
- Open and honest communication is essential for these relationships
- Lower jealousy and higher trust than in monogamous relationships

Families

- Most children in Canada live in nuclear families
- One million live with one parent
- United States has highest global rate of single parent households
- More common in western world than anywhere else
- Less common to live with extended family in the west, but very common in Africa and Asia
- More common for immigrants and indigenous people in Canada
- Canada has lower rates of extended family homes than US
- However, this is the fastest growing household type
- Living in a nuclear family has only been possible in recent history
- Children raised by same sex couples vs different sex couples have almost identical outcomes
- Main difference is how household labour is distributed
- Usually much more equitable in same sex couples
- Equity in time spent on household chores has increased, but there's still a significant gap
- Gay men spend most time with children in relationships, straight men spend the least
- On average same sex couples spend more time with their children than different sex couples
- More acknowledgement about non conventional families in the western world
- Platonic co parenting, families connected by a sperm donor, and three parent families are all non conventional forms
- Generally people are less concerned about differing family types

Living Alone

- Population of people living alone has skyrocketed recently
- More common than any period in history
- Single person households are most common household type in Canada
- More common in wealthier countries
- Huge differences by country
- Less shift over time in poor countries, increase in rich countries
- Highest rates are in Switzerland, Jamaica
- Overall people who live alone have worse mental health and satisfaction
- Most people living alone used to have a spouse or children
- More likely to be uneducated, unemployed
- About half of single people are actively looking for a partner
- Young people are most likely to live alone
- Black people are most likely to live alone
- Men and women have equal rates
- Young men are much more likely than young women, but older women are much much more likely than older men to live alone
- Biggest reason for being single is having priorities other than dating

Fertility

- TFR is number of children a woman will have on average
- 2.1 is the replacement rate, Canada's rate is 1.4
- Record low, and record high for age of first mothers
- Rich countries tend to have lowest fertility rates
- Japan and South Korea have extremely low fertility rates
- South Korea has a rate of 0.8, a historical global low
- Highest fertility rates are overwhelmingly in Africa
- Fertility rates have fallen worldwide, just by different amounts
- Pandemic created a dramatic decline in birth rate among industrialised nations
- Led to an increase in developing nations because of access to birth control disrupted
- Highest decrease in birth rates in pandemic was in Spain
- Fertility gap is difference between desire for children and actual children
- Allowing paid family leave increases birth rates
- Stigma towards working mothers also decreases birth rates
- Gender inequality in childcare is also a significant factor
- Fertility gap is largest in Italy, Greece, Spain
- Many people want kids but can't have them
- Education also leads to decrease in fertility
- Not having a child is much more acceptable
- Delaying childbirth also led to childlessness
- Can also be because of a perceived obligation to the environment to prevent carbon emissions by not having kids￼
