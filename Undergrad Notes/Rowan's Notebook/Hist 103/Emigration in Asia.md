Chinese Emigration

- 20 million leave southern China 1840-1940
- Parallel to European emigration
- Related to industrialization
- South China connected to many transportation networks
- 90% of networks are to Southern Asia
- Hong Kong not only central port
- In 1930s, Hong Kong becomes dominant
- Long distance migration, not as long as Atlantic
- Key destinations are Vietnam, Malaysia, Indonesia, Cambodia, Thailand
- Some went to Americas
- Very long distances, Beirut to Barcelona is 3000 km
- Europe to US was 6000
- Tied to industrialization
- Carried by steamships
- People moving to work in resource extraction
- Tin mining and rubber plantations in Malaysia
- Rice and soy farming in Thailand and Vietnam
- Sources for materials in American factories
- Male dominated emigration, only 17% female
- 3.4 million women still large chunk
- Young male migrant sent by family, women stay home and take care of family
- Care for elderly, community etc.

South Asian Mobility

- Some going to same places as Chinese migrants
- Indian migrants moving at the same time as British immigrants
- Outnumber British immigrants significantly
- 29 million Indians moving over 100 year period
- 4 takeaways
- Indentured workers only made up 5% of Indian migration
- Eurocentric view to link Indian migration to indentured labor
- 95% go to bay of Bengal, Burma (Myanmar), Ceylon (Sri Lanka) and Malaya (Malaysia/Singapore)
- Significant journey
- 2 million of migrants weren't workers but police, merchants, sailors
- These migrants mostly went elsewhere (Britain, Canada, US)
- More temporary than the European migration
- 75% of workers returned after a couple years
- Significant migration, but not permanent
- Destination linked to commodity production
- Coffee, tea, rubber, rice, timber, oil
- Tied to Chinese and Europeans
- Male dominated yet again
- 66-33 ratio
- Male dominated jobs involved like merchants, police, military
- Significant number of women still involved

Race-based Exclusion

- Atkinson "Burden of White Supremacy"
- Regimes created to limit Asian mobility and create white settler societies
- Mobility of people, resources, and information was a key part in the globalizing world of the 1900s
- Immobility was also a key feature
- Forced a varied spread by preventing Asians and South Asians from coming to the Americas, sending them elsewhere
- Moya posed idea of "mightier laws"
- Even though many people were prevented from migrating to the Americas they got there anyway
- People evaded the law, paid the head tax etc.
- First act of Chinese exclusion in 1882
- More Chinese immigrants than ever in 1888
- Inspector complains that restrictive legislation not working
- Laws were not the defining force deciding where people go
- Two periods of restriction
- First period consisted mostly of Chinese restriction in white settler colonies
- Second period focused on restricting Japanese and Indian migration to America specifically
