Latin America

- Populism differs from country to country and over time
- Shaped the culture of 20th century Latin American politics
- Key of populists vs other politicians was holding followers allegiances to a person or figurehead rather than an ideology
- Need charismatic leader with nationalist ideals
- Strong connection to the working class
- Not necessarily a socialist movement, but image of benefiting the workers
- Promises better lives for the poor, increased minimum wage
- Voter turnout increased under populist rule
- Appealing to a new audience of voters
- Populism is a strategy not an ideology
- Populists have been socialists, fascist, democratic capitalists, corporatists
- Often regimes cannot be classified to one ideology
- Named after the leaders themselves (Peronismo, Cardenismo, Getulismo)
- Reinforces how populism is fuelled by the leader as a person
- 3 main leaders
- Lazaro Cardenas (Mexico 1934-40)
- Juan Peron (Argentina 1946-55,
- Getulio Vargas (Brazil)
- Experimental economics
- Keynesian economics used
- Role of the state in economic affairs increased
- Sometimes successful
- Peronism had success at first, experiment breaks down later on
- Majority of people saw standard of living improve
- Government jobs created
- Access to capital for working class
- Improved social mobility
- Subsidized transportation
- Idea of lifting up the lowest class in society at expense of government debt
- No attention paid to consequences, raise support, create change and don't worry about planning
- Populism is democratic, not a dictatorship
- Populists win elections to get into power and are often reelected
- Usually elites were the only ones with political voices
- Populists drew on the support of women and the working class in order to gain power
- Broadened enfranchisement
- Populists often centred power on themselves, undemocratic procedure
- Massive boom in voter turnout in Mexico, Argentina and Brazil due to populist influence
- This level of deficit spending is still relatively small compared to later debt taken on in the 70s and 802

Cardenismo

- President of Mexico from 1934-40
- Participated in the Mexican Revolution
- Ran a newspaper supported by Madero
- Fought as a Zapatista and later under Calles
- Used populism to beat out his former allies like Calles
- Campaigned extremely hard, gained a massive following
- Travelled all across Mexico to gain support
- Land reform was a key part of his policy which he actually enacted
- Gained support among farmers
- During presidency distributed millions of acres and reformed education
- Made good on the existing promises of the Mexican Revolution

Peronismo

- Juan Domingo Peron, President of Argentina
- Peron came to power without a revolution
- Rose to power through a military dictatorship
- Distinguished himself from the rest of the military government with populist ideals
- Ended the dictatorship via an election, gained power
- Worked in ministry of labour, used his post to assist workers
- Intervened in labour disputes and supported the formation of unions
- Government enforced the terms of these union agreements
- Wages increased for skilled and unskilled labours with the latter benefiting more
- Put labour leaders in government positions
- Cracked down on uncooperative unions
- Socialists and communist unions were repressed or taken over
- Asserted dominance of the state
- Key feature was social justice and assistance for the less fortunate
- First 4 years saw dramatic economic growth
- Wage increases, more benefits like pension plans and vacations for workers
- Lady Peron played a key part in supporting Peronism
- Created a foundation that funded many of the social programs that Peron offered

Legacy of Populism

- Pros and cons
- Increased participation in politics
- Incorporated women and the working class
- Improved the living condition of many
- Destabilized political order
- Often followed by dictatorships
- Marginalized those who did not benefit

Conclusion

- Transformed Latin American politics
- Democratic focus
- Drew more lower class support
- Created social program
- Played important role in shaping future political systems
