Fascism in Japan

- Several shocks to the economy in 1920s
- Leads to economic depression
- Workers make demands to the government, general unhappiness
- 15% of workforce unemployed, labourers militant
- Political crisis, prime minister assassinated
- Growing presence of the Japanese military
- Worried about losing position in Manchuria
- Corrupt cooperation between zaibatsus and the government
- Political system transforms over course of 1930s
- Decline in power of political parties, businesses, unions, co-ops
- Marginalized from mainstream power
- Creates the rise of a "fascist" state
- Japan never called themselves fascists and did not identify with Italy and Germany
- Leaders were inspired by previous fascists leaders
- Presence of military nationalism
- Military controlled politics and the economy
- Intermediary between state, capital, and the public
- Mobilizing to wage a "holy war" against China
- Race based hierarchical nationalism established later on
- Democratic system is dissolved
- Fascism based on practice, not label
- Concerned about social conflict, moral decline, and the modern woman
- Also blamed Britain and US for lack of success

WWII in Asia

- War already in progress during rise of fascism
- Japan invades Manchuria in 1931
- Puppet state of Manchukuo created in 1932
- Escalates in 1937 to second Sino-Japanese War
- Civil war between Chinese Nationalists and Communists at the time
- Extreme violation of human rights
- Unit 731 participated in many unethical acts in occupied territory
- Human medical experiments of observing hypothermia, performing surgery with no anaesthesia
- Researched chemical and biological weapons
- 500,000 killed by Unit 731
- War against civilians, not soldiers
- Expansion of Japanese military in Manchuria set the stage for WWII in Asia
- Prior to pearl harbour, Japan expands further into China, spreading into French Indochina
- Declares war on the US on Dec 7
- Timing of Japanese expansion is linked to events occurring in Europe
- Japan also took over parts of Hong Kong, and Dutch East Indies
- Pearl Harbour was an attack on the US's main naval base in the Pacific
- Goal was to pre-emptively eliminate US from the war before they even joined
- Assumption based on culture that Americans would simply give up
- Instead, ignited the American war machine and gave the US reason to enter Europe
- Two front war from US perspective
- Backfired horribly for Japan, Italy, and Germany
- US, UK, and Netherlands had been levying sanctions on Japan after expansion
- Limited Japan's access to rubber and oil
- US lost many of its battleships, but not its aircraft carriers
- Affected military strategy, used more planes
- By 1942 Japan controlled almost the entire Pacific
- From Asian perspective war started when Japan invaded China, not when they bombed Pearl Harbour as Americans asserted

War on Civilians

- WWII was a war on civilians, many countries strategized to target civilians
- 23 million people die in the Asia-Pacific region
- 10 million in China die
- Political element to these statistics
- Japanese government denies the extent of this information
- Nanking Massacre or The Rape of Nanking from Dec 1937 to March 1938
- 200-300,000 civilians killed, sexual violence rampant
- Violence against civilians was a key part of Japanese military strategy
- First major atrocity committed during WWII
- Took forms of medical experiments, forced labour, and death marches
- Prisoners of war were treated horribly, 27% of Allied POWs died
- Similar to German treatment of Soviet POWs
- Scorched earth policy adapted in 1942
- Three Alls (kill all, burn all, loot all)
- During Defence of Singapore massacred surrendering platoons

You Know What Happens Next

- Hiroshima and Nagasaki get nuked on August 6 and August 9
- Goal is purely to kill civilians
- 200,000 killed in the blast alone, radiation killed many more
- Decision was subject to heated debate
- Argue that US didn't need to use the A-bomb
- US had the atomic bomb but Germany had already surrendered and they wanted to use it against the Nazis
- Also desired to punish the Japanese for their actions during WWII

Conclusion

- Japanese politics, economics, society all change to resemble fascism
- Ideas on holy expansion, rapid expansion into East Asia, Pacific, colonies
- Brutal war on civilians, multiple war crimes committed as part of expansion
