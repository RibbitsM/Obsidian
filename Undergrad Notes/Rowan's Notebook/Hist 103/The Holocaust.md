- Word holocaust means complete devastation
- Also means catastrophe in Hebrew
- Systematic killing of Jews by the Nazis
- The holocaust needed World War II in order to take place
- Took away from military strategy
- Parallel goal of Germany's empire building
- Effectively prevented Germany from winning the war by focusing all it's resources on fighting
- Extermination camps is a more appropriate term for concentration camps
- There were 25 camps in total, 6 of them are extermination camps
- 3 largest camps were Auschwitz, Treblinka, and Belzec
- The reason Auschwitz is talked about most is because it had the most survivors
- Only 7 survivors of Chelmno
- Only a small part of how Germany went about enacting the holocaust
- Most camps were local and concentrated on exterminating eastern european jews
- Auschwitz was the only exception
- Focusing on Auschwitz is a West European-centric way of viewing the holocaust

Genocide Beyond the Camps

- Killing squads and specialized police groups carried out mass killings
- Included non-German units
- Would go into a town, take the entire Jewish population to the woods and kill them in mass graves they dug themselves
- 33,000 Jews were killed in two days by a killing squad
- Similar in scope to extermination camps
- Death camps killed 3 million, 2 million killed by death squads
- Both Jewish and non Jewish populations targeted
- 3.3 million Soviet POWs killed out of 5.7 million captured
- Meanwhile only a few british and american POWs died
- Primarily through starvation
- Final 3 million deaths were due to exhaustion and starvation in work camps, and by military during uprisings

The Final Solution

- In January 1942 a conference is held in Berlin
- 6 months after war is declared
- Discussing how to deal with Jewish population
- Where the term "Final Solution" came from
- Considered a symbolic turning point of genocidal warfare during WWII
- Was important for finding those responsible post WWII
- Organizing genocidal activities
- Death squads already active since 1941
- Gas chambers in Auschwitz opened in 1941
- Most main death camps opened after the conference however
- The Wannsee Protocol was written during this conference

Conclusion

- WWII was a war on civilians
- Racial nationalism and ambition drove mass killings
- Death camps, killing squads and POWs accounted for most killings
- Killing Jews was the priority of German genocidal policy
