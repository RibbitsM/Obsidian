Late Qing Dynasty

- Lasted 1644-1912
- Last dynasty in Chinese history
- Important figure is dowager princess Cixi
- Most powerful political force in the government
- Her nephews are in charge but she had influence over them
- Dies in 1908, revolution occurs
- Cixi was holding Qing dynasty together
- First Opium War 1839-1842
- Increasing imperial pressure on china, undermining Chinese government
- Reduced economic ability to enact reform
- British ports drained revenue away from the government
- Local traders and Europe benefit
- Treaty ports are where European countries hold power
- Undermines trade relations and Chinese authority
- European matters settled by other Europeans
- British merchants sell opium from British India to china
- Chinese government attempts to curb opium trade
- Tries to ban opium trade, British go to war with china
- Britain wins, win Hong Kong and maintain trade routes in china
- Over next 30- 40 years Hong Kong and shanghai become British controlled
- Kneecaps China and benefits Europe greatly
- Direct access to international trade to HK locals
- HSBC formed at this time in 1865
- Becomes biggest bank in China
- Makes most loans to Chinese state, China is now financially dependent on a British bank
- Qing power heavily undermined by British trade and expansion
- Coastal Chinese citizens are more loyal to Britain, because European trade benefits them
- Local economy undermined by superior European production
- Peasants are taxed more to compensate for struggling economy, causes unrest
- Britain slowly cutting off resources from China
- China undergoes it's own restoration to re-establish it's authority
- Happens by embracing industrial changes but with established Chinese cultural ideals
- Mirror to the Meiji restoration

Challenges to Qing Rule

- Self Strengthening Movement (Chinese Values; Western means)
- From 1860s-1890s
- Essentially adapting western technology and weapons to increase military power
- In 1890s China loses war to Japan, who embraced a similar policy
- Self strengthening movement did not have full government support, partially private
- Difference between industrial movement vs Japan
- Forcing private companies to work free for the government kneecapped the economy
- Created foreign office, Imperial customs agency, language schools and translated western texts
- Overall embracing western structure, changing from imperial court to more modern bureaucracy
- Boxer Rebellion 1898-1901
- Initially a challenge to imperial authority, but court redirects them towards western incursions
- While the Boxers never really fought the Qing, their existence was seen as a failing of the Chinese government
- Europeans see the boxers as a major threat to their incursions, go to war
- Rebellion crushed, Qing and foreign powers both satisfied
- Japan sends many troops during the rebellion and leaves many behind as "peacekeepers"
- Another assertion of Japan's dominance over China
- Peace treaty called Boxer Protocol 1901
- Qing have to pay 18000 tonnes of silver to foreign powers as payment
- No new weapons imports for 2 years, diplomatic concessions, government must crack down on other anti foreign institutions

Rivalry with Japan

- China was always major player in East Asia, and Japan's rapid growth while China is undermined by European imperialism closes the gap between them
- With equal power, tensions and rivalry rise
- Sino-Japanese War 1894-95
- Success of Meiji restoration, failure of Self-Strengthening Movement
- China loses huge reparations, territory, and trade routes
- China is forced to open ports to trade with Japan
- This weakening of China opens European colonies to move in, claiming territory and ports
- Russia also occupies parts of China
- Twenty One Demands
- In WW1 Japan is a major player, collaborating with Germany colonies in Micronesia
- Japan interacts more with the Western countries
- Looking to take advantage of Chinese weakness, sends list of 21 demands
- Key demand was access to Manchuria for railroads and to harvest key resources
- Backfires, seen as too much by western countries
- Europe doesn't respect Japanese Imperialism, its seen as a second rate country
- Rivalry is seen as a weakness of Japan

Revolution of 1911

- Also called the Xinhai Revolution
- Frustration that China is giving in to foreign powers
- Last straw is Railway Rights Protection Movement
- Chinese government was giving railway rights to foreign powers hurting local economy
- This movement put railway control in hands of locals, strengthened local economy
- Sichuan and Hubei province
- Leaders on revolution had studied abroad earlier
- Hosei University transmitted western ideas into the rebellion
- Ideas of republic, sovereignty and equality from the enlightenment
- Had huge impact in 20th century Chinese political thought
- Had lingering impact on the later communist revolution
- Key figure was
- o men tang group came from Hosei

Conclusion

- Qing dynasty weakened by western powers
- China embarks on a series of semi successful reforms to modernize
- Growing Japanese influence in Asia undermines historical Chinese authority and power
- 1911 revolution brought about by reformists
