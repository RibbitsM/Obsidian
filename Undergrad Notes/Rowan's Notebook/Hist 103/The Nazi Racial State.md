Nazi Rise to Power

- Key part of the Nazi rise to power was the instability of the economy
- Stock market crash made radical solutions more appealing
- Both far right and far left gained support
- Nazis used political propaganda very effectively
- Focused on the decline of society
- National restoration
- Used Jews as scapegoats, both local and international
- Later fused this with an anti-communist sentiment too
- Appealed to workers/farmers by involving socialist policies
- Anti communist as well to appeal to businesses
- Appealed to racial nationalists with anti-Semitism
- Messaging continued after 1933
- Huge economic transformations occurred in their first few years in power
- Grew economy greatly, unemployment disappears
- Ignored many elements of the treaty of Versailles
- Grew military to previous levels
- Territorial acquisitions in Austria and Czechoslovakia
- Catered to workers through populist programs
- Corporatist party, saw different parts of society as isolated parts that need to be united and controlled by an authoritarian "brain" state
- Gave vacations to workers, similar to Argentinian populism
- Groups receiving tangible immediate benefits, improves support
- Some remilitarization done in secret

Race and Nationalism

- Anti-Semitism was key to Nazi thought
- Decided that Jews were unable to be assimilated into German society
- Created idea of the self and the other
- Jews were not considered full members of German society
- Created theory that communism, their military defeat and the treaty of Versailles were all caused by Jews
- Another important part of forming the racial state was _Volksgemeinschaft_
- Used to talk about Germany as a national ethnic community
- Different way of defining Germany other than political borders
- Goes hand in hand with anti-Semitism
- Violence against Jews begins in 1938
- Emigration schemes not working
- "Night of Broken Glass" on November 9th, German storm troopers break windows of Jewish stores, govt liquidates Jewish firms
- 90 Jews killed
- Turning point in German hostility towards Jews
- Comparison between Nazism and Fascism
- Anti-Semitism was the main difference
- Italian Fascists had a significant number of Jews in the Fascist party, Mussolini was sympathetic to the Zionist cause
- After 1938 Italy began to adopt some anti-Semitic laws
- More of a mimicking of Germany than anything else
- Nazis came to power through democratic means
- Political strategy of appealing to racial nationalism and appealing to various groups
- Violently opposed communists and Jews

The Racial State

- Nuremberg Laws codified this racial nationalism
- Stripped Jewish Germans of citizenship
- Law for Protection of German Blood and Honour
- Prohibited marriage between Jews and non-Jews
- Reich Citizenship Law
- Stated that only those of German or related blood could be citizens
- Used blood quantums and heritage to determine citizenship
- Operationalization and codification of nationalist ideals
- Eugenics was another segment of the racial state
- Eugenics is not synonymous with anti-Semitism and racial nationalism
- Belief that society could be improved by encouraging some to reproduce and others not to produce
- Very loosely based on understandings of genetics
- Qualities viewed undesirable were usually genetic defects, but were also used to control racial populations
- Could be positive (promoting procreation of preferred groups) or negative (preventing procreation of non-preferred groups)
- Harshest version is forced sterilization
- Existed for over 50 years before Nazis
- Toxic mix of eugenic science, radical ideas and a totalitarian state
- Propaganda posters encouraging women to have more children
- Spread fears of Jewish population spreading faster than Germans
- Sterilized 400,000 people against their will
- Sterilized people for reasons such as stealing, homosexuality, and alcoholism
- Had an ideal image of the "perfect German family"

Conclusion

- Nazis rose to power by appealing to specific groups and taking advantage of crisis
- Anti-Semitism was a key part of Nazi policy and thought
- Through Nuremberg Laws they codified these thoughts, targeting Jews
- Science of Eugenics used as justification for discrimination￼
