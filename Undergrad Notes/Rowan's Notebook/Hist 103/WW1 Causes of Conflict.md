- Series of related events that caused war

Imperial Rivalry

- Major actors: Germany, Austro-Hungary, Russia, France, Britain, Serbia
- Most imperial powers already disliked each other because of colonial competition
- Britain v France in Africa, Germany challenging in Africa
- Britain v Russia in central Asia
- Economic competition between Britain and Germany
- Philosophy that colonies decided your global influence, made your country "great"
- Imperial nationalism was a large part of this animosity

Alliances and International Relations

- Key feature of power in Europe at the time was secret treaties and assurances
- Exact contents of treaties not public knowledge, and not known by other countries
- Shaped the diplomatic behaviour of other countries, based on guesswork
- 3 important treaties made:
- German-Austrian Treaty, Franco-Russian Alliance, Anglo-French Entente
- Signed between 1879 and 1904
- Europe essentially divided into 2 camps based on treaties
- Causes examined after the war, looked at these peace treaties and agreements
- Considered a "faulty" system in 1920s by Woodrow Wilson
- Hidden treaties were a critical mistake leading to war
- New system kept treaties, but assured that these new treaties must be open and publicly known

July Crisis

- Diplomatic crisis, opened the cracks in the international relations system
- Franz Ferdinand assassinated in Sarajevo by Serbians on June 28, 1914
- Ethnic militancy against colonial rule, ethnic Serbs wished to make Sarajevo part of Serbia
- Assassination used as excuse to wipe out Bosnian Serbs, and expand Austro-Hungary by annexing Serbia
- Austro-Hungary was one of the few multi-ethnic empires at the time
- Wide variety of ethnic minorities lived in the various provinces of Austro-Hungary
- Mix of Germans, Hungarians, Czechs, Slovaks, Poles, Ukrainians, Slovenes, Croats, Serbs, Romanians, Italians
- Different groups divided into respective areas
- Austro Hungarian leaders can't afford any single group rising up and challenging authority
- Strategy of rapidly putting down any rebellion that might arise
- Germany supports Austria's annexation
- Strategy to deter Russia from intervening
- Doesn't work, Russia mobilizes troops
- Austria declares war July 28th
- Germany wants to support Austria, but fears Russia's military
- Germany decides to invade France, enacts Schlieffen Plan
- Cause could potentially be preplanned strategy, France wouldn't have been invaded otherwise
- Attacks France on Aug 3
- Idea is to defeat France quickly, cuts through Belgium
- This violates neutrality, and forces Britain into the war
- Germany gets ahead of their supply lines and dig trenches to wait for supplies
- Battle lines barely change for rest of the war

The Fischer Thesis

- Fritz Fischer writes Germany's Aims in the First World War
- Consensus was that WW1 was simply a mistake
- Fischer asserts that Germany's desire to be a global power led to the start of WW1
- Imperial ambition caused the war by encouraging Germany
- Theme carries through to second world war
- Caused huge controversy in 1960s Germany
- Opponents argued that Germany was just propping up ally, Chancellor tried to pressure Austria to call for peace
- Also argued that Germany genuinely thought they could fight Russia and France without involving Britain
