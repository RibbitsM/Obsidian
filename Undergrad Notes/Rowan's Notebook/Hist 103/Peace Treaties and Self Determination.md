Paris 1919

- Paris conference plan to end the war
- Over 6 months of 1919 Britain, France, US, Italy, and Japan come
- Britain France and US main powers
- Many other officials from colonies (Ho Chi Minh, etc.) experienced the conference
- None of the defeated powers negotiated
- Russia wasn't involved, signed their own treaty
- Main issue was transforming land into nation states
- 5 peace treaties signed in Paris
- Not just treaty of Versailles
- Treaty of Sevres also important
- Austria and Hungary separate, sign different treaties
- Czechoslovakia and Yugoslavia are created
- Romania and Poland are given land
- Hungary loses about 2 thirds of territory and 73% of population
- Bulgaria lost Thrace to Greece
- Loss of sea access, very important
- New Austria much more homogeneous than new Hungary
- Map of Europe is transformed greatly
- New states are created in the Baltics too, later become associated with USSR
- Several Ottoman colonies change hands

Outcomes

- Major outcome is reparations imposed on Germany
- 132 billion gold marks demanded
- Germany assumes guilt for starting WW1
- Loses a significant amount of territory as well
- About a seventh of it's total territory
- Compared to countries like Hungary, got off pretty easily
- Lost all it's colonies as well
- Self Determination was the idea posed by Woodrow Wilson
- Idea of ending empires and splitting them up into democratically governed ethnic sovereign nations
- Promotes autonomous development
- 8 of the 14 points are about redrawing European borders
- Self determination was a Eurocentric concept limited mostly to central and eastern Europe
- Britain, France, US, and Japan come out easily
- Get to keep all of their colonies, none are given "self-determination"
- However, the idea inspires many revolutions and anti imperialist movements
- Britain colonies like Egypt, India, Iraq, Afghanistan, and Burma all have activism
- Same in French colonies like Algeria, Syria, Indochina, Morocco
- Wilsonian moment, hopes crushed
- Arabs are also not granted self determination at Paris 1919
- Delegates representing Arab groups are present, but calls are ignored
- Clear hierarchy of countries shows that only white elite countries allowed self determination
- Promises to create a Jewish homeland in Palestine
- Syria and Lebanon are French mandates, Iraq, Transjordan and Palestine are British
- Claim to Ottoman territory is codified in international law
- Promises made to 2 different groups, not all granted
- Key outcome is creation of the League of Nations
- Wasn't effective enough, but laid groundwork for the UN
- Focused on security, disarmament, and creating a system of dispute resolution
- Was able to effectively establish a world health organization

Self Determination and Aftermath

- Came out of a series of nationalist movements from multiethnic empires
- Based on the idea that countries should be ethnically homogenous
- Dominant idea of ethnostates being most effective
- Paris 1919 gives legal weight to this idea of nationalism
- Mismatch between number of ethnic groups and number of republics
- Caused a huge shuffling of refugees between countries
- Bulgaria now has a ton of refugees coming from places like Thrace, where they were rejected by Greece
- In 1923 Greece and Turkey exchange a huge number of citizens
- 1.5 million Christians come from turkey, 500,000 Muslims go to Turkey
- Armenian genocide is occurring at the same time
- Start of a new system encouraging deportation of ethnic minorities

Conclusion

- Old empires split up into smaller ethnostates
- New systems of democracy encouraged by US
- Discontent with peace treaties, unequal distribution
- Economic troubles prevalent
- Rise in right wing populist leaders, many dictators arise across Europe
- Over 2 thirds of Europe elect a dictator in the next 30 years
- Self determination tends to breed dictatorships apparently
