Causes of Emigration

- Key factor in emigration was industrialization
- Millions of people moving for job opportunities as jobs move to other colonies
- Search for wage labour
- New locations are attractive
- New methods of transportation facilitate easy emigration
- From 1840-1940 57 million Europeans leave Europe
- 91% of these emigrants cross the Atlantic
- Period of emigration never seen after this point
- Most places emigrating to are already industrialized like US, Argentina, Canada
- Migration clusters around trade routes
- Also major migration to southeast Asia and Manchuria at the same time
- Same thing happened with people from Malaysia, etc. moving to India
- Major migration locations were US, Canada, Argentina, Brazil
- All 4 got millions of immigrants over the early 1900s
- Population growth creates increased economic activity and drastic urbanization
- Common theme of cultural pluralism in these countries because of diversity of immigrants
- All countries in the Americas were open for immigration
- The cities experiencing the greatest growth were all on the east coast or at least connected closely to it via railroad
- Towns becoming cities over a small period of time
- Big cities are New York, Chicago, Montreal, Sao Paulo, Rio de Janiero, Buenos Aires
- Migration is not limited by policy
- Social networks are key, people tend to migrate following friends and family
- Emigration fever was a real thing, trend in society to move overseas

Gender and Migration

- Male dominated migration, two thirds of European migrants were male
- Unique movement, most migrations in history were female dominated
- New gender ideas about work
- Most new jobs in industrial nations were male dominated
- The typical strategy was sending one man away from the family and have them send back money to eventually bring over rest of family
- System encouraged male travel heavily
- 19 million women also migrated from Europe, still large number

Neo-Europes

- Other places in the world that are culturally and ethnically similar to Europe
- Become much more pronounced due to increase in European migration
- Also called settler colonial worlds
- Creates the divide between these settler colonial worlds vs the colonies populated by the people they exploited
- Many also displaced indigenous people in the Americas
- Focused on agriculture and controlling territory
- Vision of a national identity between members of the territory
- Focuses on seizing control of land and food/water sources to control land
- Settlers themselves were not necessarily aware of what they were doing but they were certainly complicit
- A key difference was rather than extracting the value and resources of the land, settler colonialism is transforming the land into your new home
- Occupy the territory and import your people, food, culture, religion etc.
- Key part was indigenous elimination, which came in many forms
- End result was Europeans outnumbering indigenous residents and overpowering them
- Settler colonialism relies heavily on mass immigration
- Non-European migrants were actively excluded from these settler colonies
- East coast societies had few pacific immigrants, west coast had a few more
- Furthered the divide between the temperate and tropical colonies
- Policies of discrimination against pacific immigrants were connected to settler colonialism
