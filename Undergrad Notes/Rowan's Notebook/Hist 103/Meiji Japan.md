**Meiji Restoration**

- Major political reorganization of japan
- Growing industrialization and global communication, increased
- Starts in 1816
- Lasted from 1868 to 1912
- Taisho and Showa lasted from 1912 to 1989
- Transition from feudal economics to modern society
- Signified an end to japans isolationism
- Embraced westernization for better of japan, adapted a lot of ideas from the west
- Forced to sign unequal treaties with European nations, were renegotiated under Meiji
- China was facing incursions from Europe too
- Japan asserted control over the European powers and refused to succumb to treaties
- Had a similar industrialization process to Germany
- Became a political rival to China

**Economic Expansion**

- Japans economy grows at 5% per year vs US
- Manufactured goods become major export for Japan
- 3 key sectors, textiles, shipbuilding and shipping, coal and metal production
- Textbook pillars of many other industrializing nations of the era
- Japanese manufacturers compete with Britain for textiles
- Silk especially is produced en masse
- Shipping boom, competing from west European shipping companies
- By 20th century they were a global shipping enterprise
- Japanese state fostered and encouraged industrialization
- State created railroads across japan, financed development
- Put tariffs on competing imported goods from other countries
- Many large monopolies (zaibatsu) arise
- Modern examples are Yamaha, produces wildly different things
- Zaibatsu cover a wide variety of sectors
- This is connected to an existing agrarian society
- About half of workers are still in agriculture
- Ongoing migration from farms to factories
- Rural society feeds industrial expansion
- Many exports are food and agriculture related
- Not a drain but a coexistence between these sectors
- Population goes from 35 million in 1880 to 55 million in 1919
- 29 km in 1872 to 1914 11,400km of railroad
- Common theme in industrialization
- Carry exported goods/crops to port and imported materials to factories
- Keep in mind the size of japan, 11,400 is a very extensive network

**Workers**

- Need more food, more markets, and transportation
- Average factory worker was a migrant or child of one
- Rural people moved to avoid exploitation
- Workers were paid particularly low wages compared to others
- Textiles were fuelled by mostly women and young girls from agrarian communities
- Worker exploitation was very common
- Massive gender pay gap in Japanese factories
- Annual factory turnover was 100% at some points
- Work conditions were terrible, women had it worse
- Incubators for disease, tuberculosis ran rampant between workers

**Empire of Japan**

- Japan became a major player in east Asia in this period, finally challenging china
- It later became a big force in ww2
- The concept of a Japanese Empire was started, territorial expansion began
- Many other east Asian nations used this same strategy to resist European incursion
- By 1890s Japan is met with resistance by going to war with China
- Sino-Japanese war from 1894-1895
- Japan attempts to free Korea from Chinese control and succeeds thanks to industrial progress
- First step in a series of expansions
- Has semi control over Korea, control over Taiwan and limited control in Manchuria
- Briefly seized key Chinese shipping routes on Liaodong peninsula
- Proof that Japan was a force to be reckoned with
- Russo-Japanese War 1904-1905
- Japan was very invested in controlling Korea and keeping it from China
- Russia was encroaching on Manchuria and Japan sought to chase them off
- Russia controlled railways to Liaodong peninsula
- Japan took some northern islands from Russia as well
- Asserted economic control in Manchuria
- Korea was an independent state controlled by Japan
- Forced the Korean military to disband in 1907, no other countries around to challenge
- Korea was eventually annexed in 1910
- Over small time period Japan acquired Korea, Taiwan, and some Russian islands as well as establishing a commanding economic presence in Manchuria

Conclusion

- Japan underwent huge economic change after 1868
- Influenced by industrial revolution, new technology jump started economy in 1870-1914
- Urbanization a huge force
- Urban workforce growing
- Women played important part in revolution
- Lots of worker exploitation, especially female exploitation
- 1890s-1920s Japan becomes key power in Asia
- Economic growth encourages imperial expansion to Korea, Russia, China
