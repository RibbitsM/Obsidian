- Key text for this is John Mearsheimer's "Why the Ukraine Crisis is the West's Fault" in Foreign Affairs (2014)
- Refutes the common assumption that the Ukraine crisis is the fault of Putin's imperial aggressions
- Argues that the true fault is that of NATO and the West, and NATO enlargement prompted Russia to respond by invading Ukraine
- Also blames the EU and American meddling in Ukraine's domestic affairs
- The fact that Mearsheimer ignores any domestic politics in Ukraine is evidence of his neorealist viewpoint
- Talks a lot about "backyards" in the article, theme that great powers seek to influence their neighbouring countries
- Attacks the liberal view that peace can be secured in Europe through spreading ideas of rule of law and free market economies
- Argues that Russia did not attack the Baltic states when they joined NATO in 2004 since Russia was too weak to respond
- In addition, the Baltic States applied to join in 1992, but were passed over in the first round of enlargement in 1999
- In 2008, pro-western leaders in Ukraine and Georgia both applied to join NATO, but only received an endorsement instead of an offer to join
- Russia's invasion of Georgia later that year is given as evidence that Russia wanted to prevent Ukraine and Georgia from joining the NATO
- Also feared EU expansion, which Russia saw as a Trojan horse for NATO
- Mearsheimer refers to the spread of Western values and democracy as a tool for power politics, not an end unto themselves
- He then brings up the hypothetical of China inviting Canada into a military alliance similar to NATO
- The issue with this is that Canada does not feel threatened by the US, while the Ukraine-Russia scenario is much different
- Turning point in the crisis was when Yanukovych rejected an EU economic deal in favor of a counteroffer given by Russia
- This was after he had publicly promised to sign the agreement
- Caused anti government protests who were upset that Ukraine was moving away from liberal values, causing Yanukovych to flee to Russia
- Mearsheimer argues that the US backed this coup
- Shortly after the coup, Russian forces took Crimea
- At the time, Ukraine was mainly struggling with domestic issues, but fuelled by a rise of liberal values
- Ukraine's purpose as a buffer, in neorealist thought it is only natural for a great power to want a neighbouring state in it's sphere of influence
- If NATO set up shop on Moscow's doorstep, Russia would have no choice but to retaliate
- Argues that the reason the US didn't see this coming was that they were blinded by their liberal views and failed to see the machinations of geopolitics right in front of them
- Putin was working with realism, and Clinton was working with liberalism
- Denies the charge that Putin is mentally unbalanced and argues that every action he has carried out made perfect strategic sense
- States are aggressive, but they are also rational
- Brings up that even if Russia was to invade Ukraine, they would have significant trouble
- Putin understands that he cannot subdue Ukraine, and is acting defensively, not offensively
- Mearsheimer's solution to the crisis is a neutral Ukraine that supports neither the West nor Russia
- NATO should reject the membership of Ukraine and Georgia and provide financial support to the Ukraine via the IMF, EU, and the great powers
- Argues that since the US and EU did not want to come to the defense of Ukraine, it is not a strategic interest for them
- However, if it was not in their interest, then what do they have to gain from harassing Russia?

**Gotz Article**

- Argues that the Ukraine crisis is the product of geopolitics, similar to Mearsheimer
- EU eastern partnership a point of tension with Russian government, trojan horse for NATO
- States that tensions will increase, but no full scale invasion will occur
- Instead, Russia will fuel ethnic conflict in Ukraine to prevent them from joining NATO
- The example of a Canada-China agreement comes up again
- Brings up that this is good news, saying that Russia is not a revisionist power
- Bad news is that Russia will always seek regional power regardless of leadership

**Ukrainian Nuclear Deterrent**

- In the wake of the cold war, Ukraine had the third largest nuclear arsenal in the world
- In Mearsheimer's view, this qualifies Ukraine for great power status
- Two reasons for a nuclear Ukraine
- First, we need peace between Russia and Ukraine, and they will not get help from others
- Ukraine cannot defend themselves without nuclear weapons
- The second is that Ukraine is unlikely to give their nuclear weapons to Russia, which turns out being incorrect
- Nuclear deterrence promotes stability, and to prevent future expansion of Russia a nuclear Ukraine would be needed to keep them in check
