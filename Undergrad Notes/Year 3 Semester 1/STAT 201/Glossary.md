Sample: collection of observations taken from the population  
Population: All possible observations  
Observation: information on one unit, or one case of the data being collected  
Parameter: measured quantity of a population or sample, like mean, variance, etc. Generally used to refer to the population as the "population parameter"  
Point Estimate: single value estimate of a parameter, so if the sample mean of sample number 5 is 56.6, then our point estimate for that sample is 56.6, used to refer to samples since it is an estimate  
Generally speaking, the population parameter is the "real" value and the point estimate is the estimate derived from a sample  
Estimates: point estimates from many different samples  
Estimator: Equation for finding an estimate?  
Sampling Distribution: distribution of all point estimates of all possible samples in a population  
Variance: squared mean distance from the sample mean  
Standard Deviation: variability across a single sample, square root of the variance  
Standard Error: standard deviation of a sampling distribution  
Confidence Interval: instead of a single point estimate, we give a range that the population parameter is likely within
