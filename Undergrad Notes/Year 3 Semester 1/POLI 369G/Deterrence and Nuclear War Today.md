**US and Russia**

- Princeton University did a simulation of nuclear war between the US and Russia
- Simulation argues that conflict would start with warning shots
- Starts with escalation of a conventional war between NATO and Russia
- To prevent a NATO advance, Russia fires a nuclear warning shot which is responded to with a single tactical nuclear strike
- This leads Russia to escalate and fire 300 warheads to halt NATO troops, and NATO responds with 180 warheads
- Finally, the US responds to the destruction of Europe with a counterforce strike of 600 warheads aimed at Russian missiles
- Russia launches on warning, with the final result being millions of casualties after only a few hours
- Now that we are in full scale nuclear war, each side launches a countervalue attack skyrocketing the death toll to a total of 91.5 million dead
- An all out war would cause a minimum of 360 million immediate casualties

**India and Pakistan**

- In the case of a regional war between the two powers there would be about 100 15-kiloton weapons launched
- These would target urban areas, causing anywhere between 27-50 million deaths on the low end and up to 150 million on the high end
- In this scenario it's a limited and tactical war, with a 7 day simulation where increasingly large urban centers are targeted every day
- Start with military targets, and finally major urban centers
- Of course this assumes that India and Pakistan don't use all their warheads
- This model also argues that a full scale war between India and Pakistan could cause a nuclear winter
- Even in the absence of a US-Russia war, we could still see a doomsday scenario from a regional conflict
- For other possibilities we have India-China, US-China, Israel-Iran, Iran-Saudi Arabia, Russia-Ukraine, and just North Korea and anyone
- Also the possibility of a three-way war between India, China, and Pakistan
- Of course, Iran and Saudi Arabia don't currently have nuclear weapons, but it is entirely possible that they could build them on fairly short notice
- Ukraine also doesn't have nuclear weapons, but it is possible that Russia may use tactical nuclear weapons on Ukraine
