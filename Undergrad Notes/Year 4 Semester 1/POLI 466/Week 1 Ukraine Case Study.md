- How to legally justify the 2014 annexation of Crimea?
- A couple key arguments are advanced by Russian legal scholars
- One is the protection of Russian compatriots abroad
- The Russian government loosely applies this definition to ethnic Russians, Russian speakers, etc.
- This idea of enacting the principle of self defense to justify invading a sovereign state is common is hostage rescue situations
- For example, during the Entebbe Crisis an Air France plane going to Israel was hijacked by the PLF and rerouted to Uganda, where the passengers were held hostage
- In response, Israel sent in 100 commandos to free the passengers with the help of Kenya, killing 45 Ugandan soldiers in the process
- The main one is the result of a referendum held in Crimea where the majority of people voted for Crimea to join Russia, although the referendum had no oversight
- This plays into the principle of self-determination, and the example of Kosovo is often cited as a justification
- While its meaning and significance has changed greatly over time, it originated with Woodrow Wilson's Fourteen Points that eventually led to the creation of the UN Charter
- It's not explicitly described, but the term is used to describe how European states like Austria and Poland should be treated
- However, the dominant interpretation of the principle relates to the decolonization process
- Generally the territory seeking self-determination is geographically distinct from the country controlling it
- The principles set out by the 1933 Montevideo Convention are also used as a loose definition of what a state is
- Kosovo can also be used as a way to justify humanitarian intervention on the part of Russia
- There is also the argument that Crimea invited Russia in by holding the referendum
- It's important to note that Russia isn't just doing whatever they want, they are attempting to justify their actions through international law
- This sometimes backfired, as when Russia accused Ukraine of committing genocide against ethnic Russians in Eastern Ukraine, which required them to accept the jurisdiction of the ICJ

**2022 Invasion**

- One thing that's important to remember is that Putin spent the year or two before the invasion of Ukraine in almost total isolation
- He was extremely paranoid about COVID, had limited interaction with only a few hawkish advisors
- This was a moment of weakness for the US and NATO, many countries were questioning why the alliance even existed
- The US had an attempted coup, China was enemy number one and Europe was infighting
- But the attack on Kyiv not only stalled, but it also unified NATO and forced them to put their energy into supplying Ukraine
