**ICC and ICJ**

- Discussions that would eventually create the ICC began in the aftermath of WWII with the goal of establishing individual culpability and punishment for war crimes
- The Rome Statute of the International Criminal Court finally establishes the institution in 1998
- Prior to this, these issues were handled by special criminal tribunals like the ICTY and the ICTR
- Both of these bodies were created by the UNSC, the only international power great enough to authorize such an act
- The ICC inherits DNA from these tribunals, but expands its jurisdiction to all signatories who ratify
- Territory also plays a part, US soldiers in Afghanistan were investigate despite the US not ratifying the Rome Statute
- For example, Putin is being charged by the ICC for forced removal of children from Russian-occupied Crimea since it occurred on Ukrainian territory and targeted Ukrainian nationals
- Individuals can also be charged for aggression which is basically ordering the violation of Article 2(4), but a state has to ratify a special addendum to the Rome Statute
- The same thing is going on in Gaza, as the Palestinian Authority is allowed to accept the jurisdiction of the ICC as an observer state in the UN
- The ICJ on the other hand, deals with states and not individuals
- In the context of the war in Gaza, the ICJ is important because it is still accepting a case against Israel despite them not accepting compulsory jurisdiction of the ICJ
- This is because Israel signed the 1948 Genocide Convention, which means they accept the ICJ's jurisdiction in cases of genocide and this is what South Africa is accusing them of
- States are allowed to withdraw their acceptance of jurisdiction, but only before another state has filed against them
- Some countries like the UK accept the jurisdiction in name, but ignore any cases brought against them
