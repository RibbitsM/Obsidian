Empire of Migration

- Huge period of migration in Europe, east Asia, and Africa
- 1815-1830
- 51.7 million Europeans migrate elsewhere
- Strategy is to invade new areas with migrants
- Helps establish British power and culture
- By 1929 71% of British immigration is within the empire
- Official policies of encouraging women to move in order to maximize British gene spreading
- Indians are the largest group of empire migrants
- More than even British people
- 2 way flow, British people moving to colonies and Indian migrants moving away
- Movement around bay of Bengal, East Africa, south east Asia
- Structurally related
- Managed by the British elite
- Middle class of migrants forms police, merchants, labourers
- Short distance mobility for Labourers
- Mobility is key to supporting the flow of the empire
- 40,000 Indians move to Africa
- Impact on colonies receiving migrants is huge
- British immigration plays a key role in shaping Canada
- EX: Trinidad has 1300 British but 47000 Indian, affects Trini culture
- Control of migration is a key theme
- White settler colonies are concerned about mobility of Indians, Africans etc.
- Lead to policies allowing whites to travel but not Asians or South Asians
- Restrictions lead to several predominantly white colonies
- South Africa, New Zealand, Canada, Australia
- First two enact policy of Natal formula
- Literacy test preventing Indian labourers from migrating into colonies
- Even if labourers could speak multiple languages, officials would test them on increasingly obscure languages for the literacy test
- Canada created the head tax and Gentleman's Agreement to prevent Chinese and Japanese migration
- Gentleman's Agreement was an agreement between Japan and the Canadian government allowing Canada to limit Japanese immigration without Japan's treaty being violated
- Canada also established the Continuous Journey Law to prevent Indians from migrating while giving Ukrainians and other Europeans a pass

Civilizing Mission

- "white man's burden"
- Ideology of the British empire was to spread "civilization" to every corner of the globe
- Also a religious mission, missionary colonialism was a huge phenomenon
- Sense of superiority vs pity grows over the century
- Church was prioritizing spreading Christianity
- While missionaries had priority over military and economy, their interests were often connected

Important book: Edward Said, Orientalism

- Orientalism is Said's word for the ideology of white, Christian, European superiority over the various other civilizations Britain colonized
- Argues that Europeans see themselves as the only true modern civilization, all other countries are in a plight
- It was the right and responsibility to colonize these fallen empires and raise them up
- Became an imperial ruling strategy
- Study of other cultures, analysis of discussions about colonial countries was filled with pity and disdain
- Formed a society based on prejudice based entirely on how the process of colonialism occurred
- Never an official policy, but rather was the predominant culture and thoughts of the citizens of Europe at the time
- Not just an excuse for economic expansion but an ingrained thought

Anticolonialism

- Anticolonialism movement emerged not long after rise of colonialism
- Two examples, Indian National Congress (1885), and African National Congress (1912)
- Both important local movements attempting to remove colonialism
- 2 key figures: Gandhi and Pixley Seme
- Gandhi moves to London to do law in 1888
- Works in the Natal colony as lawyer, elite member of colonial society
- Discovers white supremacy and restriction of rights, becomes interested in being a civil rights advocate
- Seeing how Indian people were treated in South Africa was a big part in opening Gandhi's eyes to the nature of the British Empire
- Growing violence and discontent shapes his desire for Indian independence
- Writes Hind Swaraj, key text for denunciation of British colonialism while living in South Africa
- Becomes more important in Indian independence movement
- Becomes advocate for rejecting British culture, non violent resistance, passive disobedience
- Pixley Seme, born in Natal
- Goes to school in US, studies law in Oxford
- Lived in South Africa (Natal) same time as Gandhi
- Founded South African Native Farmers Association
- Established to resist British system marginalizing black farmers
- Encourages farmers to buy their own land and gain economic independence
- Laws are put in place to prevent Blacks from purchasing land
- This was the first major segregationist legislation, led to apartheid eventually
- Creates African National Congress, sows seeds for the resistance of apartheid
- Same organization Nelson Mandela would eventually lead
