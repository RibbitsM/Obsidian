- Many wars were won by the Ottoman empire, causing areas with large Shi'a populations in the Ottoman Empire
- After WWI the British carve up the Ottoman Empire and split it into three provinces
- Mandate with a Shi'a majority and a Sunni and Kurdish minority
- Shi'a not treated well prior to the division and the British maintained this mistreatment
- Made tensions worse by installing puppet rulers to the benefit of the British
- Many revolts suppressed violently in Iraq
- Still a monarchy in 1939 but instability
- Multiple coup attempts and changes of government
- Eventually the entire royal family is killed alongside the prime minister in 1958
- Conflict between communist party and Ba'ath party
- Saddam Hussein was born in a village outside of Baghdad in 1937
- Gets involved with the Ba'ath party and acts as a hitman/thug
- Rising in the Ba'ath party by means of violence, flees whenever the communists are in power
- Meets the CIA in Egypt during this time
- A relative from his village gains power in the govt and helps Hussein greatly
- Became president in 1979 but was pulling strings before then
- At this time Iraq had nationalized oil and was making loads of money
- Iraq was one of the most progressive states in the Middle East, more than even the US and Canada
- Well funded education and healthcare, free daycare, representation of women in the workplace
- Created a police state modelled after Stalin's govt
- Cult of personality, similar to Stalin and Assad, his face seen almost everywhere you go
- One of the most brutal middle eastern rulers until the rise of Assad
- Hung 17 supposed Israeli spies as one of his first acts
- Filmed most of his killings to circulate them around the country
- Torture chambers found in his home
- Only gets worse after the war

The War Begins

- Shi'a compose most of the country but face a good amount of persecution
- Even though most leaders were Sunni, there was no clear cut hatred between the groups
- Not all Shi'a supported Iran even though it was a Shi'a state
- Mostly divided by class, middle class supported Iran, lower class supported the communist party
- During the war most Shi'a stayed loyal to the Iraqi military
- Khomeini calls to export the Islamic revolution, causing concern in many countries where Shi'as are oppressed
- A few months after this Saddam becomes president, aspired to rule the middle east
- Iran was a major power, and the revolution created a huge power vacuum
- Divide between the republics and monarchies in the middle east, so the Iraq-Saudi alliance was unusual
- When the war went wrong and Iraq was losing money, they were being bankrolled by the gulf countries
- From the outside it appears that Khomeini has power, but it's really a struggle of multiple different groups with Khomeini at the top of a divided govt
- Causing problems in Iraq, Kurds hiding in Iranian border towns were attacking with support from Khomeini
- Some Iraqi Shi'a were protesting against the government violently
- Not an unavoidable conflict, the two governments were in dialogue up until the war
- Al-Sadr, a famous Shi'a priest protests against the government and is executed in 1980
- Bans the Islamic Call and deports many Shi'a, a tipping point for the war
- Saddam sees the war as neutralizing a significant threat and seizing an opportunity to claim easy territory

The Iran-Iraq War

- Tremendous loss of life and billions of dollars in damage
- Border towns on both sides most heavily devastated
- The war helped Khomeini consolidate his rule, the opposite of Saddam's intentions
- Instead of fracturing Iran, Khomeini united the country under the goal of nationalism
- Also used the war as an excuse for mass purges in almost all institutions in society to get rid of any enemies in his country by labelling them as traitors
- Works so well that Saddam asks for a ceasefire and Khomeini refuses
- Prior to the war, the US had an up and down relationship with Saddam
- A couple years into the war, picked the Iraqi side after the Iranian hostage crisis and Iranian threats against Saudi Arabia
- Provides training, weapons and money including equipment for starting a nuclear program and bioweapons division (Weapons of Mass Destruction)
- At the same time, the US also reached out to sell weapons to Iran via proxies
- The Soviets were also selling weapons to both sides
- Saddam thought that the US and Soviets would step in to stop the conflict but they did not
- The US gave tacit approval to Saddam's atrocities by providing him with WMDs
- Iranian-Syrian alliance got stronger, so did the Iraq-Gulf state alliance

Genocide

- Kurds living in the north of Iraq were frequently treated poorly as they were often involved in US and Iran funded uprisings
- During the war, Kurdish nationalists saw it as an opportunity to create their own nation in the north
- Ally with Iran to take over a good portion of the north, create air raid shelters and living in the mountainous regions
- Eventually Saddam disappears thousands of Kurdish men 12 and up to weaken the Kurdish population
- Absolutely no international outcry for doing this, empowers Saddam
- Saddam orders his cousin "Chemical Ali" to massacre the Kurdish population
- Destruction of villages, looting torture, mass displacement and chemical warfare
- Killed Kurds en masse by gassing air raid shelters and bombing survivors
- Again, no outcry from the international community

End of the War

- By 1988 the Iranian forces are exhausted and ask for a ceasefire
- Saddam doesn't want to accept, but US pressures him because the war is disrupting oil prices
- No real gains for either country, both economies drastically weakened
- After the war the US navy establishes a large presence in the Gulf to protect oil tankers and US economic interests
- Iraq economy in ruins, progressive reforms ended
- Need for money and US desire for retribution for Saddam's crimes leads to the Gulf War and US occupation of Iraq
