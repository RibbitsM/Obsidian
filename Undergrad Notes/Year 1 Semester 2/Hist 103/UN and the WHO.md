- The UN is a collaborative international organization made out of a group of independent states
- The increasing amount of sovereign nations led to the necessity of the UN
- Was a true continuation of the League of Nations, but much less egalitarian
- This is partly due to the UN security council, which involves 5 large nations with veto power
- This over-centralization of power is the UN's advantage
- Product of debates around international order since 1900s
- Stated goals of the UN are to prevent world wars, promote fundamental human rights, maintain international law, and promote higher standard of life
- The UN is a direct product of World War II, and is largely formed by a desire to move on from WWII and prevent a similar situation emerging again
- More developmental approach vs an exploitative one
- Initially created by the US, the UK, the USSR, and the Republic of China
- Important thing to note is that this was not the PRC, this was Taiwan
- Met in San Francisco, London, and finally NYC where they meet today
- UN contains the General Assembly, Security Council, International Court of Justice, and UN Economic and Social Council
- The Security Council has 5 permanent members with veto power
- The International Court existed long before the UN but was incorporated into the structure of the UN
- The UN ESC contains many institutions such as the IMF, World Bank, and WHO
- UN stance of colonialism was highly convoluted
- Rename to "trusteeships" which were League of Nations colonies
- US tried to distance themselves from European colonialism, but the UK and France tried to reassert colonial control in SE Asia
- An important step in the progression of the UN was giving independence to countries like India where the leader Jawaharlu Nehru advocated for global anticolonialism
- The UN gave a platform for leaders like Nehru to push decolonization
- The UN officially denounced the apartheid system in South Africa and their mistreatment of South Asians
- Nehru took this issue to London in an attempt to challenge the British empire
- This set the stage for the role of the General Assembly as a place for toothless global discourse
- The UN has no actual power over any country, but is still a respected platform

The World Health Organization

- Created in 1946-7
- Proposed by Brazilian and Chinese public health officials
- Important moment to show how the UN gave weaker countries power
- A perpetuation of the earlier League of Nations Health Organization
- However, while the hierarchy is the same the WHO is much more stable
- Before WWII, France had a powerful international health office that did not want to be replaced
- The Pan American Sanitary Bureau was a similar organization that was led by the US and focused on South America
- The UN was successful because it replaced all three, and centralized power
- Formal support from the UN made it the definitive international health organization
- Gained significant financial support from member nations in the first couple decades
- The WHO emerged at a time where governments being responsible for the health of their citizens was finally becoming a common thing
- The constitution of the UN even mentions things like mental health and children's health back in the 1940s
- Some major achievements of the WHO includes controlling the Egyptian Cholera epidemic, created fellowships of medical professionals, and worked eradicated malaria and smallpox with smallpox being successfully eradicated
- Expanded immunization programs to cover diphtheria, whooping cough, tetanus, measles, and polio
- Transcended political lines and encouraged global cooperation in times of Cold War distrust
- The US and USSR actually worked together closely on the distribution of vaccines in the 60s
- Some recent goals include combating AIDS, childhood obesity, and social determinants of health

WHO and the Cold War

- The UN and the WHO are products of the old imperial powers, and are really continuations of the League of Nations and it's components
- Extreme overrepresentation of Americans and European leadership in the initiatives and goals of the UN and WHO
- Focused on disease control rather than social wellbeing in general
- Agenda that prioritizes the developed world but does help the developing world
- Collaboration continued despite the Cold War
- By looking at a medical rather than military history, things look very different
- Another goal developed by the Cold War is population control
- North Atlantic idea of saving the world and establishing a US-led world
- The worlds problems are related to overpopulation in the developing world
