Pan-Arabism

- Gamal Nassar leads a coup with Naguib in Egypt
- Nassar is figurehead for Pan-Arabism in the early Cold War
- Replaced a constitutional monarchy, partly anti-democracy
- Banned the communist party from Egypt
- Sought to end the British occupation in 1952
- British authority had been present in the country since WWI
- Important driving factor behind the revolution and shaped their policies following the revolution
- Makes an agreement that involves withdrawal of British troops, and nationalize Suez Canal
- Motivated by anti-imperialism, also nationalize British and French companies similarly to Cuban revolution
- Economic development driven by Egyptian socialism, improve access to education and healthcare
- Active participants in the Non-Aligned Movement and worked with the US and USSR at points
- Helped organize the Bandung conference
- Opposed the Baghdad pact organized by the US because it increased US and UK military influence in the area
- Aligns with goals of the Bandung conference
- Not necessarily anti US or anti USSR
- Most significant event was the creation of the United Arab Republic made of Egypt and Syria together
- Was a country for 3 years between 1958 and 1961
- Quickly became inequal where Egyptian interests were imposed on Syrian leaders which caused it's demise
- Signified a time of change where national identities were not yet set in stone
- Iraq also briefly wanted to join the UAR but decided against it due to local politics
- Pan-Arabism drew on anti-imperial ideas and desires for strength and linguistic unity
- Was eventually stifled by competing interests, differing ideologies and foreign influence

Suez Crisis and Israeli Conflict

- In 1948 a failed military offensive in Israel encouraged Pan-Arabism
- Creation of the state of Israel heavily influenced the future of the Middle East
- Important repercussions for neighbouring countries as well
- Changes in leadership in every country that fought against Israel
- Public lost faith in their leaders for not being able to stop Israel and replaced them through re-election, coups, and assassinations
- British troops had just left Egypt when Nasser nationalized the Suez Canal which concerned Britain and France
- Came up with a plan to retake the canal with the assistance of Israel as well as replace Nasser
- Backfired horribly and was denounced by both the US and USSR, and led to the resignation of Anthony Eden
- Solidified Nasser's place as a legitimate leader in the Arab world, made him a hero
- Gave him more influence to spread his ideas to the Middle east in the following years
- From 1948-1967, Jordan and Egypt controlled key parts of modern-day Israel
- In 1967 the uneasy peace breaks due to growing tension over sea access through the Gaza Strip and water access via the West Bank
- Israel launches a pre-emptive strike in fear of violence from Egypt and Jordan and takes control of both regions
- Also acquires the Golan Heights from Syrian control
- Diminishes Nasser's status by humiliating him and set the stage for interactions going forward
- In 1973 Egypt and Syria try to reclaim these territories and fail yet again, loses more status
- Egypt signs a peace treaty with Israel in 1979
- Trans-Jordan becomes Jordan after losing West Bank
