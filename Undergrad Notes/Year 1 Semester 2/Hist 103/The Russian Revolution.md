Definition

- Tsarist government of Russia is overthrown in March 1917
- Communist government established in November 1917
- Often called the October Revolution
- Because Russians used the Julian Calendar
- Revolution that led to the establishment of the Soviet Union

What is Communism?

- Ideology pioneered by Karl Marx
- Promotes power of the lower class
- Advocates for sharing of resources and equality for all
- Considered natural alternative to capitalism, a more tiered unequal system
- A "revolution" is required for communism
- Capitalism and those who uphold it must be overthrown by the downtrodden workers
- System of social organization
- Economy and society are controlled heavily by the state and dominated by one party
- Created as response to extreme worker exploitation and poor working conditions
- Very industry focused, obsessed with factories and assembly lines
- Very materialist, focused on the meaning behind factors of production
- Role of the state heavily emphasized
- Key document is The Communist Manifesto
- Published in London, written in German by Karl Marx and Friedrich Engels
- 2 German intellectuals
- Many different terms for similar concepts
- Socialist, Communist, Marxist, Bolshevik
- All mean similar things
- Bolsheviks are created from the Russian Social Democratic Labour Party
- Vladimir Lenin changed led RSDLP, changed name to Russian Communist Party early 1918, formed USSR in 1918

Causes

- Industrial workers in the cities were very unhappy
- Overcrowding prevalent in cities
- Horrible living conditions
- Industrialization came later to Russia than in other places
- Russian working class was relatively new
- Huge increase in coal, iron and steel production between 1887 and 1913
- Population doubles in major cities in Russia over this time period
- Compared to other countries, industrial working class established late
- Very close to start of WW1 and industrial revolution
- St Petersburg renamed to Petrograd and the Leningrad
- WW1 played a key role in fueling Russian revolution
- Russia had poor leadership and low quality equipment
- Battle of Tannenberg was a major loss for Russia
- One of several disastrous battles against Germany
- Tsar Nicholas 2 assumes leadership in 1915
- Double whammy, blamed directly for poor military performance and also not present to deal with rising discontent
- Loses favor across the board
- Economic situation worsens rapidly
- Severe food shortages
- Many civilians protesting, demand that Russia pull out of WW1 to feed citizens
- Protest in Petrograd for "Peace in Bread"
- Military sides with protestors over the government
- Tsar abdicates in March 1917 (February Revolution), leaving a power vacuum in the government

Revolution

- Weak provisional government formed in Tsar's absence
- Petrograd Soviet (council) of Workers and Soldiers forms
- Two main powers, Petrograd Soviet constantly criticizes government
- Bolsheviks and Mensheviks in Petrograd Soviet, some Mensheviks are in the government
- In September the Bolsheviks take over the PS, challenge the government
- Period defined by struggle, between government and soviet, and between Bolsheviks and Mensheviks
- WW1 is still going, 1 million soldiers die during this period
- Food demands still not met in absences of Tsar
- Provisional government can't make peace with Germany without conceding significant territory
- In the end Russia ends up losing Russian Poland
- Elites pressuring government to not give up Poland
- Bolsheviks gain popularity in 1917 through criticism and propaganda
- Leon Trotsky is elected chairman of the PS
- Distribute propaganda pamphlets, run the _Pravda_ newspaper
- In November 1917 (October Revolution), overthrow government in a coup

Post Revolution

- Dual power period ends, Bolsheviks now in dominant control
- Redistributes land to peasants and seeks to end war
- Give control of factories to workers
- Follows concept of "self-determination", non-Russian nationalities given some individuality
- Still state system, elite rules on behalf of the workers but not worker controlled
- Bolsheviks sign peace treaty in March 1918
- Allows Bolsheviks to meet the demands of workers and peasants, gains favor of public
- Abandonment of commitments to Allies
- Allows Germany to rally temporarily, still pushed back by allies
- Huge swaths of Russian land occupied by central powers for 8 months
- Eventually almost all this territory is reacquired by 1940

Conclusion

- Discontent of working class and elite with the Tsar
- Abdicates, power vacuum
- Provisional government can't meet demands
- Bolsheviks seize power, meet demands of public
