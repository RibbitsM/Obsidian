Fascism in Italy

- Fascism is a political ideology
- Goal is to rearrange society under a corporatist structure
- Opposed to other ideologies such as marxism and conservatism
- Bring companies, church, education all under control of the government
- Usually concerned with national decline or cultural decline
- Concerns about social change used to gain supporters
- Not always totalitarian and right wing but usually is
- Comes from the italian word Fascio
- Means bundle or sheaf of sticks
- Idea that by bringing many weak parts together creates strength
- Initially leftist idea, strength through unity
- Italian fascism was the first movement of it's kind
- Benito Mussolini or Il Duce (the leader) was the leader of Italy
- Created a cult of personality around himself
- Mussolini was the leader of a Milan fascio, later the Fasci Italiani di Combattimento
- This became the Italian Fascist Party
- Fascism came to power democratically
- Political instability in Italy at the time
- 5 governments formed from 1919-21
- After the 1921 election the Fascists were part of the National Bloc that had 20% of the vote
- Hold a coup in 1922
- Fascist militia seize control of several municipal governments
- Called "The March on Rome"
- 30,000 black shirts gather in Rome and demand power
- Govt tries to establish martial law
- On Oct 29th Mussolini is invited to form the government
- Offered stability to the fragmented government
- Other parties encouraged this move
- Military and business supported the Fascists
- Because of a small show of force, the Fascists gained the support of the political system
- Over time the Fascists slowly turned Italy into a totalitarian state
- Started by passing the law that largest party gets instant two thirds majority
- Turned them from a minority into a majority
- Next step was to replace elections with the Fascist Grand Council
- Abolished elections, turned into one party government
- Elections held only within Fascists
- Established Chamber of Fasci and Corporations in 1939, replaced Italian parliament
- Changed system from within

Italian International Aggression

- International discourse was key at this point
- Reasserting colonial rule in Libya was an important goal
- Historic Italian minorities living outside Italy were also targeted for benefits
- Italians in Austria, Yugoslavia, Argentina, Brazil, US, given education etc.
- Road to forming a sense of international Italian nationalism
- After 1935 theme of constant intervention emerges
- Military aggression is a core tenet of fascism
- Idea of a Darwinist struggle between nations, strong deserve to rule
- Win war with Ethiopia, establish it as an Italian colony
- Italy invades Albania in 1939 for expansionist reasons
- Gets actively involved in the Spanish Civil War, led to Franco's regime being established
- Italy joins Germany in invading France and invades Greece as well
- Demonstrates how important aggression and expansionism is to Italian Fascism

Nazi Rise to Power

- German author Arthur Moeller van den Bruck writes "The Third Reich"
- Calls for a national restoration, call to arms against the treaty of Versailles
- Basically calling to dismantle the current political structure and establish a right wing nationalist government
- Blamed capitalism and liberal democracy for the problems of the Weimar Republic
- First Reich was the Holy Roman Empire 962-1806
- German speaking nobles ruling a significant chunk of central Europe
- Has little to do with Rome
- Second Reich was Imperial Germany, ruled over by the Kaiser that lasted from 1871-1918
- Bruck admired Mussolini and sought to replicate them
- Bruck and the Nazis hated eachother even though they had similar views
- Nazis were an unsuccessful fringe party for most of the 1920s
- Held only 12 seats prior to the stock market crash
- Stock market crash led to constant failing governments, allowed the Nazis to claim 196 seats
- Radical solutions seemed more appealing in the face of the constant instability
- Communists also gained significant support, but weren't able to beat out the Nazis in the end
- Instability leads to more support for extremists
- Hitler is appointed chancellor on Jan 30, 1933
- Only 3 Nazis were cabinet ministers, moderate right wing parties sought to control the Nazis
- Reichstag burns down on Feb 27, Nazis blame it on the Communists and get them banned from the government
- Parliament passes the Enabling Act in March allowing the chancellor to act without the parliament
- Thugs on the street prevent socialists and labour advocates from physically entering the parliament
- German government votes itself into a dictatorship with a 2/3 majority

Conclusion

- Fascism came from Italian word for bundle meaning collective strength
- Fascism and dictatorships established through democratic process
- Both times was preceded by political instability
- Fascist movements focused on a national revival and aggressive military action
- Fascism began in Italy with Mussolini￼
