- Palestine is a place of religious significance for Muslims, Jews and Christians
- Several different historical kingdoms and empires that occupied this territory at some point or another
- Was controlled by Muslim rulers from 634 to 1918
- The British empire post-WWI did not reflect the historic region
- High religious diversity with a Muslim majority and a growing Jewish minority
- This created growing tension between these groups, and also with the British authority
- This culminates in the Arab Revolt which included a general strike and refusal to pay taxes
- This revolt prompted the British authorities to implement the White Paper in 1939 which limited Jewish immigration and stopped settlers from purchasing land
- Jews were being driven out of Europe due to immigration policy and were fleeing to Palestine
- This was partly led by British anti-semitism and partly colonial strategy

Zionism

- Theodor Herzl wrote a book discussing the need for a Jewish state
- Had the first Zionist Congress in 1897 where this idea of a Jewish state first emerged
- This was the spark that ignited the Israel-Palestine conflict
- The goal of Zionism was to create a home for Jewish people in Palestine
- This was a response to European anti-Semitism
- The Jewish state would be a refuge from European society and was also connected to the age of empires idea that every people needs a country and vice versa
- Zangwill said that Palestine is a country without a people, and Jews are a people without a country
- Mass immigration from the Russian Empire to places like the US and Argentina, only a few went to Palestine
- An important step towards achieving this was the Balfour Declaration
- This was where the British foreign minister sent a letter to Walter Rothschild, a famous Zionist and elite saying the British supported their cause
- The letter states that no non-Jewish communities would have their rights infringed on in any way
- The language used is "a national home" which doesn't explicitly imply a state
- Europeans had an interest is supporting Christian and Jewish settlers in Palestine
- Balfour stated that Zionism was rooted in traditions much more important than the interests of the Arabs
- In addition, this would give Britain access to the Suez Canal, which gave it a geopolitical motivation as well

1948

- After 1945 decisions are made very fast
- As tensions grow, and WWII and the Holocaust come to pass, support for Jews increases
- One of the first decisions made by the UN is the partition of Israel and Palestine
- About 1.2 million Arabs and 600,000 Jews living in the area
- In the partition, 55% of the land was given to the Jews and 45% was given to Muslims
- Jerusalem was located in the Arab section and controlled by the UN
- Proposed Palestine would be 99% Arab but the proposed Israel was 55% Jewish
- Accepted by Jews, but rejected by Arabs
- The international community supported it with the exception of Arab states like Turkey and India
- Was supported by basically all Jewish elites
- Not implemented, and violence begins in Palestine leading to a large number of Arab refugees fleeing the fighting
- After the British leave on May 14, 1948, the Jewish People's Council declares the state of Israel
- All surrounding Arab states intervene immediately to prevent a huge refugee crisis and total Jewish control
- Israel wins the ensuing battle and concludes in an uneasy peace by July 1949
- At this point the Gaza strip and West Bank are under Arab occupation

Conclusion

- Creation of Israel was the product of many factors
- This included the Jewish philosophy of Zionism and influx of immigrants
- Also British and European support was key
- The state was shaped by ongoing immigration and created Palestinian refugees
