Empire and Lebensraum

- This is the core goal of Nazi Germany
- Lebensraum means living space
- Goal of gaining rural agricultural land and populating Europe with mainly German people
- Empire expansion through genocide, agricultural production, and eugenics
- Some areas conquered by Germany were incorporated, some became protectorates or otherwise not fully occupied
- Permanent seizure of land for Germany, concept of lebensraum
- Not invented by the Nazis, tied to old ambitions
- Incorporation of Austria, Poland, Czechoslovakia all territories added to the empires
- Mazower viewed Hitler as an empire builder
- Modeled the third reich after the holy roman empire, the "first reich"
- Goals were to rule with a German elite with an ethnically diverse lower class below them
- Similar to how Britain ran India
- Nazis resented their lack of colonial territory
- Lebensraum dates back to 1860s
- Blood and Soil was the newer idea driving German imperialism
- Idea of restoring the essence of Germany
- National rejuvenation through 2 core aspects, conquest and agriculture
- Richard Walther Darre invented this idea
- Born to 2 German immigrants in Argentina
- Blood: German race
- Soil: Territorial expansion
- Became minister of food and agriculture
- Influenced military strategy, pushed his ideology on Germany
- Advocated for Germany to be agriculturally self sufficient
- Part of territorial acquisition was eliminating the existing population, and then replacing them with Germans
- Published the book "The Peasantry as Life Source of the Nordic Race"
- Was planning implementation of these ideas before rising to power
