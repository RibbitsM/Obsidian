The world in the 1900s

- World of empires and colonialism
- Racial hierarchies
- Patriarchal power
- Severe environmental damage
- More rights for citizens (education, health, democracy, mobility)
- Globalizations
- Rapid technological development and change

Second Industrial Revolution

- From 1870 to 1914
- Only called 2nd revolution for Europe, elsewhere is just industrialization
- Oil, electricity, steel, railroad, steamship, telegraph all become widespread
- Steel and oil become mass produced leading to more rail and boat travel
- New powers emerge, EX Germany becomes known for steel production during this period
- Urbanization becomes more pronounced, mass exodus to cities for factory jobs
- Global power is concentrated heavily in the north Atlantic, north Atlantic countries vie for power constantly
- New technology advances the pace of colonization, most of these technologies are first significantly implemented in the colonies

Communication and Travel Networks

- 3 highways of the empires
- Telegraphs, railroads, and steamships
- Ideas and culture spread rapidly thanks to these 3 advances, come mainly from colonies to Europe
- Infrastructure is erected in colonized nations quickly, workers work extensively on shipyards, laying track, and setting up telegraph lines
- Colonial life is affected greatly by these, connects colonies to Europe closer
- Increases globalization dramatically
- Canal building also a bigger phenomenon
- The Suez canal was built, rapidly increasing the rate of trade between the UK and India
- The panama canal was also built around this time, connecting the east and west us

Commodities

- Played an important role in connecting sites of industrialization to the rest of the world
- New commodities were needed from the exploited countries to fuel new technologies
- More infrastructure to harvest and transport more commodities
- Endless loop of new commodities and increased infrastructure
- Rubber, copper, oil, bauxite (aluminum), cotton, cocoa, tea, coffee, gold, diamonds, are the main new commodities
- All come from colonized nations
- Huge volume of imports and exports, unheard of before this era
- Materials go to Britain from colonies, manufactured goods come back
- Colonies protested this Eurocentric business model, preferring to do the manufacturing themselves
- This system only benefits Europe, not colonies

Economics of Empire

- Looking at economic data from colonial countries you can see exponential growth in almost every industry
- After 1895, demand and production for all the new technologies and commodities skyrocket
- All this goes to Britain, none of the wealth actually benefits the colonized country

Conclusion

- Industrialization key to imperial system in 1900
- Commodities central to growth of the imperial system
- Railway, steamships and telegraph open up new parts of the world to colonial exploitation
- Transported info globally, transported people and goods faster, lowered cost of transport
