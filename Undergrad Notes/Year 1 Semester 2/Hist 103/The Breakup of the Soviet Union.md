- Economic stagnation underneath Brezhnev in the 70s
- Falling behind in many areas, including technology and productivity
- Leadership aging, several leaders die in office
- Heavy military spending to keep up with the US
- Two important reforms implemented by Gorbachev
- Perestroika and Glasnost, or restructuring and openness
- Youngest member of the Politburo, thought that the government needed reform
- Goals were to improve socialism through gradual reforms, and embrace aspects of capitalism and democracy
- Maintained the status quo and made changes within that status quo
- The analogy of repairing an airplane midflight was used to describe Perestroika
- Parts of the Soviet bureaucracy also resisted these changes
- Free elections for parliament established in 1989
- There were aspects of voting and democracy in the Soviet Union, but this was far from free elections
- Still guaranteed Communist representation, but allowed opposition parties and representatives of smaller nationalities within the Soviet Union
- Became difficult to silence these groups again once they gave them voices

The Eastern Bloc

- Satellite states of the USSR in the Warsaw Pact, looked towards democracy and independence
- Gorbachev was opposed to use of force in response to movements in satellite states
- Allowed the solidarity movement in Poland to succeed in 1980
- Solidarity was a trade union that challenged the authority of the Communist party in Poland
- Sought worker's rights, less bureaucracy, and social change
- Poland held elections in 1989 alongside Soviet parliament elections
- Started in Poland and flowed over into Hungary
- Hungary opened it's borders, before 1989 soviet citizens weren't allowed to leave the Eastern Bloc due to need for manpower and support
- Soviet authority doesn't intervene even when refugees flee East Germany and Hungary
- Berlin Wall falls on November 9th, 1989 and symbolizes the end of the USSR
- Agrees on reunification between East and West Germany and withdraws from Germany

Soviet Nationalities

- USSR was a multiethnic empire, like most empires are
- Only about half the population was Russian
- Not many policies of assimilation, generally respected cultural diversity
- Significant ethnic minorities even within the Russian Federation
- Applies both to eastern Europe but also Siberia
- Used the word "nationalities" instead of "ethnicities"
- Diaspora of these nationalities did not necessarily line up with state lines
- 25 million Russians lived outside of Russia as citizens were encouraged to move around within the Soviet Union
- A key reason why the Russian Revolution was successful was because of nationality policy
- Korenization was a word referring to the creation of local and native leadership of Communist parties
- Pushed for modern ideas of nationalities as early as the beginning of WWII
- Emphasis on the autonomy and self-government of these nations within the USSR
- Called the Union of Soviet Socialist Republics, the republics were such a key part that it's included in the name
- Looked a lot like the affirmative action policies that the US would implement later in the 1990s
- Revolutionary ideas of how to distribute power implemented in the 1930s
- Nationalism was considered to be a product of capitalism but was seen as a necessary evil that could be used to the benefit of the Soviet Union
- In the mid 1980s there was protests asking for more and more autonomy and national representation in the Ukraine and Lithuania
- The 1989 elections gave these groups a voice after reforms opened the gates
- The Kremlin no longer had control over these groups
- 5 weeks after the fall of the Berlin Wall, the Lithuanian Communist Party splits off from the Soviet Communist Party
- Gorbachev refused to use force to fix the situation
- Came to visit the country and was met with mass protests
- Ukraine was the most important republic in the USSR, had most of the food production and industry
- Gorbachev never intended to give all these benefits to national independence movements
- Glasnost exposed their past transgressions, with less KGB power groups were able to move freely
- Democratization also encouraged independence movements, and ending travel bans increased exchange of ideas

Outcomes

- Over the next few years, several countries sign the Belovezh Accords to officially dissolve the USSR
- Some Baltic states don't even sign as they never considered themselves part of the USSR
- Ongoing tensions in Georgia, Crimea, Moldova, the Caucuses, and of course Ukraine
- Perestroika and Glasnost enabled national independence movements
- Allowed for changes in the satellite states which accelerated the breakdown
- New Russia was at peace with breakup but is rethinking some decisions made with small republics
