Berlin Wall

- Symbol of the clear divide between East and West Germany
- Second symbolic turning point in the occupation alongside the Berlin Airlift
- Solidified the partition of Germany
- Established very quickly and mostly unannounced
- Citizens who lived on one side and worked on the other left some people stranded
- Used as a piece of propaganda on both sides
- Built entirely on the East German side
- Called the "Antifascist Protection Wall" by the East German government
- Established to protect socialist utopia from corrupting influence of capitalism
- Called a tragedy by the Western side, emphasized the human element
- Converted the perception of Germany into a outpost of freedom/democracy
- JFK gives famous speech on the prospect in West Germany
- Surveillance system established to watch the wall
- The wall encircled West Berlin for 156 kilometers
- Had two walls with a 100m open area in between protected by guards, barbed wire and watchtowers

Forking Paths

- Wirtschaftswunder means economic miracle basically
- Huge economic expansion between the 50s and 60s
- By the 60s West Germany is an affluent society, buildings rebuilt and exports are high
- 0.5% unemployment rate helped by East German migrants and huge amounts of ongoing US investment
- Partly a natural recovery of a major economy
- Contrasted with East Germany that was under a centrally planned Soviet command economy
- After seeing success of West Germany shifted to the New Economic System
- Sought to close the gap by giving more autonomy and incentivizing worker productivity
- Focused on improving certain sectors
- Wanted to overtake without catching up
- Sought to overshadow West German production in key sectors
- Adequate state in East Germany
- Everyone was employed, almost all women worked
- Contrary to West Germany where many women were unemployed
- No scarcity of money or goods
- Better food supply than West Germany, focused on basics rather than luxuries
- Less meat, dairy, fresh fruits/vegetables
- West Germany grew in population much faster than East Germany
- At one point the population of East Germany actually fell slightly
- Millions of German migrants from East Germany and eastern Europe in general

Conclusion

- Germany divided
- Major economic boom in West Germany
- East Germany adequate not affluent
- Berlin wall becomes defining symbol of cold war
- Wall falling symbolizes end of communism/opening of east Europe
- Connected to Iron Curtain
