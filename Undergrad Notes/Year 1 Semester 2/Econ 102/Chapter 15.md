- The liquidity preference model describes demand for loanable funds
- Downward sloping so as interest rate rises, less is demanded
- Money supply is a vertical line that intersects the MD curve at the equilibrium
- If demand is above or below the level of supply, the interest rate will change to match it
- Therefore there is also an equilibrium interest rate
- The bank of Canada can lower this interest rate by increasing the money supply
- Can do this by buying bonds
- Purchasing or selling treasury bills affects the money supply
- Relates to the money multiplier, purchasing bills leads to an increase in supply
- Purchasing bills drives rate down, selling bills pushes it up
