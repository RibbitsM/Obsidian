1. In general a decrease in real GDP will cause an increase in unemployment and an increase in real GDP will cause unemployment to decrease, meaning there is a negative relationship. However this relationship can be observed in reverse sometimes because of other factors such as government policy and size of the labour force. For example even if the economy is doing well, raising the minimum wage can increase unemployment. On the other hand, businesses streamlining their hiring practices can decrease unemployment even in a recession. The actual answer is that after a prolonged recession people will give up on looking for a job and leave the labour force, creating a decrease in unemployment. In a similar way, after a period of growth many people will join the labour force, and in the time before they get jobs they will increase unemployment.
