- Discuss what ideology is and what it does
- Look over the main ideologies
- Main is the key word here, there are many more ideologies
- Every ideology does three things
- First, it describes the current political order, usually in a critical way
- Second, every ideology presents an ideal version of the political order
- Finally, it provides a way to transform the existing world order into it's ideal form
- Fukuyama wrote the book "The Last Man" declaring that the defeat of communism signified the end of political discussion

Liberalism

- Most common ideology in the western world
- Dominant western ideology, also used by many former colonies
- Dates back to the 17th century, has roots further back but mostly concerned with Locke
- Focuses on the individual and emphasized rights/freedoms, rationality, self interest and equality
- Each person is rational, equal and self interested, and the goal of the state is to respect their sovereignty and protect their rights and freedoms
- In the original theory these rights and freedoms were given to you by God
- Also included right to revolt against an unjust state
- Individual should be protected from their peers and political authority
- Equality of opportunity is important like nationalized exams
- Classical liberalism prefers negative freedoms, new liberalism prefers positive freedoms

Socialism

- Dates to the late 18th and early 19th century alongside the industrial working class
- Factory owners discovered that they could use women and children as labour for cheap or even free
- This led to the rise of thinking in terms of class, and led to Marx writing the Communist Manifesto
- Marx was the first to explain communism and turn it into a true ideology with a goal and endgame
- Working class felt vulnerable to the upper class who owned the law enforcement, factories, and government
- Considered the ethical and right path, and was historically inevitable
- People who own the property vs people who don't own property
- Communism was immensely appealing to the common person because it was an easy story to understand, allegory to the bible
- Focused on equality of outcomes, not structure
- Socialists believe in the good of humanity, and state that in the absence of control, people would cooperate and trust eachother
- There is inherent worth in all people and a shared human dignity
- Sourced from Protestant views and Martin Luther, we are all equal before god
- Utopian idea, endgame is a heaven/utopian idea
- So utopian that practical application is never considered true communism
- Contains strong elements of social design, and as such has authoritarian characteristics
- Violence is also considered necessary for creation of a communist society
- Things like labour laws, 8 hour work day and 5 day work week are all products of socialism
- A milder version is social democracy, which is essentially a soft version of communism that eliminates the authoritarian aspect
- Much more reformist and gradual version of the ideology

Conservatism/Neo-conservatism

- Conservatism is a very old ideology, maybe one of the oldest
- The modern version emerged in the 18th century from Edmund Burke
- Argued that the French Revolution was a terrible thing because it destroyed a proven institution that had functioned for thousands of years to make way for an idea
- Edmund Burke was the only philosopher in Europe who predicted the outcome of the French Revolution
- Burke advocated for a reform of the monarchy instead of a revolution, he did not necessarily agree with the monarchy, but he saw it as a better alternative to violent revolution
- Looks different depending on what is being conserved, but is unified by a desire for slow reform over revolution and fast change
- Values tradition and long standing institutions
- Works well with liberalism and capitalism, especially free markets and property rights
- Neo-conservatism began with the Bush administration and advocated for spreading your country's ideology across the globe by force
- Antithetical to the original spirit of conservatism by advocating for revolution in other countries
- Put extra emphasis on patriotism and had much more of a totalitarian view of society
- Encouraged imperialism in things like the Iraq War
