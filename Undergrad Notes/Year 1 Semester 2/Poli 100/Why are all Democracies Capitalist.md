- Basically referred to mob rule
- Furthered by the Iroquois, had universal suffrage and merit based promotion
- Liberal democracies emerged in 17th century England
- The French and American Revolutions caused this
- Farmers revolted because they were being taxed without any representation in government
- Washington led a huge army against these farmers, larger than the army that fought the British
- Huge amount of money spent, about the principle more than anything
- Origin of connection between democracy and capitalism
- These two ideologies both emerged through individual freedoms
- In England there was a growing rich middle class who were being taxed heavily by the king
- House of commons was richer than the house of lords
- Demanded representation in the government
- Won the civil war and gained the ability to regulate the budget
- People who became rich independent of their political position wanted to keep their money

Middle Class and Economic Power

- Voting was tied to income in England
- Democracy started with only rich white men voting
- New powerful middle class created by production technologies and global trade
- Many expeditions were privately funded, and were extremely lucrative
- Money did not necessarily translate to power, middle class had economic power but no political power
- This lead to some middle class people buying titles from the local monarch
- However, there were so many of these people the titles became meaningless
- Many realized that money gave them so much power they didn't need to obey the nobility
- Wanted political protection for their belongings
- Liberalism is effectively the protection of the individual from the power of the state
- Related to free speech, property rights, and the free capitalist market
- Same essential values of modern democratic capitalism, but with more participation in government

Is Democracy in Danger?

- Elections are being used to legitimize authoritarian practices
- Older European democracies are being slowly but surely undermined
- Populism and extremism are being exacerbated by online algorithms and extreme inequality
- Rising malcontent everywhere
- Fascist parties in Europe are banding together despite their policies involving all hating eachother
- Many scholars discuss the death of democracy and how we can save it
- Income inequality and economic inequality is becoming ridiculously extreme
- Difference between the rich and the poor in the US is worse than the differences in ancient Rome which was founded on slave labour
- The difference between a slave and a roman senator is less than the difference between a CEO and a minimum wage worker
- Income of the top levels have tripled during the pandemic
- So is the US still truly a democracy?
- One theory is that the US has become an oligarchy ruled by economic elites
- Martin Gilens and Benjamin Page wrote Testing Theories of American Politics on this topic
- Collected every single policy proposal in the US parliament for 22 years, amounts to more than 1900
- Also recorded all kinds of information of what interest groups are involved and whether it passed or failed
- Found that powerful economic groups had substantial impact on policies while mass-based interest groups and citizens had little to no impact
- Their existence in the US political system had barely any purpose at all
- The US could function the same as it does now with only rich interest groups participating
- This data was only recorded from 1981 to 2002, and is almost certainly worse now
- The study was incredibly popular, and then had no impact on society and quietly fell out of the news cycle
- Very hard to argue that the US is a democracy in the face of this evidence

Competing Theories of Democracy

- Elitist democracy is where the elites run the system instead of the people
- A small group of elites represent the entire population
- Economic democracy argues that you need economic thinking for a democracy
- Also argues that voting can be explained through economic thinking
- This approach has been debunked a billion times
- Participatory Democracy argues that a democracy earns it's value through it's accessibility
- One of the most hopeful views of democracy and looks at the entire society
- Deliberative democracy emphasizes institutionalized debate about topics to ensure that all decisions are given careful consideration
- Cosmopolitan democracy seeks to extend democracy outside the state
- Condemns the undemocratic actions of Canadian companies in foreign governments
