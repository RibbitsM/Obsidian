- The first level is Liberal Democracy
- Free and fair elections, universal suffrage, personal liberties for all
- Canada is a good example of this
- Second level is the Illiberal Democracy
- Unfair elections, state control over media and economy, limited rights
- A good example is Hungary, where the ruling party is so powerful that they restrict many freedoms
- Third level is the Authoritarian state
- No elections, no accountability, centered on a powerful group
- Over a third of the world lives in authoritarian states
- Authoritarianism exists on a spectrum of representation, some states like Iran and China are actually quite representative despite being authoritarian
- Iran has elections and diversity rules in parliament, some areas in China have powerful local meetings
- Fourth level is the Totalitarian state
- Extreme interventionist state, little distinction between private and public life
- No barrier between the state and people's private lives
- Lots of active propaganda, high state control
- Main example is the DPRK
- Libya is the only country in North Africa that was able to supply their entire population with education and fresh water
- This was all under Gaddafi, after the US intervened it became an enormous slave market
- Authoritarian regimes are not always bad, higher level control means more efficiency
- It is important to understand that these definitions are highly western-centric

Role of the State

- 4 major theories of the state
- Pluralism, Elitism, Socialism, and The New Right

Pluralism

- The idea that society is made up of many competing interest groups
- Pluralists say that the government reflects the balance of power between these groups
- Some groups get stronger but none are dominant
- Doesn't matter if this is in a democracy or a authoritarian state

Elitism

- The theory that there is an elite ruling class in society
- Exists in economic, military, administrative, religious, and political aspects
- Connected to corporatism where coordination of state, industry, and trade combine to form society, the state is the brain and the rest of society are body parts
- Small elite controls the rest of society
- Elitism is also connected to dynasties, where many people from the same families remain in power for years
- Richest families in Italy have remained the richest for hundreds of years

Socialism

- Socialism theorizes that the ruling group or elites control the means of production
- Everything revolves around the means of production
- In a capitalist state, the economic elite are always in control
- The state is simply a tool of control used by the elite capitalists
- Conflict between the bourgeoisie and proletariat
- An example is Manchester during the Industrial Revolution
- When factories were first emerging there were no labour laws
- They were able to pay women and children much less
- The police were used as a tool to bring children into the factories and keep the parents from bringing them home
- Argued that only a revolution can bring about real change
- The workers are stuck at the bottom of the power pyramid

The New Right

- Range from free market thinkers to libertarians
- Think that the state oversteps its bounds already
- Should restrict the state more
- The state has no obligation to do things like run free health care and free education
- Bureaucracy expands solely for it's own benefit and is self serving
- Competing for control of the state just creates paralysis

Future of the State

- Is the state still necessary?
- Apple is not a state but outperforms most states financially and power wise
- Apple benefits from the states it occupies and grows even larger
- The state is no longer as economically important as it was
- Countries are willing to minimize taxes just to encourage companies to reside there
- Ireland has an incredibly low corporate tax rate, so many huge tech companies have their headquarters in Ireland
- In this situation Ireland is the weaker partner in this relationship, the company has much more power
- When NAFTA was established, Mexican tomato and avocado producers were vastly overshadowed by Monsanto's incredible production capacity, putting Mexican businesses out of business
- This may have increased GDP of Mexico, but GDP does not indicate inequality
- Mexican citizens lost their jobs and immigrated illegally to the US to work for the companies that put them out of business, taking jobs of American unskilled labourers because they work for less
- State has become weaker in terms of border and territory control
- More and more people are global citizens and have multiple citizenships
- Does the state still matter? Yes, but it's being hollowed out
