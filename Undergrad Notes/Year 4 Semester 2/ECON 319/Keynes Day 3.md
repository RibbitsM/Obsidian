**Chapter 18**

- Argues that the economic system is fairly stable
- While previous thinkers assumed that the economy was always at full capacity, Keynes says that the economy is often operating in suboptimal conditions
- Fluctuations tend towards extreme states but never really reach them and often snap back in the opposite direction, creating business cycles
- One thing that does induce stability is keeping the multiplier above one, but not significantly
- If change in the interest rate does not affect investment, this also creates stability
- These are both observed to be fairly constant features of the economy
- Also, when employment changes, real wages tend to follow the change but slowly and not disproportionately
- Again, this is because of the unions slowing down the process of wage changes
