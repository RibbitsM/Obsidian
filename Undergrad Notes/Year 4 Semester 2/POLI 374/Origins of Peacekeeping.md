- Early missions were defined by four main themes
- The first was improvisation, in the infant years of peacekeeping, the UN had no devoted mechanism for peace operations
- Cold war politics
- Decolonization
- Regional wars

**Greek Civil War**

- One of the first problems faced by this system was the Greek Civil War beginning in 1946
- Conflict between Greek communists and those opposing communist reforms
- Fighting broke out in the form of open armed conflict, with the communist forces aided by communist Albania and Bulgaria
- The UNSC came to an agreement in 1947 to begin an investigation into whether or not there were arms crossing the border from these aiding countries
- The USSR didn't veto this motion because they didn't think their plot would be discovered, but this assumption turned out to be incorrect
- The US proposed the formation of a multi-country observer force to prevent arms transfers, and the USSR immediately vetoed this initiative
- Early example of UNSC internal politics restricting progress on international security measures
- In response, the US took the matter to the General Assembly, and got a resolution authorizing the creation of the UN Special Committee on the Balkans in October of 1947
- Observers were deployed until 1951 with the mandate of observing and reporting arms transfers
- Over time, supplies to Greek communists faltered and the conflict was eventually resolved
- This was never officially a UN peace operation since it was not authorized by the UNSC
- Despite this, the mission created the model that many future observer missions would follow

**Indonesian Independence**

- Elsewhere, the independence of Indonesia post-Japanese colonization was another hot issue
- As the Netherlands tried to assert control over the then Dutch East Indies, armed independence movements emerged to combat Dutch soldiers resulting in mass casualties
- In 1947, the USSR proposed the creation of a commission to create a ceasefire based on the belief that the communist Indonesian independence movement had the upper hand
- The US actually ended up supporting this resolution, because they believed that Western backed Indonesian forces had the upper hand
- But, this resolution ended up being vetoed by France, because they were worried that this would set a precedent for support of independence movements happening in French colonies
- Australia stepped in and proposed a Consular Commission consisting of observer forces from countries which held consuls in Indonesia
- This commission ended up being created in 1947, but was not supported by a UNSC or UNGA resolution and just happened without opposition
- By 1949, the UNSC did mandate the UN Commission of Indonesia in 1949 that legitimized the efforts of the Consular Commission and included some personnel from that commission
- This commission supervised a settlement between the retreating Dutch and the Indonesian independence movement supported by the US and USSR
- In the end, the US was right and the Western faction came out on top
- This example is one of the first examples of the observer method being used for decolonization
- Again, this was never an official UN peacekeeping operation
- Very ad hoc, no one was trying to create larger models and applying solutions case to case
- However, patterns began to emerge with the placement of UN forces on the ground to support the peace process

**Arab-Israeli War**

- In 1949, the UN established the UN Truce Supervision Organization in Israel and Palestine, where it still exists today
- At the time, the area was controlled by British mandate, and while the UNGA attempted to create a partition plan for the region which failed
- In response, the UK just gave up and left, and Israel was immediately attacked by neighbouring Arab states
- The UNTSO was recognized as a UN peace operation, and is often cited as the very first
- Had the mandate of controlling borders, providing communications services, and facilitating safe travel
- Today, UNTSO headquarters are located right outside Jerusalem where observer vehicles still frequently patrol the region
- Mainly deals with the Israel-West Bank border, the Golan Heights have its own mission
- The third operation in the area is UNIFIL, assigned to the Lebanon border
- Agreement on UNTSO was reached based on a peace agreement between the PLO and Israel

**India and Pakistan**

- Nearby, the first of many wars between India and Pakistan over the Jammu-Kashmir region broke out
- The UN helped broker a ceasefire in the war beginning in 1947, and as part of the agreement the UN Military Observer Group in India and Pakistan was established in 1949
- Mainly mandated with maintaining the "Line of Control" between the India and Pakistan-administered regions
- Small mission, only about 44 observers for an 800 km long border
- As a result of the 1971 war in the region, India withdrew its consent from UNMOGIP and as a result it could no longer patrol the Indian side of the border
- To this day, the mission patrols the borders and files reports on any issues that arise

**Observer Mission Model**

- UN observer missions follow the holy trinity, especially the non-use of force (they are unarmed)
- Ralph Johnson Bunche is responsible for most of the development of these principles, and was a member of the US delegations on the UN Charter and the UDHR
- Later became an under-secretary general at the UN
- Observer missions are usually very small-scale and personnel are generally unarmed or armed with only a sidearm
- Their main functions are simply observing and reporting issues to support an existing ceasefire
- Observer missions occupy the low end of the UN peace mission spectrum
- Use very little force, and are deployed to relatively peaceful environments
- This model is still used today, mainly because of how easy it is to accept by host governments
- Also very easy to deploy since they require much less personnel and equipment while still providing a very useful source of information and a communication channel
- However, these missions are still limited since they have no mandate or enforcement capability
- Dependent entirely on the goodwill and consent of host states
- At minimum, a ceasefire is required, but ideally a full peace agreement is in effect
- If there are any spoiler groups in the region at all, they have no way to defend themselves
- Also, the lack of resources limits the ability to support with humanitarian or peacebuilding efforts

**Applying the Observer Model**

- Our case study will be the Iran-Iraq war beginning in 1980 and ending in 1988
- Iraq invaded Iran to protect oil reserves and the spread of the Iranian revolution to Iraq
- Mainly restricted to the border between the two countries
- Heavily entrenched, and occasionally escalated to the use of chemical weapons
- UNSC passed resolution 479 to urge a peaceful resolution, did nothing
- How can you design a conflict management scenario and peace operation to support it?
- First we need a ceasefire, or some sort of peace agreement
- Requires a third party mediator, some sort of leverage or international pressure
- This requires a lot of different things, but any significant action will require a UNSC resolution
- If we're creating a peacekeeping operation, that means a mandate and rules of engagement
- To actually implement this you need finances, contributed troops and personnel, etc.
- Also need a Special Representative, identify the TCCs, and the basic mission structure
- What is the size of the mission? What is it's purpose? Where is it being deployed?
- Main components of your answer should be identifying the politics of the situation and the mission planning process as well as mission structure
- Also may identify risks, challenges, and other possible pitfalls
- In real life, things panned out pretty much how we simulated
- A UNSC resolution established the UN Iran-Iraq Military Observer Group with consent of both
- Classic UN observer mission was deployed to monitor the ceasefire and patrol the border
- This model is still used today, and not just by the UN
- The MFO was deployed to the Sinai as part of an agreement between the US, Israel, and Egypt as a result of an inability to restore the UNEF II mandate
- Patrolled the Israel-Egypt border to ensure withdrawal of forces, no UN involvement
- The MFO is still deployed today, with US 82nd Airborne Division members patrolling wearing badass maroon bucket hats
- Also has a naval component, pretty unusual for observer missions
- Of course this is an armed mission, fairly different
- For a more recent example, the OSCE deployed a mission to Ukraine in 2014
- Job was to deploy along the Luhansk-Donetsk border to observe, and talks are in progress to establish a similar mission in the event a ceasefire is established in Ukraine
