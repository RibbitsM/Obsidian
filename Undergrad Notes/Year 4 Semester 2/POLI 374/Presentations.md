**Somalia (ATMIS)**

- Gained independence in 1960, led by Siad Barre from 1969 to 1991
- After he was removed from power, enormous vacuum opened and power struggle ensued
- In the early 2000s, the Islamic Courts Union became involved and created Al-Shabaab
- AMISOM was deployed in 2007 to curb the influence of Al-Shabaab
- Transitioned into ATMIS in 2022 to focus on government support

Headquartered in Mogadishu, mainly operates in the South as Somaliland and Puntland are mostly fine

- Established in Res 2628, which also set a withdrawal timeline
- Mainly focuses on transitioning security to the state
- Also does counter-terrorism against Al-Shabaab
- Lacks funding because it is AU-lead and isn't part of assessed contributions
- Replaced AMISOM because of constant withdrawal delays
- Most forces come from big African TCCs
- Designed to lay the foundation for AUSSOM which is an upcoming peacebuilding mission
- Mainly struggles from a lack of funding and general instability in the Somalian government

**South Sudan (UNMISS)**

- Under Egypt-British control from 1899 to 1956
- North was generally more invested in compared to the south
- Declared independence in 1955, and civil conflict almost immediately broke out
- In 1972, south was allowed to self-govern but fell apart in 1983, with war lasting until 2005
- Wanted full independence from the North
- After a peace agreement in 2005, South Sudan became independent in 2011
- Leader of the liberation movement became president, and ousted his vice president after coup accusation
- Vice president started movement in exile to fight back
- Dinka support government, Nuer support opposition
- Several mediation efforts have been carried out by the UN, regional orgs, and many third parties
- UNMISS was created the same time as South Sudan, and evolved from UNMIS after Sudan refused consent
- Multidimensional mission, main goal is to build capabilities of South Sudan
- Basically just keep the country stable and help it build
- Mandate has shifted to add more tasks like humanitarian aid, monitoring, etc.
- Also tried to support a new peace process
- When the civil war happened in 2013, Res 2132 reinforced protection of civilian capabilities
- Suffers from instability of course, but also public distrust
- Did not have the troop capacity to fulfill its mandate, and the rules of engagement were inconsistent
- Successes came in the form of protection of civilian sites used to avoid immediate violence, but had limited funding and suffered from sexual violence and crime
