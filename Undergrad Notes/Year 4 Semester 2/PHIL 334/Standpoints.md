**Analysis of Knowledge**

- The traditional definition of knowledge is as a justified true belief
- Ideally, when we are talking to other people we assume that all of us are committed to seeking and creating knowledge
- We assume that everyone has the ability to reason, perceive, etc.
- Depicting a person or a group as irrational, illogical, etc, we are disregarding their epistemic ability
- A common theme in oppression is the denigration of a group's epistemic ability
- Additionally, another common tendency is to attribute the personal failings of a member of an oppressed group to the entire group when this is not done for the dominant group
- Another major category of epistemic injustice is hermeneutical injustice
- This term refers to restrictions on the interpretation of injustice, oppressed groups are often prevented from communicating their experiences of oppression because of gaps in worldview
- Sexual harassment is a common example of this, broad epistemic gaps mean that most of the population did not interpret these issues as significant or harmful
- Two different interpretations, either harmless fun or a genuine concern for workplace life
- Power dynamics mean that the men's interpretation is dominant, and the interpretation advanced by women is largely ignored
- This means that not only can victims of hermeneutical injustices not share their experiences, but it also limits their ability to properly comprehend what is happening to them

**Privilege and Ignorance**

- Since privilege comes with the power to shape knowledge production and epistemic trends, those in a place of privilege generally do not need to engage with knowledge outside their groups
- Their knowledge is normalized within the cultural worldview, and determines how the world works
- If you do not experience these gaps in interpretation, there is no indication that your way of viewing the world is harmful, incomplete, or incorrect in any way

**Controlling Images**

- An "image" is a way of viewing and interpreting the world, and a controlling image is a method of encoding epistemic injustices into an image
- Generally understood as a stereotype of one group that is defined by a more powerful group
- This stereotype will always serve the interests of the dominant group, and will appear to be natural or inevitable to an outside observer
