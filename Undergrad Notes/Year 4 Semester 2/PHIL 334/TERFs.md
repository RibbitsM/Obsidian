**Denials of Authenticity**

**Raymond**

- Pinnacle figure of trans exclusionary feminism of the 70s
- Main argument focuses on the medicalization of trans identities
- A common theme of trans exclusionary arguments is the assumption that being transgender requires demanding surgical or hormonal alterations to the body
- Raymond, and other feminists as well believe that medicine is a tool for perpetuating normative gender, and medical treatment is highly gendered
- The belief is that trans people are actively colluding in this system
- Trans people are mostly understood through self-reporting, and is identified by a feeling that you are "in the wrong body"
- What the "right body" would be is a normatively gendered one
- Raymond is arguing that trans people are equating male and female identities with male and female bodies which is supporting the gender normative system
- This is actively harmful to the goals of feminism
- By attempting to emulate the female body, trans woman are making a caricature of womanhood based on degrading male stereotypes of the female body

- Basically, Raymond is denying the authenticity of trans identities by saying they are ignorant about what it truly means to be a woman

**Responsibility and Blame**

**Stone**

- Stone is pretty much the originator of trans feminism, first person responding to Raymond who was able to get academically recognized
- Stone's point is straightforward, she argues that Raymond fails to consider the subjective experience of trans people and the social dynamic they exist in
- At this time, gender affirming treatment was in its infancy and Raymond focused heavily on this
- Stone agrees with Raymond to an extent, she is correct in the sense that some trans people are associating being a women overwhelmingly with female body parts and stereotypes
- However, this is not a problem with trans ideology, it's a product of the system
- Trans people want gender affirming care, and they will do whatever they can to get it
- In many places, this requires a gender dysphoria diagnosis
- At the time, the definition of gender dysphoria revolved heavily around gender stereotypes and aversion to their genitals
- As a result, many trans people had to "play along" with this definition of dysphoria to get their desired outcomes, and many self-reported falsely as a result
- This ended up with people confusing the obsessions of the medical industry with genitals and gender stereotypes with the views of trans people
- Trans people are commonly seen as challenging the gender binary, we generally assume everyone must be one of two genders, and your gender is fixed
- As a result, anything outside of this system is outside of the norm, and is unnatural or odd
- We do not deny that these things exist, but at the same time we don't accept them as a real part of life
- There must be some sort of perturbation or confusion that is causing this effect
- Stone's argument is that all genders are socially constructed, and there is no natural state
- All people seek "gender affirming" action to try and reinforce their identity, regardless of gender
- What trans people are doing is simply representing bodily and social role configurations that exist outside of the norm, but are equally as valid as the genders of cis people
