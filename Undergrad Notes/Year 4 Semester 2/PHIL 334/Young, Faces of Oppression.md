- Frye's analysis of oppression is universal, and assumes that all forms of oppression share these characteristics
- Instead, Young advances a pluralist model of oppression that appears in multiple different forms

**Exploitation**

- The first face of oppression is exploitation, where the produce of one group is taken to benefit another
- This transfer is the primary characteristic of exploitative systems, and the archetypal example is capitalism, where labourers have a portion of their labour extracted as profit to the capitalist
- A key part of exploitation is not just the material aspects, but the mental and social aspects as well
- Being separated from the value of your labour is an alienating experience, and inherently lowers the self-value and self-respect of the people being exploited
- When you are consistently undervalued by a system, you begin to undervalue yourself
- These ideas also apply to gender exploitation, with reproductive labour being the prime example
- Reproduction is another double bind, if you don't have children you are stigmatized and if you do you are disadvantaged
- For the most part, reproducing as a man is beneficial where it is always a disadvantage otherwise
- Emotional and sexual labour are also seen as being extracted from women without adequate recompensation
- This is where intersectional identities start to come into play, most people are economically exploited, but women workers are doubly exploited

**Marginalization**

- The next face of oppression is marginalization, or exclusion from certain activities
- This can be exclusion from social groups, work, education, representation in media
- Marginalization also breeds dependency, either dependency on others or dependency on the state
- Often, dependency leads to violation of autonomy as assistance generally comes with conditions
- For example, if you're on welfare you're subject to regular checkups from the state and rules for who qualifies for welfare
