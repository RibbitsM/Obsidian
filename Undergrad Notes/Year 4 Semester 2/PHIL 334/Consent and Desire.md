- Sex-positive feminism believes that sex is ethical only if consent is present
- This is basically just the general approach to consent and sex
- Metaphor of a sandwich, noone can take a bite of your sandwich if you don't want them too, but not getting a bite of the sandwich isn't oppression
- Issue comes when we think about the metaphor a little bit more
- If everyone in your school is letting eachother have some of their sandwiches but not with you, maybe there is an issue
- Lets say there's also evidence that this non-sharing is happening because of demographic factors
- Why are some people sharing sandwiches but not others? What are the patterns between those who are sharing and receiving? Under what conditions?
- We should also question who is and is not allowed to consent, who is asking and who is giving, etc.
- These are all questions we should ask ourselves as feminists
- Generally, we think of love and sex as crucial parts of a fulfilling life
- If we are interested in creating a more just and fair world, this is probably something we should consider
- Even if consent is established, ethical sex depends on communication and respect
- Pleasure and desirability are also important concepts to consider

**Sexual Exclusion**

- The term incel is short for involuntary celibate, most are men
- Core concept is that they believe they are owed sex, and that women owe them sex
- Often adopt right-wing ideology, convinced that left-wing cultural movements are to blame for their lack of sex
- Generally, women are objectified as a way to satisfy sexual desires in this process
- Pretty unsympathetic group, are likely excluded from sex due to their sense of entitlement
- Sexual exclusion is not limited to incels, also appears in racism, transphobia, or just exclusionary beauty standards in general
- The problem is that we agree that there is no right to sex, but at the same time love and sex are conditions for minimally decent lives
- However, we may have an obligation to reflect on our desires and consider how this may affect our sexual lives
- Is there any way you can transform your desires to change the way you interact with others
