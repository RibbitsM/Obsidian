- Ptolemy created the first map with both latitude and longitude

Latitude

- Angle of north-south
- A parallel is a line connecting all locations of a given latitude
- Poles are where the earth's axis meets it's surface
- The equator is the largest parallel, and is located at the halfway point between the poles
- The arctic circle is the southernmost latitude where the sun is above the horizon for 24h
- The Antarctic circle is the northernmost latitude where the sun is up 24h
- The tropics are the latitudes where the sun can be directly overhead
- The tropic of cancer is the northernmost point where this is possible, and the tropic of capricorn is the southernmost point where it's possible
- The tilt of the earth causes the seasons, changing how the sun hits the earth

Longitude

- Angle of east-west
- Meridians connect all points of equal longitude
- The prime meridian is chosen arbitrarily, and runs through the royal observatory in Greenwich
- The poles have no longitude value as the meridians originate from them

Shape of the Earth

- All models of the earth have various issues
- Three common models are the sphere, the ellipsoid, and the geoid
- The geoid is an approximation of the physical earth, but with calm oceans
- Used mainly for calculating elevation
- However, it requires lots of careful calculations and measurements so it's usually not worth using
- In that case, we use the ellipsoid or the sphere instead
- The ellipsoid is a mathematical approximation of the earth
- Doesn't have precise elevations but has the general shape
- A sphere is a less accurate approximation, doesn't have elevation or the precise shape of the earth
- Earth spins at a 23.44 degree tilt
