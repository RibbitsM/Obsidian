- On drosophila, the gene for eye color is sex linked
- When you see a gender bias in how a gene presents itself, it's likely sex linked
- Inbreeding creates homozygosity
- A self cross has a 50% chance of creating a homozygous result
- The more rounds of inbreeding there are, the more homozygous genotypes are created
- This applies to all genes in the genome
- Say you have 10 rounds of inbreeding, each individual gene would have a 1023/1024 chance to be homozygous
- This is because a heterozygous gene has a 50% chance to become homozygous every time, but it cannot go back
- (1/2)10 equals 1/1024
- Not all genes have only two alleles
- Most have multiple alleles that influence the expression of the gene
- A common example is ABO blood type which is influence by three alleles
- The three alleles are IA IB and I
- A and B are codominant, meaning they don't overrule each other, but they both overrule the little i
- Blood type A means you have AA or Ai
- Blood type B means you have BB or Bi
- Blood type AB means you have AB or BA
- Blood type O means you have ii
- Each blood type comes with it's own antigens
- A has A antigens, B has B antigens, AB has both, and O has none
- Each antigen differs by only one sugar
- They all have the same base with one minor differentiation
- Your immune system will create antibodies to fight the blood types you DON'T have
- Therefore blood type AB can receive blood from anyone because they have no antibodies
- Blood type O can only receive blood from other O type donors because they have both
- Also, anyone can receive O type blood because it has no antigens
- Blood type also has what's called an Rh factor that can either be + or -
- + is dominant to -
- Same deal with blood type, antibodies are created for the type you don't have
- This can be a problem if a mother with - blood gives birth to a + fetus
- This will give them antibodies for both, causing issues
- The H antigen is the precursor to the A and B antigens
- It provides the base for the ABO blood types to be created
- 1 in a million people lack the genes necessary to modify the H antigen
- Therefore even if you have the A or B alleles, your blood type will be O because you can't create the A and B antigens
- Your phenotype doesn't match your genotype
- This is called the Bombay (or Mumbai) phenotype
- This is an example of something called a gene interaction
- The gene interaction modifies the ratio of blood types
- Another allele (the h allele) is working behind the scene
