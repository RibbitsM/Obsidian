- Mutation are inheritable since they affect your DNA
- Mutation doesn't occur at a constant rate
- People have hypothesized that environment has a significant effect of rate of mutation
- When the environment changes, mutations tend to happen faster
- Different kinds of radiation or chemicals induce different kinds of mutations
- Certain chemicals cause frame shifts, translocation, deletion etc.
- Nondisjunction can occur in mitosis, but won't affect your children
- Drugs like colchicine can be used to force ploidy issues, both aneuploidy and polyploidy
- Women are born with all of their eggs, so chance of aneuploidy increases with age
- It increases exponentially, raises fairly frequently at age 40
- Anaphase 2 only happen AFTER an egg has been fertilized
- When chromosomes pair up in meiosis, in cases of trisomy one chromosome won't have a pair
- You either get a gamete with two chromosomes or a gamete with one
- This is why triploids are infertile, because pairing can't occur evenly
- If there is an imbalance, it won't work
- Say we have three chromosomes, A, a1, and a2
- No matter how pairing happens, we'll have one chromosome at one pole and two chromosomes at the other pole
- The probability of creating a normal haploid gamete is (1/2)n-1
- N equals the number of chromosomes in a species, the one half represents the likelihood that the single chromosome makes it into the gamete
- This makes creating a normal haploid gamete very unlikely
- First chromosome sets the pattern that other chromosomes follow
