Sex Chromosomes

- Males are said to be hemizygous, meaning they have only one X chromosome
- Therefore men will express any recessive gene on the X chromosome
- Inheritance can be either autosomal or sex linked, and dominant or recessive
- Sex linked means X-linked
- The Y chromosome barely carries any genes relating to phenotypes
- We don't consider Y linked traits in this course
- The propositus is an arrow pointing to an affected individual in a pedigree
- They're the originator of the gene in question
- Generally in the case that two unaffected parents have an affected son, it's likely a sex linked recessive gene
- This is because we always assume the solution that requires the least genes
- Sex linked requires only one allele, autosomal requires two
