Gregor Mendel

- Austrian monk, bred pea plants
- Changed only one variable per sample set
- Large sample sizes, multiple repeats
- Proposed Mendel's Laws which are still used today
- Peas were used because they have easily recognizable traits that express only two phenotypes
- He could also control how they mate by transferring pollen with a brush
- Continuous means that genes are expressed on a spectrum, usually in the shape of a bell curve
- Discontinuous means the gene is expressed in one of several choices, with no in-between
- Mendel was breeding groups of pure bred plants together
- The off spring would always show the dominant phenotype, but when they self fertilized some offspring would be recessive
- TT x tt = Tt Tt x Tt = TT Tt Tt tt
- Usually F2 had a 3:1 ratio, except for situations with incomplete dominance where a 1:2:1 ratio emerges
- Can use a test cross to determine if the genotype is homozygous or heterozygous
- Cross with the homozygous recessive and observe the ratio of the offspring
