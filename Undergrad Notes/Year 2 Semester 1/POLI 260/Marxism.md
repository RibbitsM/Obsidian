Crisis of Relevance

- For all of recent human history, states purported to be communist have failed
- This was due to the state's authoritarian characteristics
- Look no further than the Berlin Wall which existed to prevent it's citizens from escaping
- The fall of the Berlin Wall signified the end of communism's influence on the western world
- So what shall Marxists do today as they struggle to convince people of the relevance of their ideology?
- A degree of separation of the application of Marxism and the theory of Marxism is necessary to talk about it's relevance
- Think of Marx as an idealist whose ideas were twisted in application

Origins

- Apart from Marx and Engels, Lenin and Trotsky also played large parts in the origins of Marxism
- The core of the argument is a criticism of capitalism
- This is why it is called a critical theory, it directly opposes embedded liberalism and liberal capitalism
- Also provides criticism of realism
- Marxism is a materialist theory, meaning it looks at history through the lens of economic development, Marxists believe that all social change was driven by economics
- This runs contrary to the common assumptions that economic forces are only one of a cast of larger factors in history

Principles

- History is marked by shifts in economic development
- We went from hunter-gatherers to farmers, to feudal states, to slave societies, to capitalist societies, colonialism, advanced capitalism, etc.
- For Marx, this shows an endless inward spiral of constantly advancing capitalism towards a mysterious endpoint where everything breaks
- At some point, the contradictions of capitalism will break it and a huge social revolution will usher in a permanent era of utopian communism
- However, as history has shown, capitalism just keeps evolving and evolving with no end in sight
- The future and past of society is determined by economics
- Communism is considered the highest and final form of society
- For Marxists, the key actors are not states, but classes
- According to Marx there are only two classes
- The Bourgeoisie, who own the capital, and the Proletariat, who sell their labour to the upper class
- The state doesn't matter, institutions don't matter, borders are irrelevant
- The state only exists as a form of control used by the Bourgeoisie
- The purpose of Marxism is the emancipation of the Proletariat and the overthrow of the Bourgeoisie
- The ideal future is the destruction of class as a concept
- If class struggle is the source of all problems in history, a classless world is a utopia

Lenin

- The issue is, where is the revolution? Why isn't everyone overthrowing the upper class
- WW1 was considered the turning point in history, the moment where the revolution finally erupted
- Hypothetically, the communist revolution was supposed to happen in Germany, which was the most advanced capitalist state in the world
- Except it didn't and it happened in Russia
- Now the process began of trying to plug the holes of Marxist theory to make it functional
- Lenin attempted to solve the problem of how to spread Marxism worldwide
- Marx assumed that revolution would just happen domestically simultaneously everywhere
- So Lenin came up with the theory of Imperialism

Lenin's Theory of Imperialism

- Lenin stated that capitalist systems will inevitably become monopolistic
- In order to continue growing, these states must look abroad for new markets, labour and resources
- So naturally, all capitalist states become imperialistic
- As these states attempt to conquer the whole planet, they will come into conflict with eachother
- War breeds revolution
- According to this theory, that war would have been WW1
- But he still believed that global war between capitalist states would usher in communism
- After a while he came up with a new theory to hurry up the process of communist revolution

The Vanguard Party

- Because of the strength of control systems in capitalist states, the Proletariat is no longer capable of freeing themselves
- Small appeasements to the working class preventing the revolution held them back (trade unions, councils, democracy etc.)
- What Marxism really needs is an elite group of enlightened Marxists to lead and usher in the future
- The idea is, this elite group would lead the masses in the revolution, and ease in the working class until eventually becoming part of the working class
- Obviously the enormous problem of this theory is the fact that it's just creating a new elite class
- However, it gave hope to many Marxists worldwide that they could be part of this Vanguard

Marxism, Power, and the World System

- Power is held exclusively by the economic elites
- The world is defined by dependence and exploitation, there is not interdependence, everything is another system of control
- The world is inherently exploitative and unequal
- War exists only as an instrument of the ruling class
- States only go to war for the benefit of the economic elites
- The only solution to these problems is to overthrow capitalism
- There are two main categories of Marxist IR theory
- World System Theory/Dependency Theory (Andre Gunder Frank and Henrique Fernando Cardoso) and Gramscianism/Hegemony Theory (Antonio Gramsci and Robert Cox)
- Marxists see the world as a collection of a few rich countries, with the rest on the periphery
- However, the privileged and wealthy cooperate across state borders to protect their interests
- This cabal of global elites has just as much importance and power as any state
- Looking at the liberal vs realist view of the world compared to how Marxists see the world, it's drastically different
- Hegemony theory looks at how the culture of the ruling class is passed on from generation to generation
- Elites must be able to replace themselves while controlling the proletariat
- Civil society is just another exercise of power, including churches, media, government, education
- Universities and schools just exist for social control and create new elites

Marxism and Modern Global Politics

- Some argue that despite it's history, Marxism has never been more relevant than it is today
- Countless global wealth reports continue to show the enormous gap between the poor and the rich of the world
- This creates the bizarre paradox of modern society
- 80% of the people in the world today live better than any king in human history
- However, the bottom 20% is the problem, why haven't their lives improved?
- Marxists look at this growing inequality and points to those 20% as the casualties of capitalism
- In a world where economic inequality is enormous and still growing, Marxism is relevant
- The climate crisis is a product of capitalism, subsidizing MNCs is wrong, resource wars are inevitable
- These are just some views Marxists have on modern issues among many others
