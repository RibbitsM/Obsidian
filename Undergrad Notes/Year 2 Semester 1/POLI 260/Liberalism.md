Origins

- Anchored in liberal thought
- Political and economic liberty of the individual
- Belief in rule of law, democracy, and constraints on state power
- Based on the writings of Kant, Locke, Mill, and Bentham
- Keynes described the problems of mankind as an inability to combine economic efficiency, social justice, and individual liberty

Principles

- Liberals believe in the importance of values, morality and laws
- These principles should take precedence over the pursuit of power
- Also believe in an international society where states follow norms and laws
- Progress over continuity, human society can learn to avoid past mistakes, there is a possibility for widespread cooperation
- Nothing is inevitable, and nothing is unavoidable
- Prosperous societies are peaceful, and peaceful societies are prosperous
- These two factors reinforce eachother
- Principles, Progress, and Peace are the three main pillars of political thought
- Instead of the globe being a system of big and small circles, the world of global politics is more of an interconnected web
- Embedded liberalism is the idea that today, all interactions and organizations are built on the principles of liberal thought
- This is not limited to government interactions, it's in every aspect of life
- Liberals believe in the importance of many actors in global politics
- Believes that non-state actors can be just as important as states
- These can include NGOs, corporations, global institutions, and ethnic/religious/racial groups
- Liberals also believe that states are not unitary actors
- Meaning that division within a state's government and population has significance
- Also argue that the state is in decline, it is being challenged by the forces of globalization
- States have less and less control over their borders as time goes on
- International institutions have power and significance
- Even though states may join international institutions for personal reasons, they are still subjecting themselves for the laws of these organizations
- Liberals also talk about international regimes, which are essentially sets of norms, rules and expectations that countries gather around
- Mechanisms for cooperation, can be very large scale or very small scale
- Some examples are the International Agreement on the Maintenance of Certain Lights in the Red Sea, International Tropical Timber Organization, International Seed Testing Association, and the Organization for the Prohibition of Chemical Weapons
- As you can see, there are regimes based around cooperation on any number of things
- The world of global politics is not anarchic, it is interdependent

Interdependence

- All free trade organizations are about the same thing
- Free movement of goods, capital, services, and people
- The European Unions names these as the four most important freedoms to uphold
- Not all free trade agreements have all four
- For example the trade agreement between Canada, the US and Mexico only upholds the first three
- Despite constant economic crashes, world trade is still trending upwards

Liberalism and Power

- Liberals believe in the importance of soft power
- Soft power is the ability to attract, persuade and lead without coercion
- Argue that the value of hard power is shrinking, and the value of soft power is growing
- People following you because they want to is more useful than people following you because they have to
- Power can include cultural impact, power of ideas, media etc.
- War is not inevitable, it is the product of faulty cogs in the international machine
- Bad leaders, autocratic governments, mistakes, and faulty international structures
- In order to create a world free of war, it must be built

The Liberal Peace

- This is a concept that details the elements required for world peace
- There must be economic interdependence, trade promotes trust and makes war more costly
- There must be democracy, there is a theory that democratic states never fight
- It seems silly, but there has not been war between two "truly" democratic states ever
- Democratic states will treat other democratic states with more respect than normal
- However, there really isn't that many democratic states to fight eachother, but still this hypothesis has never been disproven
- International law and multilateral institutions are also required for liberal peace
- Finally, arms control and disarmament are very useful as it deincentivizes war and makes it less costly
- However, Liberals still believe that war is still sometimes necessary
- They do this through the principles of collective security
- If a state invades another, in a system of collective security all members of the system will come to the aid of the invaded state
- In the case of the Russian invasion of Ukraine, neither of the two countries were involved in a collective security system
- In reality, a system like this is not as consistent as it seems on paper
- But should a security system intervene in the case of a humanitarian crisis?
- In response to the Rwandan Genocide, the UN Security Council enacted a doctrine that allows the international system to intervene in cases of gross human rights violations
- Of course, this is also used as an excuse to intervene in cases where it's not necessarily needed, but it is strategically advantageous
- These interventions are essentially just liberal wars

Liberalism Today

- Liberals focus on building the system of liberal peace
- Spread democracy, build international regimes, encourage trade, build civil society
- Global issues require cooperation
