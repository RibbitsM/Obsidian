- IPE (International Political Economy) is a combination of politics and economics
- Combines who gets what, when, and how those things are produced, consumed, and distributed
- It examines the ways political entities shape economic systems and how they are shaped by those systems and interactions
- The politics and economics of IPE cannot be separated, they are essential to eachother
- The European Coal and Steel Community was created in 1951 (France, Belgium, Italy, Germany, Netherlands)
- By coordinating economic policy, management and trade of certain goods could be eased
- Political goal was to bring West Germany into the western economic sphere, goal was to protect it from the ambitions of the USSR
- Also attempted to ease French and German tensions over Alsace-Lorraine
- Economic growth shapes how countries see eachother, China would not be viewed as such a threat if not for their exponential economic growth
- There is current debate over whether economics drives politics or vice versa
- Economists and political scientists each argue for their respective discipline
- Generally accepted that either is true depending on context
- IPE also examines the relationship between the state and the market
- States and markets can clash, they are very different units
- States have preferences and goals, while the market only seeks maximization
- Global market is larger than it has ever been, and involves more and more flows over borders
- More and more often national economies are being influenced by the international market, not the other way around
- Two main strategies for economic crises, austerity and stimulus
- Austerity seeks to reduce spending, eliminate deficits, reduce national debt, reduce taxes (burden on economy), deregulate the market
- Stimulus runs short term deficits to equalize, pay back revenue later with higher taxes, and increases employment to raise disposable income
- For a long time austerity was the primary method of market management because it limits inflation
- However, austerity puts more burden on workers than stimulus, which focuses on market growth
- Stimulus is the new primary method mainly due to the pandemic, but inflation is getting out of hand
- High government stimulus, high food prices and high energy costs due to the invasion of Ukraine are all driving up inflation
- Disagreement on economic policy creates tensions in domestic politics
- IPE also examines the financial relations between states (and non-state actors)
- This is not limited to just trade, international finance is also important
- Many concerns have been raised over the instability of the global financial system, there have been multiple economic crises that tanked the international economy in the past few decades
- In addition, the effectiveness of International Financial Institutions (IFIs) like the World Bank or the IMF are often debated
- IPE is also interested in globalization
- Many people think that globalization is inevitable or natural, it is not
- Globalization was conceived and constructed by people, and it can also be dismantled
- It was attempted once before by British scholars, but was cut short by the outbreak of WWI
- Many people around the world today are opposed to globalization, the opening of economic borders have done irreparable damage to local communities
- This has had great effects on the domestic politics, influencing anti-globalization movements worldwide
- This started on the left, concerns about global exploitation and equality prompted protests like the one in Seattle, or the Occupy movement
- Recently, anti-globalization has manifested as right-wing protectionism, advocating for nationalism and economic protectionism, like Donald Trump and Jair Bolsonaro
- These movements and leaders are directly connected to the conditions of globalizations
- While many of this phenomena is considered matters of domestic politics, it is very much influenced by global politics
- The COVID-19 pandemic raised many new questions like how to revive economies in the short and long term
- Will the pandemic damage global financial transactions, free trade, and globalization?
- How will supply chains change in response?
- On top of this, the Russia-Ukraine war exacerbated most of these issues and added new ones such as energy scarcity in Europe

World Bank Prospects

- World growth is projected to slow to 2.9% from 5.7% in 2021
- Emerging countries will experience the harshest results
- 75 million more people than expected will be in extreme poverty
- Combination of post covid inflation and economic stagnation could be devastating
- We still have the US dollar as a valuable central currency that everyone trusts
- Central banks in most countries have reliable histories of managing inflation, assuming domestic politics doesn't interfere
- The economics are sound, but only assuming the politics permits it to function

The Beginning of Globalization

- The liberal international economic order was created intentionally in the aftermath of WWII
- LIEO had two main goals, increase economic growth, and prevent economic depressions
- The example of Weimar Germany is used as a cautionary tale for economists
- When economies are affected by ailments, they will turn to protectionism, which causes rampant inflation and encourages political extremism
- This is the formula that led an economic depression to the rise of Hitler
- Therefore the LIEO was created to ensure this never happened again by encouraging trade, not just between states but between individuals, firms, and households too
- Exchange is unrestricted and mutually beneficial
- States are free to act in rational self-interest
- This included the notable exception of colonies, and the USSR and all their allies
- The economic system was built by West Europe and the Americas, for West Europe and the Americas

Principles

- Based on Adam Smith's theory of Absolute Advantage
- If left alone, states would specialize to a niche in the global economy by producing the goods they could create most efficiently, and trade for those they could not acquire easily
- Of course, absolute advantage is rare in reality, most states are better at producing all goods than countries with less industrialization
- Instead we use comparative advantage, where countries simply produce what they are best at producing, even though other countries are better at it
- Not absolute efficiency, but comparative efficiency
- Without economic trade countries are unable to take advantage of economies of scale
- Prices are higher, and industries are prone to monopolies without competition
- With trade, we have increased competition and interdependency between countries
- Global resources are used more efficiently, overall economic output is higher, and interdependency encourages peace between countries

Problems of Trade

- Trade disrupts domestic economies and workforces
- Specialization eliminates whole domestic industries in the name of long term growth
- Benefits of this system are not evenly distributed, some aspects of the economy are affected more than others
- While all states should benefit, some benefit much more than others
- Barriers to trade also exist, not all places have the same free movement of goods, production, and labour
- In order to maintain this system, global financial institutions and governance are needed
