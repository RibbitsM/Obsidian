- Global politics is the study of the interactions between states
- States are the primary unit of politics, so IR focuses on interactions between them
- In recent history, there has been a rise in the importance of non-state actors like multinational corporations, alliances of countries, and NGOs
- IR also focuses on social patterns like the distribution of wealth, goods, and ideas between the states of the world
- Culture, norms, laws, institutions are all spread on a global scale

**Definition: The study of the interaction between states, non-state actors, and social/physical structures**

- IR emerged from the study of political science, but over time has evolved into more of a interdisciplinary subject
- Despite this, IR is still rooted in the study of power, meaning political science
- It is an interdisciplinary study of power on a global scale

Levels of Analysis

- Agency can be divided into three levels
- Individuals, States/Groups, and Systems
- Individuals are defined as people who drive action or wield some sort of power, like CEOs, heads of state, public figures
- Can also analyze groups, and how the movements of these groups or states influence the world
- Focus less on the leadership of the states, and more on the population as a whole
- Alternatively, there is the argument that all of these things happen only because the systems in play permit them
- It is the form of the system that determines how these actors perform
- When asking why something happened, you look at it through each of these three levels, and determine which one had the most impact
