- In the recent years there has been an explosion in the number of theoretical frameworks for IR
- Postmodernism, Constructivism, Postcolonial theory, Race and IR, and Ethics and IR are the main theories that came out of this expansion

Postmodernism

- This movement emerged out of denial of positivism
- Positivism is the idea that objective truth and facts exist, and it is possible to determine these facts through science
- Neutral facts are a separate entity from values and beliefs, and they are discovered via the scientific method
- Postmodernists declare this to be an illusion, how we view the world is subjective
- Everything we know is a product of our social selves, our society, our upbringing
- It's impossible for the observer to be impartial, nothing can be truly determined
- People simply see the world as they wish to see it
- Horkheimer said that we must perceive things both through the character of the object and the character of the observer
- Alongside Jurgen Habermas and Theodor Adorno, Max Horkheimer pioneered the postmodernist approach
- Foucault and Derrida also had significance influence
- The discovery of truth is impossible, it's simply the reflection of the preconceptions of the scholars seeking it

Principles

- Everything is social, not material
- The world is socially constructed, as are all of our belief systems
- In order to determine if something is true, you need several people to agree that it is true
- Therefore truth is socially constructed and subjective
- The goal of a framework is not to create objective knowledge about the world, that's impossible
- The concept of truth in societies has changed over time
- Once, it was considered an objective truth that the sun revolved around the earth
- If truth is subject to change, can it really be called truth?
- What we call truth is the product of a dominant discourse, a hegemonic belief system that determines what is true
- Power creates knowledge, those in power determine what is true and false
- The purpose of postmodernism is to expose these biases and so called truths of global politics
- In the case of the Rwandan genocide, ideas played a huge role
- The socially constructed idea of scientific racism, brought by the Belgians, pervaded Rwanda
- The society was divided into Hutus and Tutsis based on nothing more than cranial measurements
- Created one of the worst genocides in recent history

Constructivism

- Constructivists borrow heavily from postmodernism, but recognize a portion of the material world
- Mix of postmodernism and positivism
- They claim that global politics is a social and historical construct
- There is no given, natural elements to global politics, they are all socially constructed
- There is no such thing as anarchy or interdependence etc.
- Structures are primarily made by shared ideas, not material reality

Principles

- Global politics is driven by the beliefs and norms of the actors
- There is a combination of embedded beliefs and the material world that creates action
- The world is not primarily material, nor entirely socially constructed
- For example, when countries are determining which countries are a threat, there are multiple factors
- On one hand, the material world matters because a country with more weapons is therefore more dangerous
- But we also need to consider the social implications of alliances, history, leaders, etc.
- North Korea is much weaker militarily than the UK but we consider it to be much more dangerous because of the embedded beliefs at play
- States are just as vulnerable to the forces of socialization as any other actor
- Think of how our conception of sovereignty has changed in the age of globalization
- The UN is willing to violate the sovereignty of countries where they deem human rights violations to be occurring
- The definitions and labels we use change too, Europe was not always considered some sort of homogenous unit, the West included Japan at one point
- Whether a country is perceived as a threat or not depends greatly on people's perceptions
- By changing the perception, you change the world
- Identities create how they think, how you think defines your action, your actions determine identity
- If there is no definitive infinite truth of global politics, then it can become anything
- In anarchy, there is no force determining that states must behave selfishly, they have choice
- Anarchy is not an objective truth, states have a choice to behave however they want
- States act the way they do because of their embedded beliefs, not because of the structure of global politics

Power

- Status and reputation is very important for constructivists
- Norms define how actors behave, and they are built via social movements, international organizations, etc.
- You can change how actors behave by constructing new norms

Postcolonial IR Theory

- Examines the influence of colonialism and racism on the landscape of global politics
- Challenges the long held domination of European ideas in IR
- Proposes the idea that IR needs to move away from Eurocentric ideals to be truly global
- Purpose is more to expose the Eurocentric aspects rather than overturn them
- Also looks at theories emerging from oft neglected cultures
- Emerged from reaction and rebellion against colonial rule
- Dubois, Roxanne Doty, Sankaran Krishna, and Edward Said are some of the most important researchers

Principles

- People in colonized countries experience global politics much differently than those in powerful countries
- There is a hierarchy of global politics where colonized states are still oppressed
- Think of the phrases Third World, developing or underdeveloped countries
- Racism shapes history: politics, media, education, everything carries it
- What we think of as our objective historical narratives are products of colonialism
- Our current economic and political structures have their origins in the exploitation of colonized countries and use of people and resources
- Imperial occupations are always accompanied by violence, displacement and genocide

Power

- Postcolonialists believe in military and economic power, but also control over social narratives
- The West has long held themselves in superior regard to the "rest" of the world
- Power is wielded in terms of cultural genocide and domination
- Decolonization of basically everything is required
- Laws, institutions, terminology, education, history etc.
