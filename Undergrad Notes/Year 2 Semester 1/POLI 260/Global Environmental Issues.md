- Environmental issues are debatably the largest issue of the 21st century
- Multidisciplinary issue, requires a holistic understanding of the science behind the causes of climate change and the social and political consequences of climate change
- Result of increased awareness of the relationship between human society and the environment
- Examines global governance, science, non-state actors, structural inequality, capitalism, and sustainability
- This is an issue that entirely ignores political borders, making it a truly transnational issue
- Science has given us the information required to recognize the issue, we just need to act on it
- Awareness of environmental issues is much more significant than most paint it, awareness and understanding of an issue is a key part of solving it
- Silent Spring by Rachel Carson is a fundamental text of environmentalism published in 1962
- Its release triggered a huge backlash from pesticide and chemical companies, further proving the truth of her arguments
- First major international environmental conference was held in Stockholm in 1972
- Led to the formation of the UN environmental division
- This was followed by several high-profile environmental disasters, including Chernobyl, acid rain, and depletion of the ozone layer
- The Brundtland Commission released in 1987, setting the foundation for multilateral cooperation on environmental issues
- It called for decisive political action on environmental issues and was based on scientific predictions of issues that are just now coming to pass
- The 1992 UN Conference on the Environment and Development or Earth Summit was held in Rio
- Another book released, Collapse by Jared Diamond, argued that many great civilizations failed because of their inability to handle environmental issues

Responses

- The global response to environmental issues took many forms
- International treaties and regimes have been formed to varying effect
- Many NGOs have been created for environmental purposes
- Increased research, many grants were issued towards green issues
- The global environmental/green movement grew slowly but consistently, and is now enormously influential
- Some actors reacted with denial, especially those who would lose the most from green policies
- Many scientific warnings have been concocted and spread, countless assessment reports and environmental outlooks predicted accelerating rates, scales, and scopes of danger
- The Millennium Ecosystem Assessment looked at the consequences of resource exploitation and the resulting failures of development goals
- Drivers of climate change continued to grow to unprecedented heights, consequences became more dire
- The IPCC 2018 report posited that the Earth has entered a new geological epoch as a result of human activities
- The overwhelming opinion is that business as usual is no longer a viable option

Environment and Human Society

- The relationship between human and earth systems has been researched heavily over the past decades
- The ecological footprint was a concept developed at UBC in 2008 that assesses the biocapacity of earth's systems as a function of square kilometers
- The question is, how much land does a person need to sustain their lifestyle
- The result was the discovery that humanity currently required 1.5 earths to sustain their lifestyles
- This is projected to double by 2050, assuming 2008 rates of consumption
- The good news is that we have comprehensive knowledge of what to do and how to do it
- The bad news is that we need to do a lot of things, and what we need to do is unacceptable to many people
- Some of the major drivers of climate change are population (assuming current consumption), industrialization, urbanization, economic growth, consumption, and energy demand
- The majority of global consumption is attributed to a small percentage of the population, those people that live in rich countries
- Of course, rises in any one of these drivers can be mitigated with environmental policy, with the possible exception of consumption
- Reduced consumption offsets population, green technology offsets industrialization, eco-density offsets urbanization, environmental firms offsets economic growth, and sustainable energy offsets energy demand
- The two main atmospheric stresses are air pollution and ozone degradation
- The concentration of particulates in the air have become so prominent that brown clouds are visible over polluted areas
- Land stresses mainly focus on human land use including farming, logging, and habitat destruction
- The nitrogen cycle has been altered by human activity, with unsustainable amounts of nitrogen ending up in soil
- Oceans have become acidified and polluted, coral reefs are being destroyed and fisheries are being depleted
- A good example of this is the presence of plastics in the ocean
- This leads to animal mortality, entanglement and ecosystem damage
- Microplastics are one of the newest contaminants to be discovered
- The global supply of freshwater is dwindling, and drinking water and safe sanitation is scarce in many areas
- Water scarcity is a growing issue worldwide, affecting 2.67 billion people per year
- We are in a great extinction period of global history, massive amounts of species are becoming endangered and extinct
- Ecosystems are constantly facing multiple pressures from human activity
- Fragmentation of habitats is a new concept and focuses on the distribution of deforestation instead of a surface area measure
- Fish stocks are being depleted rapidly by destructive and unselective fishing practices
- Human actions are undermining the Earth's biosphere, compromising the Earth's ability to support humanities continued wellbeing
