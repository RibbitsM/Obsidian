- The world can be divided into wet and dry bands
- At the equator, the air is very humid and warm, this is where most jungles are
- Countries at the equator have monsoons, very green etc.
- We call this region the tropics, it has low pressure and easterlies
- Above this we have the subtropics, where all the deserts of the world are
- This is where the dry air falls from the tropical Hadley cell
- These are usually high pressure areas, very dry and warm
- Then there are the mid latitudes
- Here, cold polar air collides with the warmer subtropical air to make polar fronts, called a Ferrel cell
- In the mid latitudes we have many stratus clouds, which are formed by the collision of two high altitude air masses
- Warm air rises above advancing cold fronts and creates a thick layer of cloud
- Defined by storms, low pressure, and wet weather
- The highest latitudes are the driest of all, even more than the subtropics
- The cold weather sucks all the moisture out of the air, making the arctic and Antarctic deserts

With Continents

- In the northern mid latitudes, the winter causes high pressure to form over the continents
- This is because the ocean is warmer than the land
- Land and water have different heat capacities, it takes much more energy to heat water than stone
- Also, water is much better at transferring heat downwards through convection while land can only do this through diffusion
- In the southern mid latitudes, there are few landmasses so the ocean is mainly low pressure
- In the summer, there is low pressure over the land and high pressure over the ocean
- This creates the monsoon, when large amounts of moist air flows in from the ocean and rises over the land to create huge storms
- This phenomenon is strongest in southeast Asia because of the Himalayas, which helps the air rise to an altitude where monsoons are created
- When looking at the globe, the northern hemisphere has much more land than the southern hemisphere
- Therefore it heats and cools much more quickly than the south
- Because of this asymmetry, the ITCZ, the band of clouds, isn't actually at the geographic equator
- It's at the thermal equator, which is in the northern hemisphere in June and July, and in the southern hemisphere in December and January
- Wherever it is summer, the ITCZ will follow

Atmospheric Circulation

- The purpose of air circulation is to try and distribute heat as evenly as possible
- Because the earth is hotter near the equator than the poles, the earth tries to move heat towards the poles
- At the equator, more energy comes in than goes out, and the opposite is true near the poles
- This shows that atmospheric circulation is effective
- Near the poles, storms make the air warmer, while they make the air colder near the equator
