What happens to energy after it leaves the Sun?

- Energy radiates in all directions
- Spreads out into a larger area, becomes less effective
- Solar constant: total amount of energy emitted by the sun divided by the distance between earth and the sun
- Solar constant is 1370 W/m squared (So)
- The farther away a planet is from the sun, the less energy it receives
- Size of the planet doesn't matter, it is a measure of energy per square meter
- Also, the Earth is an orb, not a disc so you must modify the solar constant
- The actual amount of energy is 342 W/m squared accounting for the shape of the earth (Se)
- Say a planet is twice as far from the sun as another planet
- The further planet would be receiving a quarter of the energy the closer one is receiving despite being twice as far (inverse square law)

Absorbing Energy

- A blackbody is a surface that absorbs all wavelengths, this is most solids
- A selective absorber absorbs some wavelengths but transmits others, letting them through uninterrupted, most gasses are selective absorbers
- Some surfaces reflect wavelengths as well
- Air scatters blue light, making the skies blue
- Water absorbs red light and lets blue through
- The deeper you go into water, the more red is absorbed, making large bodies of water appear blue
- Radiation can be absorbed, transmitted, or reflected
- But in all cases it causes emission in the form of heat
- Emission and reflection are different concepts
- Emission changes the nature of the energy, reflection doesn't
- The average albedo of the Earth is 0.3 or 30%, meaning 30% of the radiation Earth receives is reflected
- Aerosols and air pollution actually increase the albedo by making more cloud cover, fixing air pollution will warm the planet
- The Earth emits 240W per meter squared to account for the incoming radiation
- By these calculations, the earth's equilibrium temperature is -18 Celsius
- The greenhouse effect traps some of this emitted radiation, warming the Earth
- Example questions could be predicting how radiation is emitted, reflected, or absorbed by different planets and stars
