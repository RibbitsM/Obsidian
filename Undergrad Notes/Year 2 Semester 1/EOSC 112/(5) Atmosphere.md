- The tropopause is the boundary between the troposphere (where 90% of the mass is contained)
- This is why anvil clouds have their shape, the flat top of the cloud coincides with the tropopause
- The height of the tropopause is different depending on the latitude
- The tropopause is higher at the equator than at the poles
- The temperature causes the air to expand, increasing the height of the troposphere
- The height of a vertical isobar, or pressure level, is a representation of the mean temperature of the air, we call this the geopotential height
- Because the tropopause is higher at the equator, the pressure is higher there compared to the poles
- Air flows from the high pressure at the equator to the low pressure at the poles
- The geopotential height is fairly equal above each of the three cells, the decrease goes between the cells
- The transition from one cell to another creates a jet stream
- These jet streams are caused by a sudden pressure difference when the geopotential height decreases between cells
- The Coriolis effect turns this poleward wind into a westerly geostrophic force in both the northern and southern hemisphere
- Jet streams are always westerly, and there are four in total
- Wind gets stronger the higher up in the troposphere you go, jet streams are the strongest
- Wind is also stronger in the winter hemisphere, the reduced temperature exacerbates pressure differences
- In Vancouver, the sub-polar jet stream is north of us during the summer, meaning we get dry summers
- In the fall it comes back down and gives us a wet fall, winter, and spring
- The position of the jet stream is tied to the temperature of the ocean
- Wherever you see a sharp gradient in ocean temperature, it's a sign of the jet stream
- The lower temperature corresponds to a fall in the geopotential height
- Jet streams move with the seasons, by shifting towards the summer hemisphere

Climate and Weather

- The main distinguishing factor between weather and climate is time
- Weather is the moment to moment description of atmospheric conditions, while climate is the accumulation of weather conditions and patterns over a long period of time
- Weather is the product of interactions between one of several large air masses
- These air masses have similar qualities such as density, temperature and humidity
- When they meet, it creates large pressure differentials and high winds
- These interactions are often unpredictable, and create instabilities

Geopotential Maps

- The map we usually use is the 500 mb map, where all areas shown are at equal pressure
- The 500 mb map is roughly the middle of the troposphere, while the 250 mb map is closer to the tropopause
- The lines show the different altitudes of where 500 mb is in different places
- A higher altitude means higher pressure and higher temperature
- Because of the Coriolis effect, wind flows parallel to the lines on the map, with the higher pressure to the right (in the northern hemisphere)
- The closer together the lines are, the stronger the wind will be
- This shows a steeper gradient, and a stronger PGF
