- Earth is made up of several layers
- When we group earth's layers by composition, we call the first layer the crust, and the next two the mantle
- This is because the crust is made of rock, and the mantle is more iron rich
- Another way of grouping the layers is by their mechanical properties
- The lithosphere is the first two layers, and the asthenosphere is in the third layer
- This is because the lithosphere breaks under stress, while the asthenosphere bends
- Between 100-350 km below the earth's surface, the earth is partially melted
- This is because above this area the temperature is too low, and below this point the pressure is too high
- The lithosphere is a set of rigid plates that move on top of a semi liquid or plastic layer, the asthenosphere
- This is the basis for plate tectonics

Plate Tectonics

- Plate tectonics originated with Wegener's theory of continental drift
- This was the idea that the continents were not fixed in space and moved over time
- Most oceans have ridges in the middle of them, and trenches near the continental boundaries
- Seafloor spreading was proposed as a mechanism for continental drift
- At the seafloor ridges, molten rock from the mantle was extruded from the crust and pushed away the crust around it, moving the continents
- This was later more convincingly proved by measuring the magnetism of the ocean crust
- Around the ocean ridges, the magnetic stripes were symmetrical
- Occasionally, the earth's magnetic poles switch, and this leaves an imprint on the rock's magnetic field
- As lava cools, it is magnetized to the direction of earth's magnetic field at the time it cooled
- Since the earth changes magnetic poles, rock that cooled at different times has different magnetism
- Current, the North pole is the negative, and the south pole is positive
- We know that about one million years ago, the poles were reversed
