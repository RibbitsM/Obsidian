Solar Radiation on the Surface

- Each wavelength is absorbed by the Earth differently
- For example, UV radiation is usually absorbed by the ozone layer
- Meanwhile, most visible light reaches the surface

Radiation Emitted by the Earth

- Remember that the Earth is much colder compared to the sun and emits in the infrared, not the visible spectrum
- When looking at the Plank curve for the earth there are many gaps caused by greenhouse gasses
- Greenhouse gasses absorb and emit radiation in the same infrared band as the earth
- It transmits solar radiation, but absorbs infrared radiation
- This is not reflection, as it re-emits in the infrared range but slightly differently from the earth
- When a gas absorbs a photon, it begins to vibrate or bend
- In order to effectively absorb infrared radiation, a gas must be able to stretch and bend asymmetrically
- CO2 and H20 can do this, but N2 and O2 cannot
- Different greenhouse gasses absorb at different specific wavelengths
- If a gas has multiple ways of stretching/bending it can absorb several different wavelengths
- By looking at what radiation a gas absorbs, you can identify it
- Water vapor is the most powerful greenhouse gas but it is a natural part of the climate cycle
- The amount of water vapor in the atmosphere is controlled by the climate, not forced into the system like CO2
- However, water vapor creates a feedback loop where the warmer the planet gets, the more water evaporates and warms the planet more
- The 220K mark is the temperature that the atmosphere emits at
