- Sun moves in an 11 year cycle
- Every 11 years its magnetic field flips completely, due to the movement of charged gasses
- This affects the presence of sunspots on the sun
- Sunspots appear as dark spots on the surface and have a lower temperature, but higher luminosity
- More sunspots means more solar irradiance
- The size of this variation is relatively small, less than 2 w/m squared
- Although attempts have been made to link the two, it seems that sunspots have not been the main cause of global warming
- However, periods of time with few sunspots have been observed to be noticeably cooler, called a "little ice age"
- Solar constant isn't actually constant, it has increased by about 30% since its formation
- This means that an early earth had a much stronger greenhouse effect in order to maintain its temperature

Volcanoes

- In addition to volcanoes emitting CO2, larger eruptions also emit SO2 into the upper atmosphere
- These particles have a long lifetime and create clouds in the upper atmosphere, increasing earths albedo
- In the short term volcanoes cause cooling, but warm the earth in the long term
- On longer timescales, volcanoes have little more effect on climate change than solar cycles

Albedo

- Earths albedo depends on four main factors
- Cloud cover, cryosphere, precipitation/land cover, and tectonics
- These can either create a forcing effect where change comes from outside the system, or a feedback effect where the change occurs inside the system and creates a looping effect
- Higher rates of sea floor spreading mean broad ridges, low rates mean thin ridges
- Broader ocean ridges displace more water and raise sea level
- Sea floor spreading results in water covering more surface area, reducing albedo
- Continent position also affects albedo
- Deserts and ice caps have high albedos, and rainforests have low albedos
- Large continents create deserts, small continents create rainforests
- Land masses at the poles are required for ice sheets to form, so albedo will be lower if there are no polar land masses
- However, even if there are continents at the poles this does not guarantee that there will be an ice sheet
- Tectonics is the main forcing effect, but there are feedback effects too
- Clouds are the main contributor to albedo and are created via a feedback effect
- Clouds radiate energy both towards and away from the earth, so the net effect depends on cloud type
- Low clouds have high albedo and low greenhouse contribution, high clouds have low albedo and high contribution
