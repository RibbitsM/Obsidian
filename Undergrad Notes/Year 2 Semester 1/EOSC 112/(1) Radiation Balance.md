How much radiation does the Sun emit?

- Electromagnetic radiation is made up of a combination of electric and magnetic fields, and travels at the speed of light
- Because EM radiation has a constant speed it's differentiated by the wavelength of the radiation or the distance between adjacent crests
- This also determines the frequency
- (speed of light) c is equal to wavelength x frequency
- Short wavelength means high frequency, long wavelength means low frequency
- The energy of an EM wave is calculated by dividing a constant variable of the plank constant times the speed of light divided by the EM wavelength
- The longer the wavelength, the less energy it has and vice versa
- For example, gamma rays have some of the shortest wavelengths and highest energy
- Radio waves are the opposite, and visible light is in the middle
- In terms of blackbody radiation, the temperature of an object determines the amount and type of radiation it emits
- Hot objects can emit visible light, for example orange and yellow flames or blue stove burners
- The "cooler" the light, the higher the temperature, red is the coolest
- This is called Wien's Law
- Temperature is found by dividing Wien's constant by the peak wavelength
- We also use Boltzmann's law to find the energy flux of the sun
- Boltzmann's equation states that the energy flux of an object is equal to a constant times the temperature of the object to the 4th power
- In conclusion, you use the electromagnetic spectrum to determine the wavelength maximum, use that to determine the temperature with Wien's Law, use the temperature to determine the energy flux with Boltzmann's Law, and multiply that by the radius of the sun to find the total energy emitted

Changes in Solar Radiation

- This calculation may not always be accurate, solar radiation changes depending on the solar cycle, the age of the sun, etc.
- Sunspots are areas of intense solar radiation that appear and disappear
