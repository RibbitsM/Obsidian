**Discourse Before Reagan**

- Prior to JFK, the phrase "terrorism" and "terrorists" was infrequently used
- Emerged first to refer to Communist aggression, and was also used to refer to the Nazis during WWII
- Exists mainly to discredit and delegitimize efforts made by enemies
- LBJ also used the term to refer to the Vietcong during the Vietnam war
- At this point the Islamic world was pretty much absent from terrorism discourse, it was focused on the Communists
- In the Reagan era, terrorist acts committed even by Palestinian militants were often linked to Communism
- The attacks on Israeli athletes at the Munich Olympics were also described as terrorism
- Even domestic forces, like students protesting the Vietnam War and other Nixon policies were labelled as terrorists for acting against the government
- Although regimes changed from Democrat to Republican frequently, the discourse on terrorism remained the same throughout
- During the Carter administration, the label of terrorism was especially applied to the Iranian Hostage Crisis

**Nicaragua**

- Reagan was advised that the future of warfare was terrorist attacks such as hijackings and car bombings, but he was focused on the restriction of communism
- Attempted to link the Soviets to terrorism in South America
- Nicaragua was controlled by the Sandinistas, who had links with Cuba and the Soviets
- Nicaragua was labelled as a communist state and a terrorist state by the US
- Justifies military and economic action against the state of Nicaragua
- Placed Nicaragua in a group of "global terrorist states" including Cuba, Libya, Iran, the PLO, and North Korea, preclude to the later "Axis of Evil"
- All of these states are accused of "sponsoring terrorism"
- The US funded, trained, and armed the "Contras", who were groups of right wing radicals in Nicaragua who sought to overthrow the government
- Later on, the CIAs involvement with the Contras was revealed, leading to a huge PR disaster

**Counter-Terrorism in the Middle East**

- The Iranian Revolution occurred in 1979, overthrowing American puppet govt
- Prior to the Lebanese Civil War, the term "terrorist" wasn't really used in the Middle East
- Terrorist attacks were carried out on US embassies and training camps in Lebanon, killing many
- Reagan also attempted to provoke Libya into a war due to their Soviet ties

**Terrorism Under Reagan**

- George Shultz was responsible for handling many terrorism related incidents due to his role as Reagan's Secretary of State
- The Johnathan Institute held a conference to determine the definition of terrorism
- Shultz believed in pre-emptive attacks, the govt reserves the right to attack potential terrorists
- The public does not need to understand all the reasons, or debate on potential actions
- The conference emphasized the war on terror as a civilizational struggle, especially in terms of Israel vs Palestine
- This conference and the Israeli perspective as a whole greatly influenced Reagans policies

**The Fall of Communism**

- After the Berlin Wall fell, the threat of communist terror was greatly diminished
- The US was actively engaged in finding a new enemy to fight in order to keep the military industrial complex churning
- Huntington was working with the CIA at this point, influenced by Clash of Civilizations
- The next wars would be between cultural enemies, not political enemies
- Islam became the next opponent for America

**Afghanistan**

- The Reagan administration were exploring possibilities to get the Soviets out of Afghanistan, and decided on Operation Cyclone
- There were several options for groups they could have funded in Afghanistan, but to appease Pakistan they sided with the Mujahedeen
- Pakistan was used as the middleman for many of the Mujahedeen, and they profited greatly by keeping a lot of funding and weapons for themselves
- Labelled as "freedom fighters"
- Volunteers in Afghanistan were not directly funded by the CIA, but they definitely benefitted from the process

**Changes in Terrorism Discourse**

- Terrorism was now labelled as a kind of warfare, and was combatted by the military
- Reagan also introduced the concept of state sponsored terrorism, laid the groundwork for Bush
- Hypocrisy, when other states fund armed groups its state sponsored terrorism but when the US does it they are freedom fighters
- Terrorism was depicted as the enemy itself, not just a tool
- The GWOT became framed as more of a civilizational conflict

**Clinton**

- The two main methods Clinton used to respond to terrorist attacks were to either empower the police, or empower the military
- Pioneered the term "homegrown terrorism"
- The 1993 WTC bombing occurred in Clintons first term, a serious attack that was blamed on Islamists
- Instead of declaring war, Clinton responded by empowering the police and tightening domestic security, effectively defining it as a crime
- A few years later the Khobar Towers were bombed in Saudi Arabia, killing US soldiers
- The Saudis were presumably targeted for their close relations to the US
- Clinton used both crime and war metaphors in response
- As opposed to later definitions of terrorism, this attack did not target any civilians but was still labelled as terrorism
- Emphasized the global scope and reach of terrorism, it can strike anywhere
- The sarin gas attack on a Japanese subway created whole new elements in the rhetoric, including the focus on the danger of WMDs
- Bush later adopted this rhetoric and preached the danger of widespread WMDs being passed around through underground organizations
- Rising public fear of WMDs, especially chemical weapons
- At this point, Iran and Iraq were both labelled as terrorist states, with Iraq being labelled as such in the wake of the Gulf War
- The 2003 invasion of Iraq had been brewing for many years, ever since 1991
- There was also a focus on democracy promotion as a countermeasure against terrorism
- The Oklahoma City bombing was quickly blamed on Islamic groups, but was discovered to be the fault of white nationalists
- Handed over to the police, not depicted as an act of war
- This attack also spread the idea of homegrown terrorism
- During his first term, anti-terror legislation was expanded greatly, including discussions of disregarding civil liberty
- In Clinton's second term, the war metaphor was used more and more frequently
- 6 months after Bin Laden declared a fatwa, the US embassies in Kenya and Tanzania were attacked simultaneously
- Politics of fear were leveraged more, the enemy was depicted as inhuman, terror is the enemy of all humanity, Muslims hate us because we love freedom and rights
- The war metaphor became stronger, diplomacy and police responses were framed as insufficient
- Bombings were carried out in Afghanistan and Sudan, not harming al-Qaeda at all and only helped the rhetoric that the Americans were the enemy
- At this time, Bill Clinton was also going through the Lewinsky scandal
- The focus was on non-state terrorism here instead of the state sponsored variety
- Links between terrorism and Islamic culture and religion
