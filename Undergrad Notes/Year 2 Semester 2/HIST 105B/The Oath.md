**Abu Jandal**

- Bodyguard to Osama Bin Laden, now taxi driver
- Sees Osama as a father figure
- Introduced Hamdan to jihad and blames himself for his fate
- He took the oath, but advised Hamdan not to
- Older, has a family and so does Hamdan
- Believes the new generation of al-Qaeda has become misled
- Too aggressive, his moderate ideas made him a target
- Lies about his identity when driving to avoid being killed
- Real name is Nasser Al-Bahri, name changed to Abu Hamza and again later to be unique, means One of Death
- Trained in Bosnia to be a jihadi
- Volunteered to join al-Qaeda and swore an oath, wasn't invited
- Effectively broke oath by leaving to be a taxi driver
- Regrets leaving, still remembers being a jihadi fondly
- The threat of America is too great in his mind to do anything else
- Conceives of al-Qaeda as being above politics, to enter politics is to give up
- Violence will give better results than a peaceful approach
- Sought to fight Americans man to man on the battlefield, not through terror attacks
- Imprisoned when the war in Afghanistan began
- Also prepared new recruits to join al-Qaeda
- Knew all of the September 11 attackers personally
- Against shipping off people to join jihad indiscriminately, but believes those prepared should go
- Doesn't encourage people to join jihad, but tells stories of al-Qaeda and regrets leaving at least slightly
- Went through a re-education program in Yemen called the Dialogue
- The Dialogue was mostly created to protect Yemen, but seems to have been effective
- Teaches mainly through religion, and gives reformed students jobs, how Jandal got the taxi
- Stated under interrogation that he left al-Qaeda due to them associating with Taliban
- After discovering that al-Qaeda carried out 9/11, he gave up tons of actionable information
- Revealed Salim Hamdan's name to the US government
- After going through The Dialogue, began to believe more in the power of pens vs swords

**Salim Hamdan**

- Driver for Osama Bin Laden
- Condemned for association to Osama
- First man captured after 9/11 related to Osama
- Restricted within Osama's organization, was not involved with weapons or stockpiles
- Defense team won in Supreme Court, US actions ruled as against Geneva Convention
- In response, Congress created new laws to try him under, creating the new charge of material support of terrorism
- Married to the sister of Abu Jandal's wife
- Captured while trying to take his family to Pakistan
- Wife barred from the trial because her husband is accused of terrorism
- Found not guilty of conspiracy to commit murder, and guilty of 5 of 8 counts of material support of terrorism
- Sentenced to time served plus 5 months
- Returned to his family, but much more introverted and reduced after his imprisonment
