**Survey Study**

- 80% of the world learned about the attacks within 2 hours of the first plane hitting
- What attacks get shown on TV and what attacks are swept under the rug?
- Out of a poll of 37 countries, all but three preferred to extradite the terrorists rather than intervene militarily
- Military intervention would avoid civilian targets, but this means little
- Those three were India, Israel, and the US, with the US having the smallest majority at 54%
- India, Israel, and basically every NATO country except Israel were willing to send forces
- Most countries were against military action
- There was much sympathy for the US position in North America and Western Europe, but things got a lot more dicey in the Global South in terms of alliances
- India and Israel are the immediate outliers, but the reasons why become clear later on
- These polls were very short and very broad
- Tons of nuance behind questions like what military action is acceptable, and the difference between provision of aid and direct involvement
- Of course grouping by countries causes problems, since people in the same country would often have different opinions
- The difference between not understanding why Al-Qaeda did it and understanding but still supporting the US is huge, but isn't captured by this survey
- More attacks were launched post-9/11 which further influenced these opinions
- Surveys taken at different times would get drastically different results

**Government Reactions in the Middle East**

- Reactions were unevenly represented

- Candlelight vigils were held for the victims of the attacks in several countries
- None of this was televised
- The media focused on the anti-American population that were overjoyed by the attack
- Mubarak comes into power in Egypt and criticizes US and Israeli foreign policy, blaming the attacks on the constant US support of Israel
- Of course, Egypt is equally responsible for supporting the Israelis and also receives US aid
- Incredibly hypocritical, especially looking back since Egypt benefitted the most from the GWOT

- Links attacks to local "terrorism" in Syria, meaning his local opponents
- Offers support conditionally if they address the injustices they perpetuate, incredibly hypocritical

- Of course they weren't in any danger, Saudi oil is too valuable to America
- The Iranian government was in a tough spot, since their government is split between authoritarian and democratic influence
- The moderate democratic leader Khatami comes out and condemns the attacks and offers to help the US
- However, he is shut down by Khamenei, the Grand Ayatollah who rejects any relations with the US
- Behind the scenes, they're looking for ways to benefit from the GWOT despite publically being opposed to it
- Iran had a ton of vested interest in Afghanistan and other targets of the US
- Iraq was in shambles since Saddam realized that this was the US neocon's chance to finally shut him down after the Gulf War
- In order to protect himself from the impending US invasion he tries to rally support from neighboring countries, who mostly reject him since he was widely hated
- Government positions were mostly mixed with the exception of Iraq and Israel

**People's Reactions in the Middle East**

- Mixed reactions across the board, within groups and individuals as well
- Yes, the US had done horrific things to the region but the terror of 9/11 was also so great
- Many people sympathized for the victims, and the American public as a whole
- Significantly, there were candlelight vigils in Iran, a prominent opponent of the US
- There was also hatred towards the attackers, but for many reasons
- Long historical memory of US interventions, and interventions from other countries as well
- A song was written to answer Bush's question of "why do they hate us?"
- In many countries there was no unified response
- Ethnographic studies were done to better understand the responses in detail
- People also distinguished between the US people and the government
- Fear was rampant, many countries feared to consequences of 9/11 for the Middle East
- As time went on and the US invaded the Middle East, people began to resent them more
