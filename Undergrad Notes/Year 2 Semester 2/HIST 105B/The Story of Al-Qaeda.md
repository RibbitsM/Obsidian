- Origins start in the Afghan War when the US armed mujahideen fighters to combat the Soviets
- At the same time, the Saudis and the Egyptians were also sending fighters to Afghanistan
- Basically funded domestic jihadi groups to leave Saudi Arabia and Egypt
- Osama Bin Laden's family came from Yemen, moved to Saudi Arabia very poor and eventually built a huge construction empire
- Very wealthy, heir to his father's empire, raised in the Saudi Arabia education system which was highly critical of Shi'a Muslims, Christians, and Jewish people
- Supported the Afghanistan jihad, met several fighters on the Afghan border
- Inspired by their work, he collected immense amounts of funds from his family and other rich Saudis to deliver to jihad fighters
- Eventually, he decides to go to Afghanistan himself, bringing money and construction equipment
- Stays in Afghanistan and establishes houses for Arab fighters, not mujahideen
- In 1984 the Jihad Services Office is established to recruit more fighters, basically propaganda
- Soon, Bin Laden begins to be seen as a hero after success in battle fuelled by propaganda
- First major disagreement with Abdullah Yusuf Azzam, wants to build more Arab only camps
- In 1986 Egypt begins to establish its influence in Egypt
- Ayman al-Zawahiri wants to gain forces in Afghanistan and train to go back to Egypt and overthrow the government

**Formation of al-Qaeda**

- Formally created in 1988
- Split off from Azzam, first position is in opposition to the Jihad Services Office
- Azzam believed in pacifism among Muslims, believed the enemy to be Israel, the Soviets, and the US
- Bin Laden's goal was Yemen, he wanted to return to his family's homeland and free it from communism
- Al-Zawahiri doesn't care about any foreign invaders, they want to overthrow the Egyptian government, only group that enacts violence against Muslims
- Azzam is mysteriously assassinated, and Bin Laden benefits greatly from the power vacuum
- Currently Bin Laden doesn't care about the US, and has no desire to fight them
- A key aspect of al-Qaeda was that their members were expected to be career jihadists, there is not just one fight, this is your life now
- Their main recruitment base was among disaffected young men in Saudi Arabia
- At this point al-Qaeda is not really a terrorist group, Bin Laden has strict oppositions to hurting civilians
- Eventually Bin Laden goes back to Saudi Arabia and continues to attract attention from the public and anger from the Saudi royal family
- He offers to lead a jihad in Yemen, but is rejected by the royal family
- In 1990 a treaty temporarily ceases the war in Yemen, foiling Bin Laden's plans
- In 1991, the Gulf War happens and Kuwait allows the Americans to build military bases on Saudi land in exchange for peace
- This angers Bin Laden, and eventually starts to push him towards al-Zawahiri's idea that maybe Muslim governments can be the enemy too
- He then heads back to Pakistan, and begins fighting with other major jihad groups
- The next step is the first international jihad mission, assassinating the former Afghan king in Italy
- It fails, and al-Qaeda is kicked out of Pakistan
- Bin Laden has been banned from Pakistan, and he can't go back to Saudi Arabia because the royal family will trap him there
- Instead, AQ moves to Sudan with Bin Laden selling almost everything he owns to fund the group
- At this point pretty much everything is in Sudan, there are almost no AQ fighters in Pakistan and Afghanistan
- Al-Qaeda begins bleeding members, when seeing the state of the group recruits jump ship and join other jihad groups
- However, it remains afloat by Bin Laden's relationship with the Sudanese government which he was funding with his immense fortune
- At this point Bin Laden actually collaborates with Hezbollah, a Shi'a Muslim organization that jihad groups would usually never tolerate
- In the wake of the Gulf War, America begins to emerge as the number one enemy, with Saudi Arabia being the second target
- AQ claims responsibility for the downing of a Blackhawk helicopter in Somalia
- Not responsible for initial bombings in Saudi Arabia but plan attacks in Saudi Arabia but don't carry them out for fears of civilian casualties
- Bin Laden's rhetoric has basically completely changed in terms of who his targets are

**Al-Qaeda, Al-Jihad, and the Islamic Group**

- At this point both al-Qaeda and al-Jihad are operating in Sudan
- Al-Jihad was basically internationally rejected due to their attempted assassination of Hosni Mubarak who was the leader of Egypt at the time
- Two young boys from al-Jihad are captured by Egypt and tortured until they reveal the assassination plot
- They were then released, and eventually caught and executed by al-Jihad
- This angered the Sudanese government greatly, and al-Jihad is basically disbanded
- With the remnants of his organization, al-Zawahiri launches an attack on an Egyptian embassy
- Al-Zawahiri attempts to make the argument that no group is innocent, anyone can be a target
- Suicide is explicitly prohibited for Muslims, but al-Zawahiri tries to draw a line between suicide and martyrdom, making suicide righteous
- Bin Laden doesn't agree with this at the time, but eventually will
- At this time the US gets Sudan to kick Bin Laden out of the country
- Instead of arresting him, they don't really see him as a threat and hope he'll just get picked up and executed by the Saudis
- Sudan also robs Bin Laden, seizing basically all of his assets leaving him destitute
- Al-Qaeda is broken and disbanded, and Bin Laden is exiled to Afghanistan
- At this point Afghanistan is in complete chaos with no government except for warlords, but soon the Taliban will rise to power, supported by Pakistan and the Saudis
- They accept him into Afghanistan, but only because they hope he'll bring money

**The Resurgence of Al-Qaeda**

- At this point Bin Laden kicks the propaganda machine into high gear
- His new home in Afghanistan has a cave, that he uses as an important symbol
- Mohammed first encounters Gabriel in the Quran, uses it as part of an analogy to compare himself to the prophet Mohammed
- Begins preaching via cassette tapes and international media to spread the word
- In 1996 he records the "Declaration of War Against the Americans Occupying the Land of the Two Holy Places"
- At this time he is incapable of carrying out any real attacks, but reporters are interested and keep interviewing him and giving him a platform
- At this point the Taliban still thinks he has money, and doesn't kick him out despite Saudi pleas
- As Osama Bin Laden becomes more popular, Pakistan decides to actually fund him
- The idea is that when he establishes training camps in Afghanistan, they can send Pakistanis to get trained for free and then send them to Kashmir
- Bin Laden demands that America remove their troops in Saudi, atone for their attacks on Iraq, and to cease supporting Israel
- Every time Bin Laden speaks up, it's a huge media spectacle made up to look as impressive as possible and they know exactly what they're doing
- At this point al-Zawahiri is now a member of al-Qaeda, with his own organization disintegrated
- Finally, the United States begins to recognize him as a threat, but the Taliban refuse to hand him over, even outright rejecting Saudi Arabia despite them funding the Taliban
- The Taliban is basically playing both sides, getting assistance from both al-Qaeda and Saudi Arabia

**Run-up to 9/11**

- Al-Qaeda is back, they have military bases, training camps, funding and personnel
- They bomb US embassies in Africa, carried out to create spectacle and increase attention
- At this point al-Qaeda has embraced al-Zawahiri's philosophy fully, killing Muslims, civilians, and using suicide bombers
- The greater Muslim community was horrified by these attacks
- Attempts to draw the US into a war in the Middle East, hoping they would win on their own turf
- Starts talking about a huge attack within the US coming that they can't stop
- Operation Infinite Reach fails, kills more civilians than combatants and fails to actually kill any al-Qaeda leadership
- Upon initially hearing about the idea for the 9/11 attacks, Bin Laden doesn't believe its possible
- Al-Qaeda also attacks a US warship at this time, hoping for a counterattack that never came due to US domestic politics
- One initial idea was that they would kill all the men on some planes but let the women and children go to hopefully get good press, but Bin Laden rejected the idea
- Planned as an attack on American values
- The CIA actually unearths evidence on what's going to happen, but doesn't act
