**Colonial Feminism**

- Started as a way to justify colonial occupation of several territories
- Represents Islam as an oppressive and inferior religion, not as advanced as Christianity or Judaism
- In terms of feminism, focuses on Muslim men's perceived mistreatment of Muslim women
- Feminism is used as a tool in service of colonialism
- Justifies oppression, feigned concern to justify intervention
- In Muslim communities there was a special focus on veiling practices, symbol of supposed oppression
- Language of emancipation, save the women to save the society
- White men saving brown women from brown men
- Lord Cromer was the ruler of Egypt for several years, British ruler
- Believed Islam was an inferior religion and believed in the need to "civilize" Egypt
- Saw the oppression of women in Egypt as inherent to the culture and a product of child rearing practices
- Ironically, he actually damaged women's education with changes to the education system
- He also banned Egyptian women from becoming doctors and was against women's suffrage in the metropole
