**Iran-Contra Affair**

- After being blocked from giving aid to the Contras by local groups in the US government, Reagan needed another way to continue the war
- The US and Britain collaborated to purchase arms from Iran and funnel them to Nicaragua
- Biggest CIA operation in Latin America since the Bay of Pigs invasion
- Funding armed groups called "contras" in Nicaragua to overthrow the left-leaning Sandinista government
- Illegal to fund counter-revolutionary groups like this and were told to pay reparations
- Contra attacks were incredibly costly to Nicaraguan infrastructure, and caused many casualties
- Economic damage from the war was estimated at 4 billion dollars when the Sandinistas disbanded in 1990
- The US was also looking at other countries in the region like El Salvador
- Crusade-like task for Reagan, was very committed to opposing communism in South America by any means possible
- Reagan accused guerillas in El Salvador of being funded by the Soviet Union and Cuba
- At this point being accused of being communist was essentially a signal to do anything to stop it
- El Salvador became the leading recipient of US military aid and training
- Contras were mainly being trained in Honduras, which was the launching point for most of US operations in South America

**Reagan**

- Used very simple rhetoric to depict the war, called the Soviet Union the "evil empire"
- Reagan was concerned about nuclear missiles, and wanted a way to discourage the use of nuclear missiles, and appealed for a project to intercept missiles
- He did not believe in mutually assured destruction or deterrence, and sought to find an alternative
- Ironically if this technology did exist, it would have imbalanced deterrence, almost assuring that nuclear war would occur
- This was dubbed the Strategic Defense Initiative or "Star Wars" and was basically envisioned as a space laser to shoot down nukes mid-air
- Depicted the Cold War as a simple good vs evil struggle, justifying arms buildup in moral terms
- Around this time, Gorbachev was recruited into the Kremlin and assigned to reform agriculture
- Fears of nuclear war was at a high, reviving anti-nuclear protests in Europe
- Many protested the project for being too expensive and unrealistic, requiring immense cuts to welfare programs to fund it
- Margaret Thatcher was also critical of Star Wars, seeing it as a waste of money
- Reagan left office triumphant after overseeing the fall of the Soviet Union, despite his many controversies and errors

**Cold War**

- Cold War tensions heightened when the Soviets shot down a Korean airliner
- Pierre Trudeau defended the Soviets, believing it to be an accident instead of an intentional act
- Trudeau had previous experience with communism, travelling with his brother behind the Iron Curtain where he became more sympathetic towards the USSR
- In October of 1983, the Americans invaded Grenada after the prime minister and several other officials had been killed by local groups
- Maurice Bishop, the prime minister was a personal friend of Castro and a staunch anti-imperialist who had many grievances towards the US
- This invasion was especially contentious since the US did not warn Britain of the attack, despite Grenada being technically British territory and Reagan and Thatcher's relationship
- Criticized by many for being illegal and unnecessary
- Received with mixed opinions from the public, some welcomed them and others were upset
- The Sandinistas were appalled, and held large scale protests in response
- Nicaragua was struggling economically with its oil profits cut off by US trade embargo
- Reagan made a famous speech in Berlin, demanding that the Soviets take down the Berlin Wall if they truly believe in peace

**Soviet Union**

- At the same time, the USSR was facing the mujahideen in Afghanistan and were suffering greatly
- In an attempt to break the downward spiral of the Soviet Union, Gorbachev implemented reforms to loosen the "iron fist" of the state
- Perestroika encouraged economic reform, and glasnost advocated for transparency in the govt and media
- As the Soviet Union became more and more approachable, the prospect of peace became likely
- The question was whether Reagan would accept it or not
- Eventually, the Soviets gave up on Afghanistan and were looking to pull their troops out and simply provide aid to the government similar to Vietnam
- Although he wanted to loosen Soviet policy, Gorbachev was still adamant on supporting Nicaragua against the Contras
- Thatcher had met with Gorbachev and approved of him, saying he was reasonable and encouraged Reagan to speak with him
- Reagan and Gorbachev met in November of 1985, where Gorbachev realized that the US wanted them in Afghanistan, prompting him to look to pull out
- Gorbachev and Reagan eventually agreed to work towards the elimination of nuclear weapons in Europe, a huge step forward in arms control
- SDI was huge point of contention between the two, Reagan was confident in its potential and Gorbachev wanted it gone
- Afghanistan was basically bleeding money from the Soviet government, it had weakened the country significantly
- However, they were concerned with hurting their image and losing their allies by signifying they would no longer support foreign leftist movements
- The next summit was held in Iceland, agreed in principle to eliminate ballistic missiles and other nuclear delivery systems
- Britain was concerned that Europe would be left without their current American missile systems, and they would be unable to fight the Soviets without them
- When Gorbachev and Reagan met again in 1987, he indicated that the Soviets would finally withdraw
- In 1989 Hungarians removed the barbed wire between Hungary and Austria and many feared the repercussions, but the Soviet Union did nothing
- This was followed by Poland, where local groups also rebelled mostly unopposed
- All across Eastern Europe, the grip of the Soviet Union had loosened significantly
- In Berlin, the barriers of the Berlin War were lifted, and people were allowed to move freely between East and West Berlin
- Almost immediately people began to demolish the wall, climbing on it and chipping away at the wall for souvenirs
- Gorbachev never wanted to destroy the Soviet Union, he just wanted to make it more efficient by loosening state control
