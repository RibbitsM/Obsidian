**Helsinki**

- The Helsinki Conference was a collection of delegates from 35 nations discussing security issues, political issues, and human rights abuses
- It was widely criticized since the discussions were so contentious few solutions were created
- It led to a human rights watchdog organization called Helsinki Watch suggested by Henry Kissinger
- Used by Soviet dissidents to undermine communist support in Poland and Czechoslovakia
- No longer really in effect, but has evolved into Human Rights Watch with it's headquarters in New York City and monitors human rights abuses worldwide
- Works mainly on the behalf of refugees, political prisoners and displaced children, worked to ban landmines and cluster munitions
- Funded landmine cleanup projects and disarmament projects
- A new pressing human rights issue was nuclear proliferation and their consequences
- Discussions were unproductive in decelerating the arms race, with the US and USSR disagreeing on what should be done
- Constant pressure between need for safety and need for a quick launch led to the US security council forcing launch sites to implement launch codes
- However, the launch sites just set the code to be all zeroes for ease of use
- The US launch system is surprisingly uncomplicated, its been optimized to the point that most US missiles can be launched within 2 minutes of a message being sent

**Iran**

- 1963, 10 years after the overthrow of Mossadegh by the CIA, Khomeini sent the Shah a message
- Insulted the US and the Shah, lambasting him for being nothing but a puppet for the Americans and having no integrity, only motivated by greed
- Seen as a lack of loyalty to Iran and lack of faith for Islam
- However, Jimmy Carter still decided to visit Iran in 1977 to praise the Shah for making Iran "stable"
- Ironically, this led to riots in Tehran after seeing the Shah publically associating with Carter
- Many middle eastern countries saw religious rule as the best choice to avoid being a puppet of the Soviets or the Americans
- By January of 1979, the protests were so widespread that the Shah was forced to leave the country, never to return
- In response, Khomeini returned to Iran victorious, and led the revolution as the Imam
- Iran became more and more anti-American, speaking out against colonialism and the imposition of American economic values on other countries
- Although they were anti American, they also opposed the Soviets, preferring to rule through Islamic fundamentalism
- This did come at the cost of laws tightening on women, many women refused to wear the veil after many years of secular rule
- Although the Shah had been corrupt, under his rule women were allowed to go to school, work in government offices, and exist in public with men
- After the Iranian revolution, all of these laws were repealed including lowering the marriage age to 9 years
- Women were protesting these policies en masse, but unlike the previous revolution this was not widely adopted
- On November 4, 1979 a group of student militants stormed the US embassy in Tehran and held 66 Americans hostage
- The hostage crisis lasted over a year, with many people being held hostage for over a year
- All women and African-Americans were released weeks after the storming of the embassy, only American white males were held hostage
- 6 of the hostages were able to escape to the home of the Canadian ambassador in Iran
- By the 1970s the Cold War is still going on, but the rise of Islamic fundamentalism was becoming a more pressing topic

**Afghanistan**

- This is what happened in Afghanistan, the countries government was being controlled by the Soviets
- Left wing military coup sympathetic to the Soviets was supported by Russian advisors
- Perfect target for communism due to the agricultural character of society
- Land reforms were enacted, giving land to the former peasants while secular Soviet values banned veils
- Seen as a threat to customs of Islam and was widely opposed by local religious leaders
- The Politburo encouraged Afghanistan to slow down and make the reforms more slowly, but they refused and continued the rapid change
- Jihad was announced against the Soviet leadership among local groups and also groups in bordering Iran and Pakistan
- In response, the US, Pakistan, and Saudi Arabia funded local opposition groups to resist Soviet encroachment
- Actually, they were being armed by the CIA for 6 months prior to the invasion, since Carter wanted to prompt a Soviet invasion of Afghanistan
- The goal was to deliver the Soviet Union a blow similar to what the Vietnam War did to the US
- The formation of the Taliban was seen as a small price to pay compared to dealing a huge blow to the USSR
- Closely guarded secret, the public did not learn of the aid until much later, the official story was that the US armed Afghanistan after the fact
- Beginning of the blowback effect
- Soviets invaded Afghanistan in December of 1979 with fighting lasting 10 years and leaving millions of land mines in the area in the aftermath
- Led to immense loss of life on both sides, was extremely costly for the Soviets
- Similarly to the Vietnam War, the invasion of Afghanistan was expensive, not widely supported, and led to huge losses in Soviet morale
- Comparatively, the US just had to spend a little money to arm the mujahideen
- The also banned grain exports to the USSR, boycotted the Moscow Olympics, and increased funding of the mujahideen
- The conflict quickly became almost unwinnable for the Soviets who did not expect such strong resistance
- Afghan troops were unmotivated compared to the mujahideen, and the Soviet troops had even less at stake in the conflict
- Armin, the prime minister of Afghanistan, took a Stalinist strongman approach to communist reform, ignoring local opposition and having opponents executed
- When he learned that the Afghan president was conspiring with the Soviets to remove him, he had the president executed
- Upon learning this, he began to look to the West for support
- This led to the decision to send in a Soviet invasion to remove Armin
- At one point the US was actually supplying the mujahideen with arms from the Soviet army who were becoming more and more corrupt as the situation worsened
- These mujahideen fighters eventually forced out the Soviets and took control of the country
- Target of a proxy war due to its proximity to ports and oil
- In addition, it shared a border with the Soviet Union
- Of course, the blowback was coming
- In 1980, Khomeini called for a political rebellion, urging all followers of Islam to reject the influence of both superpowers

**Soviet Union**

- At this time, the Soviet Union was continuing to present itself as being all powerful and superior to the West in every way
- In reality, the economy was being strained due to overspending on defense and Brezhnev was on his last legs, the Politburo was losing faith in his health
- Public discontent was steadily growing as citizens began to question the economic and political stability of the USSR

**America**

- In 1980, Ronald Reagan was elected basically on his promise of increased American action in the "Third World"
- Promised stronger foreign policy, aid to opponents of communism, and increased defense spending
- Called "The Teflon President" since although he was involved with so many scandals and dirty politics, he was still well liked by the American public
- Blamed the Soviet Union for the majority of world issues, using religious language and attacking them for being atheists
- Under his presidency, support for the mujahideen was vastly increased both publically and economically
- The war was fought with American money, but Afghan blood
- The conflict was "Vietnamized", meaning the conflict was fought by locals funded and trained by the superpowers
- American attitude towards the war was that it was a huge success, and never stopped to consider the consequences of funneling so many arms into Afghanistan
- Mujahideen fighters were even being trained on American soil, in Virginia
- Led to refugees leaving Afghanistan en masse
- Continued to provide more and more sophisticated weaponry, including Stinger missiles
- Mujahideen were specifically targeting Muslims to join due to religious motivations
